A 56-year-old male presented with the 1-year onset of progressive dysphagia and 8 months of dysphonia. Notably, his neurological examination was intact. The barium swallow showed a narrowed esophageal lumen at the C4–C5 level . X-ray and computed tomography studies revealed the classical “Ostrich beak” changes of the ossified ALL at the C4–C5 level; the disc spaces were preserved [ and ]. Utilizing an anterior cervical approach, the large beak-like osteophyte was removed, while preserving the anterior annulus; the extent and adequacy of resection was confirmed intraoperatively utilizing a lateral radiograph [ and ]. Postoperatively, he was discharged on anti- inflammatory medications for 10 days.
