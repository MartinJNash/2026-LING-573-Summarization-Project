A 27-year-old male presented with a high-intensity headache of 3 h duration. He exhibited no focal neurological deficit or any laboratory/coagulation abnormalities . The brain computed tomography (CT) scan documented a left frontoparietal lesion (e.g., 16 mm side), consistent with an acute epidural hematoma. However, the bone window CT showed no underlying fracture, and at surgery (e.g., a routine craniotomy), the lesion proved to be an acute subdural hematoma . The postoperative CT confirmed adequate removal of the clot . Subsequently, the patient’s additional studies including cerebral angiography, brain magnetic resonance (MR), and an additional full coagulation work-up all proved negative [-].
