A 17-year-old female patient presented with back pain and leg numbness for 4 mo.
The patient had an unremarkable history and complained of insidious onset of intermittent back pain followed by bilateral ascending paresthesia for 4 mo. Neurological examination revealed paresthesia below the T5 level, 5/5 muscle strength in all four limbs, and bilaterally normal reflexes. Spinal CT and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) revealed a collapsed T2 vertebra with an enhancing osteolytic mass that extended into the epidural and paraspinal region . Bone scan showed an active bone lesion at the upper thoracic spine .
Past illness history was not contributory.
Personal and family history was not contributory.
