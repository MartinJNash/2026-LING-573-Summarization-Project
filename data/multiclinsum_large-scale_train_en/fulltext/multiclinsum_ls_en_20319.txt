A 78-year-old male presented to the emergency department (ED) with hiccups, nausea, vomiting and generalized weakness.
In the preceding week, he had consumed 3 bottles of concentrated juice which were self-prepared from 1 kg of starfruits. Following ingestion of the third bottle of the fruit juice, he developed bouts of severe nausea and vomiting without abdominal pain or diarrhea.
He had a past medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus and hyperlipidemia.
No significant family history.
On arrival at the ED, his vital signs were stable (temperature was 36.8°C, pulse rate 60 bpm, respiratory rate 18 breaths/min, and blood pressure 161/78 mmHg) and there was no pitting edema. Examinations of his cardiovascular, respiratory, abdominal and neurological systems were normal.
