A 51-year-old female who was referred for evaluation of tooth 21 presented with a chief complaint of discomfort associated with an upper front tooth.

The patient has no relevant medical history.
Upon clinical assessment, tooth 21 responded negatively to both cold and electrical pulp tests. In addition, no mobility or deep probing were detected. However, the tooth was tender to percussion and bite upon testing. A periapical radiograph of the tooth in question is shown in Figure . Radiographic examination showed what appeared to be a large internal resorptive defect related to tooth 21. The endodontic diagnosis was necrotic pulp with symptomatic apical periodontitis. The patient wanted to try to save the tooth if possible. Planned treatment was nonsurgical endodontics and final restoration if the tooth was found to be restorable.
