A 50-year-old Caucasian, Greek woman presented with a pale brown nodule in her umbilicus. The nodule was asymptomatic, with no cyclical discomfort or variation in size. Her personal medical, surgical and gynecologic history was uneventful. An excision within healthy margins was performed under local anesthesia. A cystic formation measuring 2.7×1.7×1 cm was removed. There were no signs of malignancy and no evidence of endometrial component in her surrounding tissue. Pathologic examination showed a unilocular cyst with papillary projections into the lumen . The cyst was surrounded by edematous fibrous tissue. The lining consisted of epithelium cells, both cuboidal and ciliate . Immunohistochemical staining showed positivity for keratins AE1/AE3 . Finally, a histological examination posed the diagnosis of cutaneous endosalpingiosis.
