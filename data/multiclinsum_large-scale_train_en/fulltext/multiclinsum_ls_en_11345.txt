We present a 49-year-old man, with no comorbidities. The patient had a history of a road traffic accident 10 months ago when the patient had to undergo below-knee amputation at a peripheral center for distal one-third tibia fibula shaft fracture Gustilo Anderson Grade IIIc . At the same time, the patient also had a distal femur shaft fracture which was operated with retrograde femur nailing . The patient was mobilized with crutches. Seven months following the below-knee amputation, the patient had a history of fall at home. The patient presented to our hospital with pain in the right hip and restricted range of motion 3 months post-trauma. A standard anteroposterior radiograph of pelvis with both hips showed right NOF fracture and non-union of the right distal femur shaft fracture with a femoral nail in situ . Pre-operative planning was done to avoid intraoperative or post-operative complications. Distal femur nail was removed and compression plating through lateral approach with allogenic bone grafting and uncemented right THR surgery done through Moore approach as the definitive procedure for the patient.
