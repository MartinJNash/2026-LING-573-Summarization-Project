A 33-year-old woman with no history of the previous disease was referred to a general surgery department with abdominal pain and an abdominal mass. She has been suffering from abdominal pain for about 4 months and has been feeling an abdominal mass since a month ago. During the physical examination, a mass was felt along the umbilicus and the mid-clavicular line on the right side of the abdomen,
which was slightly tender. Routine lab tests and colonoscopy were normal. An abdominal CT scan showed an increased thickness in the terminal ileum while other abdominal organs reported normal. The patient was nominated for diagnostic laparoscopy. After general anesthesia, a 10 mm port was inserted through the umbilicus by the open manner. Next, the pneumoperitoneum was established, a 30-degree laparoscope was inserted into the abdomen, and the whole abdomen was explored. A solid mass with a size of about 10 × 5 cm was seen in a greater omentum . Two 5 mm ports were put in the subxiphoid area and the RLQ. There was no other pathology in abdominal exploration. Finally, the mass was completely resected from the omentum and removed from the umbilical incision with an endo bag . On post-operation day two, the patient was discharged home without any troubles. Histopathological examination of the mass showed a Hydatid disease.
