A 75-year-old man was brought to our hospital with a chief complaint of posterior neck pain during sleep. He had used a commercial massage tool before going to bed. While the ambulance was in transit, his lower extremities became immobilized. The patient's medical history showed cardiac bypass surgery and being prescribed aspirin. His vital signs on arrival were blood pressure 75/40 mmHg, pulse 47 b.p.m., regular respiration rate 13 breaths/min, blood oxygen saturation 80% without oxygenation, and body temperature 35.3°C. The patient was alert and conscious. On physical examination, muscle strength in both the upper and lower extremities had decreased to a score of 0 on the manual muscle test (MMT), and sensory loss below the papillae was observed. The muscle strength according to American Spinal Injury Association (ASIA) were as follows (right/left): elbow flexors 0/0, wrist extensors 0/0, elbow extensors 0/0, finger flexors 0/0, finger abductors 0/0, hip flexors 0/0, knee extensors 0/0, ankle dorsiflexors 0/0, long toe extensors 0/0, and ankle plantar flexors 0/0. He experienced bladder bowel disturbance and loss of deep tendon reflex. Two hours after the onset of symptoms, all muscle strength returned to MMT5 and sensation recovered spontaneously, that is, his ASIA impairment scale improved from A to E. Magnetic resonance imaging carried out on day 2 suggested a spinal cord epidural hematoma at the C2/C3 to C3/C4 level . On day 6, the patient was discharged with no symptom recurrence.
