A previously healthy 29-year-old female was transferred to the emergency department of the Second Affiliated Hospital of Zhejiang University School of Medicine because of left hemothorax with hemodynamic instability.
She had suffered from increased neck pain with shortness of breath and severe vomiting without hematemesis after eating fish two days before. The examination in a local hospital indicated a massive left pleural effusion on chest radiograph; however, no fishbone was found under laryngoscopy. Due to her rapidly decreasing blood pressure even after massive liquid infusion, the patient was quickly transferred to our hospital for further treatment.
The patient had a free previous medical history.
At the time of admission, the patient was in a light coma. Vital signs showed a heart rate of 129 beats/min and blood pressure of 69/35 mmHg.
Blood tests revealed that her hemoglobin was 46 g/L.
The enhanced chest computed tomography (CT) showed a pseudoaneurysm in the initial segment of the left vertebral artery and massive effusion in the mediastinum and the left thoracic cavity .
