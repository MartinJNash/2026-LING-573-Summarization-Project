A 68-year-old male presented with 4/5 right iliopsoas and quadricep weakness accompanied by hypoesthesia of both anterolateral thighs and bladder dysfunction. The lumbar magnetic resonance imaging revealed an extradural compressive enhancing mass at the L2–3 level and invasions of the bilateral L2/3 and left L3/4 facets . The lumbar computed tomography showed L3 dorsal osteolytic changes involving the left pedicle with L2–L3 subluxation . The neoplastic score for spinal instability was 16 , whereas the revised Tokuhashi score was 9 points .
An L2–5 posterior decompression was performed; the pathology was diagnostic for spinal metastasis from renal cell cancer. This decompression was followed by a posterolateral thoraco–lumbo–pelvic fusion. Pedicle screws were placed bilaterally at T10–L2 and L5–S1; bilateral transverse process hooks at T9 accompanied these. In addition, S2AISs were placed bilaterally, and a third rod was placed parallel to the center of the left and right rods to avoid rod breakage.
The patient’s postoperative recovery was uneventful. He received local radiotherapy at a total dose of 30 Gy for residual tumors. Although asymptomatic, radiographic assessment five months postoperatively revealed dislodgement of the bilateral set screws of the S2AIS . A second surgery was performed to replace the bilateral set screws; this construct was reinforced using transverse connectors between the S1 and S2. However, one year later, the right set screw dislodged . A third surgery confirmed that the right set screw had slipped out and the left set screw had loosened; therefore, the right S2AIS was replaced, and additional bilateral iliac screws were placed once the transverse connector had been removed . No further fixation failures occurred during the patient’s remaining life span of an additional year.
