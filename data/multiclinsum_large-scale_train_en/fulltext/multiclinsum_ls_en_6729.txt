A 67-year-old male patient presented to our outpatient clinic with 1 year of urine hesitancy, urgency, and frequency. Past medical history was remarkable for hypertension treated with valsartan. Physical examination showed no significant findings. His blood pressure was 140/60 mmHg, pulse 76/min, and SpO2 91%. Laboratory findings are shown in Table . Urinalysis showed 10–12 white blood cells/HPF, 2–4 red blood cells/HPF. Abdominal and pelvis ultrasound revealed normal findings with no hydronephrosis except for prostate enlargement 75 g. During the past year, he has taken double doses of tamsulosin. Prostate-specific antigen was 1.1. According to patient preference, we decided to perform monopolar TURP. After 3 days, under spinal anastasia, we performed the surgery. During the operation, we used 5000–6000 ml of distilled water (DW) for irrigation. We took 57 min to finish the procedure. We did not have any problems. After 24 hours of surgery, our patient developed oliguria. Vital signs and consciousness were all normal. Laboratories showed an elevation of creatinine (2.9 mg/dl) and blood urea nitrogen (BUN) (35 mg/dl) with normal electrolytes. On the third day of surgery, the creatinine was raised to 5.9 mg/dl and BUN to 79 mg/dl. As a result, the patient underwent hemodialysis on the fourth day of surgery. After 48 hours of hemodialysis, there was an increase in urine output and kidney function tests. Creatinine decreased to 1.9 mg/dl and BUN decreased to 39 mg/dl. Stabilization was maintained on the sixth day of hemodialysis. We discharged our patient after 10 days of surgery in a good state. Follow-up for 4 weeks revealed no complaints about normal renal function.
