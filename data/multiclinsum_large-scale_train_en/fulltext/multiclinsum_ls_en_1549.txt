A 46-year-old woman was referred to our hospital for surgical treatment with a cardiac tumour detected by echocardiography during a health check-up without symptoms. There were no systemic abnormalities, including the skin and endocrine glands. She had no other significant medical history, comorbidities, or family history. Thoracic echocardiography indicated the presence of a large hypoechoic mass attached to the atrial septum, occupying both the left and right atria. The mass did not cause any obvious obstruction of the atrioventricular valves. The masses in the left and right atria were measured 99 × 42 and 52 × 31 mm, respectively . Although the echocardiogram showed a bilateral atrial mass, it was noted that the left atrial mass pedicle was located near the fossa ovale, whereas the right atrial mass pedicle was not found . Contrast-enhanced echocardiography revealed slight enhancement in the mass. Without contraindications, surgery was scheduled. During the operation, a jelly-like mass protruding to the right ventricle was found in the right atrium. Additionally, a PFO measuring about 20 mm in diameter was detected. The mass originated from the left atrium and protruded into the right atrium through the foramen ovale . The pedicle of the mass was located anterior and inferior to the foramen ovale, measuring about 10 mm in diameter. The mass was completely excised, and the ASD was repaired. Histopathology confirmed the diagnosis of cardiac myxoma . Postoperation echocardiography showed mild left atrial enlargement . Upon discharge, the patient was prescribed hydrochlorothiazide tablets and spironolactone tablets. At the 3-month follow-up, no evident cardiac abnormalities were observed. Considering the possibility of recurrence, a follow-up echocardiography will be conducted after surgery.
