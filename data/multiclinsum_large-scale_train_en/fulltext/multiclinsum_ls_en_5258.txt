A 22 year old lady slipped from stairs and landed on the greater trochanter of her right side. On presentation to the emergency department, she had pain over her right hip and was unable to stand or walk. Her vitals were stable. Movements of her right hip were painless. Tenderness could be elicited on bi trochanteric compression. There was no distal neurovascular deficit. Radiograph was ordered. On the right side, an atypical fracture pattern could be seen on plain radiograph . CT scan showed an undisplaced transverse isolated quadrilateral plate fracture of acetabulum on axial and coronal sections . There was no involvement of the acetabular walls or columns. The weight bearing portion of the acetabulum, the acetabular roof was intact . We decided to treat the quadrilateral plate fracture conservatively, with non-weight bearing for 6 weeks followed by progressing weight bearing as tolerated.
