The patient, a 33-year-old woman, had epigastric distension for half a year.
Examinations were performed in a local hospital. Gastroscopy revealed erythema exudative gastritis, and computed tomography (CT) scan showed round soft tissue nodules, about 5.45 cm in diameter, in the hepatic-gastric space . The patient was referred to our hospital for an enhanced CT examination. The CT values for the plain scan, arterial phase and venous phase were 42.9 HU, 109.6 HU and 82.4 HU, respectively . The disease was suspected to be CD according to enhanced CT scan findings. Endoscopic ultrasound (EUS) was performed on the patient using a linear array EUS EG530UT with the host Fujin Su-8000. The EUS findings demonstrated low echoes in the hepatic-gastric space, and scattered star-like hyperechoes were observed inside. The color Doppler indicated that there was no blood flow signal. In order to clarify the nature of the lesion, EUS guided fine needle aspiration (EUS-FNA), (puncture needle COOK-ECHO 19G) was used .
