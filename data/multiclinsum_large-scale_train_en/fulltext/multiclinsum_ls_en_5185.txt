A 51-year-old woman with no past medical history presented with pruritic lesions involving the flexural areas. Informed consent was obtained from the patient. She reported that her father and her daughter had similar skin lesions on skin folds. Her three other children were healthy. Cutaneous examination revealed erythematous plaques, with macerated and eroded surface, partially covered by small blisters in axillary and inguinal areas, with parallel rhagades on the groins and the internal aspects of the thighs . Palms, soles, oral and genital mucosa, and nails were normal.
