A 44-year-old woman was admitted to our hospital for dyspnea with chest pain in April 2018.
The patient had no history of present illness.
The patient had no history of past illness.
The patient had no personal and family history.
No obvious abnormalities were found on physical examination.
Laboratory tests showed elevated levels of several serum tumor markers (CA19-9, 483.98 U/mL; CA125, 111.44 IU/mL; CA242, 138.50 IU/mL; cytokeratin-19 fragment, 5.34 ng/mL; and carcinoembryonic antigen, 20.07 ng/mL). Liver and kidney function tests were normal. The pericardial effusion was bloody, and tumor cells were detected. Mediastinal mass biopsy showed pathological adenocarcinoma infiltration . Immunohistochemical staining showed that the tumor cells were positive for CK20 , CDX2 , villin, and EGFR; partially positive for CA15-3; and negative for lung cancer markers, including CK7, TTF-1, and Napsin A. The Ki-67 index was 70%. Moreover, wild types of KRAS, NRAS, and BRAF were detected. The pathological results suggested intestinal metastatic adenocarcinoma. However, no other primary tumor was found on systemic examination.
Whole-body positron emission computed tomography (CT) showed an anterior calcified mediastinal mass measuring approximately 43 mm × 38 mm with an increased edge radioactivity uptake [maximum standardized uptake value (SUV) of 6.4; CT value of 41.8 HU]; increased pericardial radioactive uptake and a fluid density shadow; and increased sternal spot-like radioactivity uptake (maximum SUV value of 3.4).
