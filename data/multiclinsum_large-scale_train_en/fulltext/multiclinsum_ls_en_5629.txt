A 12-year-old boy presented at our outpatient clinic with the inability to urinate.
The patient experienced difficulty urinating for approximately three weeks before the outpatient visit. He could not begin urinating properly when he felt the urge to urinate and could not completely empty his bladder. He frequently woke up in the night to urinate. He could not recollect any characteristics of his urine stream or any aggravating or relieving factors influencing his symptoms.
The patient reported suffering a straddle injury after falling from a trampoline three months before his presentation. He presented to the emergency room (ER) with blood in the urethra meatus and the inability to urinate. Retrograde urethrography (RGU) performed in the ER confirmed partial rupture of the bulbous urethra, which was treated via the insertion of a 12F foley catheter .
The patient reported no relevant family history.
The boy appeared well oriented and cooperative, and his vital signs were stable.
At the outpatient visit, the PVR urine volume was 644 mL.
RGU performed during the outpatient visit confirmed severe stricture of the bulbous urethra.
