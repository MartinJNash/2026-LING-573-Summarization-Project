A three-year old female patient with the pre-diagnosis of neurosensory hearing loss in the left ear admitted to our hospital for CT scan of the temporal bone. Cochlea, vestibule and semi-circular canals of the inner ear structures in the left ear were not monitored, while the internal auditory canal was monitored markedly narrowed compared to the right ear on the multi-detector thin-section CT . The seventh cranial (facial) nerve was observed to have an abnormal course. The seventh and eighth nerve complexes in the left ear were found to be hypoplastic and the 8th cranial nerve could not be monitored on the temporal MRI scan . A hypoplasia was found on the petrous bone. The Welcher basal angle was measured as 152 degrees and found to have increased in line with the platybasia. We considered Michel deformity radiologically due to the defined findings. The patient whom was not implemented a cochlear implant was given recommendations.
