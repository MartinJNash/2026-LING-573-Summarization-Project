A 21-year-old male consulted for pain and swelling over left ankle over 5 months with difficulty in walking. A swelling on the external side of the left ankle was noticed upon clinical examination, with no signs of inflammation and infection. Articular motions were free, with the dorsiflexion angle measured at 10° and the plantar flexion angle measured at 25°. Radiographic examination showed a 5 cm lytic lesion on the lateral malleolus . Anteroposterior and lateral radiographs showed single epiphyseal expansile lesion extending into metaphysis with soap bubble appearance. The cortex of the fibula had thinned; however, the lesion was clearly defined with no periosteal reaction. MRI of left ankle region shows well defined T1 Hypointense and T2 Hyperintense/STIR lesion in the lateral malleolus. Computerized tomography scan revealed cortical break medially. No other significant findings over general and systemic examination. The histologic analysis confirmed the diagnosis of giant cell tumor.
