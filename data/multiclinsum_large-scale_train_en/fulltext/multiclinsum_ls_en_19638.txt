A 36-year-old white woman presented to our institution with recurrent attacks of headache. She reported initial visual impairment in her left eye that was gradually worsening. A neurological examination showed left amaurosis, confirmed by a visual field test, associated to a severe optic atrophy at fundus oculi. No other deficits were disclosed and all routine laboratory findings were normal.
A magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) study of her brain revealed a lesion of her left optic nerve (22×13 mm) with extension to the optic chiasma and a suspect aneurysmatic dilatation (6×5 mm) of the left carotid bifurcation . A computed tomography (CT) scan of her brain was performed with angiography and a three-dimensional reconstruction confirmed an aneurysmatic dilatation of the left carotid bifurcation, and it revealed a second aneurysm on the left of her middle cerebral artery and a third one on her right anterior cerebral artery in the A2 tract .
A left pterional approach was used to remove the tumor and clip the three aneurysmatic dilatations in a single stage. The tumor was removed by cutting proximally and distally the optic nerve that could not be dissected from the lesion. Histopathological examination confirmed the diagnosis of astrocytoma grade I.
Her postoperative course was satisfactory without complications , except for the left amaurosis which was present preoperatively.
