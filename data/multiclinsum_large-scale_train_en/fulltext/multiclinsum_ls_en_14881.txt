A 57 -years-old lady sprained her left ankle resulting in lateral ankle pain and swelling. There was local tenderness at the posterolatera ankle. Radiograph showed displaced split fracture of the latera malleolus . Computed tomogram confirmed cortical rim avulsion fracture and the diagnosis of Eckert and Davis grade 3 superior peroneal retinaculum injury . Endoscopic superior peroneal retinaculum reconstruction was performed . The operated ankle was protected in a short leg cast and non-weight bearing for 4 weeks. Clinically, the peroneal tendons were located in the retromalleolar groove and no subluxation of the tendons was detected. Radiographs taken 2 months after the operation showed radiolucency at the lateral malleolus and the fracture still not healed . However, the fracture healed up and the radiolucent lesion disappeared 7 months after the operation . However, the patient had posterolateral ankle pain and swelling after walking. The tenderness was located deeper than the peroneal tendons. Magnetic resonance imaging showed that the suture anchors were protruded into the ankle joint . Removal of the anchors through posterior ankle endoscopy was performed . Her symptoms subsided after the operation.
