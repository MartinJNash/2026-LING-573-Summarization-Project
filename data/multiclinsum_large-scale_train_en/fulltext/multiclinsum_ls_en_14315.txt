A 64-year-old female patient was admitted for atrial fibrillation ablation . She had a history of mechanical mitral and aortic valve implantation, mild dilatation of left atrium (40 mm), normal systolic function of the left ventricle, and a recently deteriorated functional capacity attributed to persistent atrial fibrillation. Medical treatment before admission was including acenocoumarol, metoprolol (with adequate rate control), olmesartan, and amlodipine (with normal blood pressure on admission). Physical examination did not reveal signs of congestive heart failure. The patient underwent catheter ablation with the FARAPULSE PFA System (Boston Scientific, Marlborough, MA) using the 31 mm pentaspline PFA catheter (Farawave). At the end of the uneventful pulmonary vein isolation (PVI), an atrial tachycardia occurred that was entrained from the CTI. After administration of 2 mg of intravenous nitroglycerine, a single application (with a peak voltage of 2.0 kV), in flower configuration, was delivered at the lateral annular portion of the CTI .
The patient acutely presented right bundle branch block (RBBB) and left posterior fascicular block (LPFB) without flutter termination . The QRS complex in RBBB was particularly wide and a mild HV interval prolongation of 68 ms was revealed . Cardioversion restored sinus rhythm uncovering an extremely prolonged PR interval of 344 ms in addition to RBBB (176 ms) and LPFB . The patient remained under continuous electrocardiographic monitoring without any antiarrhythmic medication. Within 12 h, RBBB and LPFB resolved spontaneously and PR interval decreased to 220 ms , as in previous ECG recordings. The following day, the patient was discharged in sinus rhythm. Fifteen days later, she was free of arrhythmias receiving sotalol 40 mg twice a day and the repeated ECG was identical to that of the day following the ablation procedure.
