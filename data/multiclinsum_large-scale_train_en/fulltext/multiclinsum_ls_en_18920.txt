A 60-year-old female presented with complaints of a slowly growing mass on her left upper lip for 8 years.
The mass was soybean-sized and did not cause any specific discomfort. One month earlier, this patient considered the mass might be continuously growing and decided to remove it. She went to another hospital and was diagnosed with pigmented nevus. The mass was removed without any extended resection and the wound was directly closed. The patient recovered well and removed the sutures on time with no infection or other complications occurring. Postoperative pathological and immunohistochemical diagnosis indicated skin MAC with positive lateral and basal margins.
The patient had a history of surgery for papillary thyroid carcinoma and denied any history of postoperative radiotherapy. No other positive history of past illness was offered.
This patient declared no smoking or alcohol history and any other noteworthy family medical history.
Physical examination on admission showed a light red scar on the left upper lip with a length of approximately 3 cm .
