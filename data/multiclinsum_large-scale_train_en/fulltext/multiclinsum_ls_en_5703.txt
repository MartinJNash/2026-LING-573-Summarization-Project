A 2 days old female newborn was referred to the neonatal department of Tabriz Children’s Hospital affiliated to the University of Medical Sciences of Tabriz, Iran on the 3rd of December 2011, immediately after birth with respiratory distress due to bilateral coanal atresia and nasal hypoplasia with very small nostrils . Her respiratory distress improved with oral airway. Her gestational age was 38 weeks. Birth weight was 3000 g and birth length was 45 cm. She was the first child of unrelated parents. The mother and father were 21and 26 years old, respectively. In the physical exam we noticed that the feeding tube sized with trans-nasal dilation catheters couldn’t pass through the nostrils .
The small feeding tube failed to pass through the posterior choans. CT scan showed normal brain and bilateral choanal atresia. Pyriform apertures were normal in size. Nasal cavities except for anterior nares were normal. There wasn’t any evidence of mid-nasal stenosis . Patient’s evaluation for other nomalies (Cardiac, Kidney, brain, skeletal and hearing) didn’t show any abnormalities.
Because of severe anterior nasal stenosis it was impossible to open the atretic plate transnasally without traumatizing the anterior nostrils. So we decided to operate the coanal atresia via the sub-labial method using a trans-nasal dilation catheter and anterior nostrils were then repaired with z plasty . Following this, beveled edge number 3.5 endotracheal tubes were introduced bilaterally and fixed with sutures sublabially . Post operatively parenteral antibiotics were administered and salin irrigation and suctioning of the tubes were taught to parents. The patient was discharged 3 days post operatively, keeping stents for 4 weeks. Postoperatively her breathing problem was resolved.
