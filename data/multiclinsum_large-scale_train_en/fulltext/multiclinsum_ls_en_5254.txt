An otherwise healthy 37-year-old male presented with a complaint of left knee pain. Several months prior to presentation, the patient began noticing painless fullness in his knee after playing soccer. One month prior he developed anterior knee pain after a six kilometer run. Inspection showed obvious suprapatellar fullness on his left side . Palpation revealed additional fullness in the patient’s left posterolateral knee. Active knee flexion was limited to 90° with normal range of extension. Radiographs and MRI were obtained . The patient underwent an arthroscopic surgical resection .
The surgical specimen measured 2.8 × 1.7 × 0.2 cm, macroscopically; the tissue was soft, yellow, smooth, and fatty. Microscopic examination revealed a villous architecture with a synovial cell lining ( and ). Some areas of synovial hyperplasia were present. Villous infiltrate consisted of mature adipose tissue with mild lymphoplasmacytic inflammatory infiltrate, all benign in appearance. Occasional hemosiderin-laden macrophages consistent with hemarthrosis were present.
