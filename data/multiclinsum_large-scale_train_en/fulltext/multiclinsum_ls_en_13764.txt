A 52-year-old Thai female without preexisting shoulder pathology or symptoms presented with shoulder pain for 3 days. She had no underlying physical or psychosocial diseases or history of normal drug use or autoimmune or allergic conditions. Two weeks prior to her presentation, she had received a mRNA COVID-19 vaccination. She positioned her arm in combined internal rotation with 60° of arm abduction during vaccine administration. Her physical examination showed pain in all directions of shoulder motion, with tenderness at the bicipital groove and over the deltoid area. The rotator cuff power test of the infraspinatus tendon showed pain during an action. She was diagnosed as a SIRVA and treated with oral naproxen (500 mg/day) for 1 week, but her clinical symptoms did not improve. At her follow-up visit, an MRI showed infraspinatus tendinosis with a low-grade (almost 50%) , bursal-surface tear at the footprint of the superior fiber with associated overlying subacromial–subdeltoid bursitis . The orthopedist (C.C.) advised her to have both intraarticular and subacromial corticosteroid injections (triamcinolone acetate (40 mg/ml) 1 ml+1% lidocaine with adrenaline 9 ml). After the injections, her clinical symptoms gradually improved over the following week.
