A 22-year-old woman, complaining of right chest pain that had persisted for about 3 mo, was referred from the thoracic surgery clinic to the inpatient department.
A 22-year-old female patient complained of right chest pain, which began after a fall injury (3 mo before admission). Chest radiography after the trauma showed no remarkable abnormality . Despite appropriate analgesia, no sufficient pain relief had been achieved.
The patient had no significant prior medical history.
The patient had no relevant personal or family history.
A palpable mass was detected over the inframammary line. The mass was 5 cm in size, hard in consistency, tender, and adherent to profound planes (unmovable).
Findings from laboratory examinations were unremarkable.
Chest radiography revealed an opacity in the right lower chest wall, with bony destruction . Computed tomography (CT) indicated a 5-cm tumor in the right anterior chest wall, with destruction of the right 6th rib . Whole-body bone scan revealed increased uptake in the 5th-7th ribs .
