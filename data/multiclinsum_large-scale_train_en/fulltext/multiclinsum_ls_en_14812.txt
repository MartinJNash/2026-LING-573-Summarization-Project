A 43-year-old man was presented to our hospital with left flank pain for 5 days. His serum creatinine level had increased from to 0.94 to 1.45 mg/dL obtained 15 months ago. He was suspected of having obstructive uropathy, and retrograde pyelography indicated that a left UPJO existed and that the site of obstruction might be intrarenal . The patient subsequently underwent insertion of a double-J ureteral stent, which resulted in recovery from uropathy. Contrast-enhanced CT showed dissolution of the left hydronephrosis and large renal cyst(s) directly adjacent to the left lower renal calyx . Six weeks after removal of the double-J stent, he underwent surgery for the left UPJO.
