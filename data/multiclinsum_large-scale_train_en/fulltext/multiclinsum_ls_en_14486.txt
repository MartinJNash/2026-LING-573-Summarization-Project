This 31-year-old woman presented with a painful scalp lesion on the left mastoid, 2 cm in diameter. She had no specific medical history, and her general condition was good. She experienced severe headache of abrupt-onset 1 month ago, and noticed a newly developed scalp tenderness on the left mastoid region. Findings from physical examination were normal except for a tender scalp lesion on the left temporal region. She was neurologically intact. Routine laboratory data, including serum calcium, phosphorus, and alkaline phosphatase, were within the normal limits. Brain computed tomography scans showed approximately 2.5 cm sized, lobulated, osteolytic lesion over the right temporal (mastoid) bone .
During the operation, an reddish lesion was attached to the bone, and had completely eroded the outer and inner tables of skull. At the margins of the lesion, the skull was thickened and gritty in an area of 1 to 1.5 mm around the lesion . En-bloc resection of the bony lesion including surrounding healthy bone enough to clear resection margin was performed. The cranial defect did not repair. After the operation, no neurological deficit was found, and a good cosmetic result was achieved.
Histopathological examination confirmed an osteoid osteoma . She was regularly followed for 10 months without any signs of regrowth.
