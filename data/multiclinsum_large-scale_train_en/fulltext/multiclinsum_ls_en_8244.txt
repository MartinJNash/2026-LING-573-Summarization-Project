A 75-year-old male presented with severe paraparesis after a fall. He exhibited bilateral lower extremity weakness (strength score of 2/5) with diffuse hyperreflexia, bilateral Babinski signs, and a questionable T12 sensory level.
The T2-weighted axial MR images showed an isointense left-sided large anterolateral thoracic disc herniation at the T10-T11 level with superior migration resulting in an image equivalent to a “complete block” herniation . The adjacent vertebral endplates were edematous due to the antecedent trauma. Further, there was gas in the T10/ T11 disc space [-]. The CT scan confirmed these traumatic findings [ and ].
The surgery included; a thoracic interlaminar laminectomy, removal of one large and several smaller sequestrated disc fragments (i.e., removed through a transfacet corridor), and placement of an interbody banana-shaped Peek cage (i.e., bilaterally at the T10 and T11 level) with T10 and T11 bilateral pedicle screws [ and ].
Six weeks postoperatively, could the patient could barely ambulate with a walker. However, the thoracic plain radiographs showed that the fusion construct was intact . We await further neurological follow-up .
