The patient is a 38-year-old, morbidly obese female with hypertension, DMII, and a surgical history of Laparoscopic Gastric Plication (LGP) and Gastric Banding. At the time of evaluation, LGP history was unknown and not reported by the patient. She only recalled having had banding four years prior to presentation, and was unable to provide or obtain any documentation from the event. Her reported starting weight was 116 kg (BMI = 44 kg/m2), lowest post banding weight was 101 kg (BMI = 35 kg/m2), which was seen one year after the index procedure. Following her previous surgery, she did not visit a dietician, psychologist, or support group. The patient weighed 114 kg (BMI = 43 kg/m2) when she was evaluated for the first time in our clinic. She underwent the standard pre-operative evaluation required for gastric surgery at our center. This included a nutritional and diet evaluation, psychiatric evaluation, and analysis of family support for continued weight lost practice post procedure. Chest x-ray confirmed proper placement of the band. Conversion from Gastric Banding to a LSG or Roux-en-Y gastric bypass was recommended due to failure of prior bariatric surgical modalities, and the patient elected for LSG.
