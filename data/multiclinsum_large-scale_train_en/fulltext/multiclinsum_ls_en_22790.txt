A 7-month-old Chinese girl presented to the hospital with nystagmus for 2 months. The patient was born at 37 weeks’ gestation and weighed 3,400 g. Her growth and development were normal, except that she was unable to sit. Her family history was negative for this condition, and her parents were not known to be consanguineous. An examination of the anterior segment of both her eyes revealed no abnormalities. Color fundus photography (RetCam III, Natus Medical, USA) showed a cherry-red spot with a ring of whitish infiltrate surrounding both macula . FFA showed normal retinal circulation and vessels . OCT (OPMI LUMERA and RESCAN 700, Carl Zeiss Meditec, AG, Germany) revealed a thickening and increased reflectivity of the inner retinal layers with a shadowing effect on the outer structures. The marked thickening and hyperreflective part was within a 1–2 papillary diameter (PD) area around the fovea. It was difficult to distinguish the inner retinal structure . The head MRI of the girl’s head revealed no visible anomalies.
A novel homozygous pathogenic variant in GM2A was detected in the whole-exome genome sequencing of the patient. The sequencing results showed that there was a homozygous deletion (Chr5: 150639196-150639548) of exon 2 in the GM2A gene, which revealed GM2 activator protein deficiency. The parents of the patient had heterozygous mutations in the same genetic locus .
Finally, the patient was diagnosed with AB variant GM2 gangliosidosis. One and a half months later, more abnormal findings were reported and these were confined to the nervous system. The girl lost the ability to roll over and hold her bottle. She developed hyperacusis, a heightened sensitivity to sound, and her natural smile lessened. However, there was no effective treatment for this disease.
