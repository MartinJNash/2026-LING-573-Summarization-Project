A 72-year-old man who underwent radical resection of rectal cancer one year ago presented with rectal stenosis for the past 6 months.
The patient underwent radical resection of rectal cancer and ileostomy one year ago for rectal cancer. A colonoscopy performed six months ago revealed a narrow rectal anastomosis, which did not meet the indications for ileostomy reduction surgery. After endoscopic balloon dilation was performed three times, colonoscopy showed no improvement in the rectal stenosis . The patient was recommended by the endoscopist to visit the Magnetic Surgery Clinic of the First Affiliated Hospital of Xi’an Jiaotong University for further treatment.
The past medical history was unremarkable.
There was no family history of this condition.
The patient’s vital signs were stable, and auscultation of the heart and lungs was normal. The abdomen was flat and soft with no tenderness. There was no mobile dullness and bowel sounds were normal. Ileostomy was visible in the lower right quadrant of the abdomen, and the intestinal mucosa of ileostomy was normal.
There were no significant abnormalities in the patient’s hematological examination.
A 14Fr nasogastric tube was inserted through the anus and 80 mL iodohexol was injected. Rectal stenosis was observed by colonography .
