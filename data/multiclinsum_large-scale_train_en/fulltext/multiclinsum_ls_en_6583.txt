A 27-year-old male was presented with 1 year of neck pain, dysesthesias in both hands, and difficulty walking. His neurological examination only showed hyperreflexia with Babinski signs.
The cervical lateral radiograph showed a 2-mm anterolisthesis at C6–C7, with bilateral defects at the pedicle-laminar junction of C6 vertebra. Furthermore, the spinous process of the C6 vertebra was abnormally long and narrow [ and ].
The axial computerized tomography (CT) showed a linear defect at the C6 pedicle-laminar junction bilaterally with sclerosis, Grade I spondylolisthesis, and an abnormally long C6 spinal process [ and ]. Other CT findings included intrusion of spinolaminar junction into the spinal canal with enlargement of C5–C6 neural foramina [ and ]. The MRI confirmed spinal cord compression at the C6 level attributed to a T1 isointense dorsolateral fibrous mass [ and ]. The T2 sagittal images also showed an increased central C6 level cord signal [ and ].
The patient first underwent a posterior decompression (C6 posterior arch) with C5–C7 pedicle screw fusion . This was followed by a C6–C7 anterior cervical discectomy/fusion (ACDF) using an appropriate size PEEK cage filled with demineralized allograft chips . Postoperatively, the patient gradually improved within 4 months and continued to do well 2 years later .
