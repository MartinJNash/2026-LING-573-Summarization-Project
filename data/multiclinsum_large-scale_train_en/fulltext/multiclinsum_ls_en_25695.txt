A 17-year-old Asian man presented with painless bilateral reduction in his vision. Vision loss had started two months previously in the right eye and two days previously in the left eye. The patient had no history of systemic disease or trauma. His best corrected visual acuities (BCVA) were 20/500 in the right eye and 20/1000 in the left eye. Refractions were -6.5 -1.5 × 180° in both eyes. Axial lengths were 29.35 mm in the right eye and 28.37 mm in the left eye. Fundus examination showed subretinal hemorrhages in the maculae of both eyes . Fluorescein angiography (FA) revealed subretinal hemorrhages in both eyes accompanied by focal leakage at the level of the retinal pigment epithelium (RPE), which was suggestive of CNV in the right eye . Optical coherence tomography (OCT) revealed breaks in Bruch's membrane with subretinal elevation in both eyes .
The patient was diagnosed with CNV in both eyes, and he underwent bilateral intravitreal injections of ranibizumab (Lucentis®, Genetech, San Francisco, CA, USA) five times over six months. The subretinal hemorrhages decreased bilaterally, and visual acuities improved . After subretinal hemorrhages had subsided, lacquer cracks were clearly seen in both eyes.
Six months after initial presentation, the patient complained of a sudden, painful visual decrease in his right eye. On examination, his BCVA in the right eye was finger count. Slit-lamp biomicroscopy revealed corneal steepening in the inferior paracentral area and stromal edema in his right eye with rupture of Bowman's layer, signs characteristic of advanced keratoconus and acute hydrops . Corneal topography of his left eye showed irregular astigmatism and inferosuperior asymmetry, both of which were indicative of keratoconus .
