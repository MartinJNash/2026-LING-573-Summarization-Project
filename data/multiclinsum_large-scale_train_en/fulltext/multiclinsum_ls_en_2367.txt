A 49-year-old man with a history of hypertension was admitted to a hospital with a left back pain. When the patient underwent abdominal MRI, a mass was detected in the left adrenal gland; he was then referred to our hospital. Based on the CT and MRI findings, no findings were strongly suggestive of malignancy. The hormone levels, in contrast to the tumor diameter, indicated a diagnosis of a nonfunctional tumor. The major axis of the tumor increased from 64 to 72 mm during the 4-month period. Thus, total resection of the tumor, along with the left adrenal gland, was planned.
