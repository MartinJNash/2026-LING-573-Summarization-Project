A 54-year-old man was admitted to the hospital because of progressive hoarseness and dyspnea. He had no cough, sputum, or sore throat.
He had a history of hoarseness lasting 7 years, which persisted and gradually worsened. The patient had been stable until 10 d earlier, when hoarseness significantly worsened and dyspnea presented. The patient was treated with penicillin at the local hospital, which provided no relief, then he was referred to our hospital.
The patient had a free previous medical history.
The patient presented with moderate inspiratory dyspnea; however, vital signs were stable. There were no other abnormal findings.
The results of laboratory examinations were normal.
Laryngoscopy revealed a smooth laryngeal mass.
