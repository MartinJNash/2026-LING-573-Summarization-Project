The patient was a 39-year-old woman. She was diagnosed with right hydronephrosis with right back pain and deteriorated renal function, and she was referred to our department from Rheumatology. Her medical history included congenital left renal hypoplasia, Sjogren's syndrome, and hyper phospholipid antibody syndrome. Serum creatinine increased from 0.66 to 0.93 mg/dL. CT revealed partial right hydronephrosis, which was suspected to be an incomplete ureteral duplication . Since right back pain was severe and renal function was impaired, a double-J ureteral stent was immediately placed on lower pole before renogram. Retrograde pyeloureterography diagnosed incomplete ureteral duplication and UPJO of the lower pole in right kidney . After ureteral stenting, right back pain was relieved, and renal function improved promptly.
She then underwent laparoscopic right pyeloureterostomy. The adhesion around the right kidney and ureter was severe, and it was difficult to dissect around the ureter. After an intricate para-ureteral tissue dissection, the ureter corresponding to upper renal pole and dilated renal pelvis of the lower pole were exposed. A lateral incision was made approximately 1.5 cm in the ureter to the upper pole and dilated renal pelvis of the lower pole . Both ends were adapted with 3-0 absorbed thread to form a support thread, and knot stitches were similarly applied with 3-0 absorbed thread from the posterior wall. After the posterior wall was approximated, anterior wall was sutured with a 3-0 absorbent thread to complete anastomosis . The operation time was 5 h and 32 min, pneumoperitoneum time was 4 h and 57 min, and blood loss was 14 mL. She was discharged on the third postoperative day without any complications. The ureteral stent was removed 1 month after surgery. There was no occlusion pattern in the furosemide renogram , and hydronephrosis and renal dysfunction were improved .
