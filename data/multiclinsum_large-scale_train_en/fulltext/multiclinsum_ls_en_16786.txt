A 51-year-old hypertensive male presented to the cardiac critical care unit with a history of recurrent episodes of acute onset retrosternal chest pain at rest for the last 2 days. He was admitted in view of ongoing chest pain associated with diaphoresis. On admission, the patient was tachypneic with a respiratory rate of 22 breaths/min, the temperature of 37.1 °C, blood pressure was 140/96 mmHg, heart rate was 102 beats/min, oxygen saturation was 96%, and oxygen was 4 l/min. On auscultation, lungs were normal, and the cardiovascular system had tachycardia with no audible murmurs. Cardiac biomarkers revealed elevated troponin 1.1 ng/ml. ECG showed tachycardia and normal sinus rhythm with dynamic changes. Transthoracic echocardiography showed wall motion abnormalities in LAD and RCA territory with an ejection fraction of 44%. He was diagnosed as acute coronary syndrome, non-ST segment elevation myocardial Infarction (NSTEMI).
