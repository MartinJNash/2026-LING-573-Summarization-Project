A 60-year-old male presented with a 1-year history of a traumatic, increasing radicular pain and loss of pain and temperature in the upper extremities, accompanied by a severe and spastic tetraparesis. Neurological examination revealed mainly spastic tetraparesis and syringomyelic syndrome in the upper limbs. The cervical magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) on T2-weighted studies showed circumferential spondylotic (cervical spondylosis and osteophytes, and posteriorly by ligament flavum hypertrophy) compression at the C3/C4, C4/C5, and C5/C6 levels . MRI on T1-WI has also revealed a cervicothoracic syringomyelia extending from C1 to T3 .
Following a C2–C6 cervical laminectomy, the patient fully recovered. The MRI obtained six months postoperatively showed complete regression of the prior syrinx .
