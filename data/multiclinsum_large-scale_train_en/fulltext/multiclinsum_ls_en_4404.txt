A 25-year-old male presented to our outpatient department following an injury to the lid of his left eye (OS), caused by a sharp iron object, that occurred while working without taking safety measures .
On examination, he had an injury to the lower lid involving the lacrimal canal , a tear in the conjunctiva, and a ruptured inferior rectus muscle . He also had hypertropia of the OS and absent inferior movement in the affected eye.
The eyelid was repaired surgically in layers. The lacrimal canal was repaired using the Teflon sleeve of a 22G Intracath , which was left in place for 6 weeks. We located the proximal end of the canal and confirmed it by syringing. We found the proximal end of the inferior rectus muscle and sutured it to the distal end, achieving proper strength, as detected by orthophoria in the primary position and inferior movements at the end of the repair .
The patient complained of diplopia in the early postoperative period. This was evaluated with diplopia charts and the patient improved slowly, both subjectively and objectively. Patient presented to us again with complaint of diplopia after 3 years, on examination we detected right eye hypertropia , inferior rectus resection was done and became orthophoria.
The patient achieved orthophoria and full movement in inferior gaze .
