An eighteen year-old Asian female presented to the ophthalmology clinic of the McGill University Health Center with a three-month history of fluctuating swelling and ptosis of the left upper eyelid. Mild discomfort was felt whenever the swelling was more intense. A well-defined, soft lesion in the left upper eyelid could be palpated, just below the superior orbital rim, without associated inflammatory signs. No decrease in visual acuity or alterations of extraocular movements was found. Intraocular pressure was 17 mmHg OD and 20 mmHg OS. Computed tomography disclosed a distinct homogeneous lesion in the left superior orbit, molding to the globe and other orbital structures . There was no bone erosion. The findings favored the diagnosis of a lymphoid lesion and a transpalpebral biopsy was indicated and performed.
Histopathological evaluation revealed the presence of structures resembling lymphoid follicles surrounded by loose connective tissue . At higher magnification, those structures were composed of numerous blood vessels lined by plump endothelial cells with oval nuclei protruding into the lumen . Surrounding the vessels, there was a chronic inflammatory infiltrate composed of lymphocytes, plasma cells and a large proportion of eosinophils. Immunohistochemical studies, performed on paraffin-embedded tissue, showed the following: Factor VIII underscored the marked vascularity of the lesion , highlighting atypical vascular lining with "epithelioid" or "histiocytoid" cells . Whole body gallium scan failed to reveal lymph node involvement elsewhere. Blood counts and urinalysis were normal. Based on clinical and histopathological findings, the diagnosis of EH was made.
