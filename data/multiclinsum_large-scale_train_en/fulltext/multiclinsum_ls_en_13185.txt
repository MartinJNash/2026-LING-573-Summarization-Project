A 46-year-old man of Italian descent, with a known history of testicular seminoma that was treated surgically with chemotherapy and radiotherapy, presented to the clinic with a tan-colored, painless papular lesion measuring 4mm in diameter in his right upper lid that grew gradually over 10 months; it was clinically diagnosed as papilloma and an excisional biopsy was performed. He provided no family history of similar eyelid lesions. On histological examination, the lesion was a well-circumscribed and sharply demarcated epithelial tumor, characterized by keratin-filled cysts and superficial basaloid cells with a sheet-like spread in the upper dermis and broad attachments to the overlying epidermis. Aggregates of mature sebaceous cells forming clusters and lobules were arranged replacing basaloid cells in the lower part of the tumor. Furthermore, foci of ductal differentiation were seen, but no mitoses were observed. On immunohistochemical examination, DNA mismatch repair proteins MSH2 and MLH1 were positively expressed in the tumor cells . After a follow-up period of 5 months, no recurrence of the lesion has been documented.
