A 1.5-year-old male patients presented by his parents to the urologic consultation for checkup complaining from empty left scrotum.
The clinical examination showed normal general examination with empty left scrotum, examination of the left inguinal region showed no palpable testis in the inguinal region. In the right side, examination of the right scrotum showed a palpable testis in the right scrotum with palpable anther testis in the right inguinal region .
There was no bulging during crying and the past medical and surgical history was negative.
The patients was sent for ultrasound examination which showed the right testis in the right scrotum measuring 16 × 5 mm and other testis measuring 16 × 6 mm in the right inguinal region giving suggestion of both testes in the right side. .
Decision for exploration was done, and during operation two testes were found in the right inguinal region with two small indirect inguinal sacs. .
Herniotomy was for both sacs. The right testis was placed and fixed in the right hemi-scrotum by a slowly absorbable suture material and the left testis was placed in the left hemi-scrotum through trans-septal window and fixed with slowly absorbable suture material. , , .
The patients has uneventful postoperative recovery with no postoperative complications.
