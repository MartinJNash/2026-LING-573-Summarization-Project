A 37-year-old female presented to the emergency department (ED) with sudden onset severe headaches. Two years earlier, she had undergone surgery for resection of a PC. A year and a half later, the cyst recurred and was also associated with intermittent purulent discharge. The night before admission, she had watery discharge from the cyst and severe diffuse positional headaches associated with photophobia and neck pain. Differential diagnosis included migraine, meningitis, and subarachnoid hemorrhage. The head computed tomography (CT) obtained in ED showed pneumocephalus in the posterior fossa . A spine magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) revealed an ASM whose caudal pole was in contact with the recurrent PC . Close inspection of the PC showed clear liquid draining spontaneously, increasing with Valsalva maneuver . Sacral CT showed spina bifida and the agenesis of a portion of the anterior sacrum .
A final diagnosis was made of headaches due to IH. The leakage site was the rupture of the ASM in the recurrent PC. We decided to repair the ASM. The patient was positioned prone. A transverse incision exposed and enlarged the posterior sacral defect. Under operating microscope, we opened the dura in “U” fashion flap. There were no nerves entering the anterior meningocele. The dural flap was sutured in watertight fashion to the spinal canal dura . A second flap was harvested from the meningocele, based in the cranial aspect of the anterior sacral defect and sutured in a watertight fashion over the first flap . Finally, DuraSeal® (Integra Lifesciences Corporation, New Jersey, USA) was sprayed over. Endoscopically, we identified the leakage point, plugged the fistula with muscle, securing it with a suture and DuraSeal®. The postoperative course was uneventful. There was no more CSF leakage from the PC, the headaches disappeared. The follow-up spine MRI confirmed the total closure of the ASM .
