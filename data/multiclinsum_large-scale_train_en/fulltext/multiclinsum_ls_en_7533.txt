The patient was a 73-year-old woman who was referred to another institution due to bloody stool. On colonoscopy revealed a Type 2 mass at the ascending colon, 5 cm distal from the ileocecal valve, was seen. Biopsy suggested moderately differentiated adenocarcinoma. CT showed wall thickening at the ascending colon . There was no apparent lymph node metastasis or remote metastasis. Three-dimensional CT angiography (3D-CTA) confirmed that the MCA and ICA had a common trunk, the right hepatic artery originated from the superior mesenteric artery (SMA), and right colic artery (RCA) was absent . The Inferior mesenteric artery arose from the aorta as usual, and the accessory MCA is absent. The right colic vein (RCV), middle colic vein (MCV), and iliocolic vein (ICV) each arose from the superior mesenteric vein (SMV) respectively . Serum carcinoembryonic antigen and the carbohydrate antigen 19–9 serum levels were within the normal ranges. The preoperative diagnosis was cT3N0M0 (Japanese Classification Colorectal Carcinoma, 9th Edition) ascending colon cancer. The patient underwent laparoscopic ileocecal resection with D3 lymph node dissection for ascending colon cancer. The operation time was 316 min with minimal bleeding.
During the laparoscopy, a 1688 AIM 4K Camera® (Stryker [Kalamazoo, MI, USA]) was used. A total of 2 mL of the ICG dye was injected, and the camera mode was switched to the overlay function. Thus, the MCA was confirmed to originate from the common trunk with the ICA, as seen on the preoperative examination . The ICA was dissected at the distal end of the bifurcation of the MCA . Based on the resected specimens, the ascending colon cancer classification was pT3N0M0 Stage IIa (Japanese Classification Colorectal Carcinoma, 9th Edition). The patient continues to receive outpatient follow-up care, with no signs of recurrence as of 2 years postoperatively.
