A 50-year-old man with severe neck pain presented to the emergency department. He complained of a severe posterior neck pain and a headache in occipital area.
He fell from a height of 5 m while working at the field of construction work. After the accident, he complained of severe neck and occipital pain. He visited the emergency room of our hospital with Thomas-type cervical brace applied by an emergency patient transfer team.
He denied any medical history.
The patient showed an alert mental state and suffered from cervical immobility and instability. A neurological exam in the emergency room showed non-specific findings.
Laboratory testing in the emergency room showed non-specific findings.
Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) images of the brain showed mild brain concussion. However, MRI images of the cervical spine showed unstable cervical spine fractures with mild spinal cord contusion. The pre-operative abdominal X-ray was shown mild ileus.
