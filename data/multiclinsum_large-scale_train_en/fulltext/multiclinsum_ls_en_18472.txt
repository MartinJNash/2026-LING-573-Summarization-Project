We present a case of a 26-year-old male, who complained of headache with a raised, painless lesion on his left frontal region [Figure –] since 2.5 years before admission. On physical examination, there was a single, skin colored, firm nodule, measuring 7 cm in diameter, with a fixated base. An excision biopsy was suggested to diagnose the tumor and to predict tumor behavior. A 2-cm margin of normal appearing scalp is incised down to the underlying periosteum, wide range of the incision was taken since the propensity of tumor spread along their facial planes; followed by undermining the defect with blunt-tipped scissors after removal of the specimen to minimize tension on the wound margin. Subsequently, an excision biopsy was performed, and final diagnosis of DFSP was made based on the histopathological findings. Hematoxylin and eosin stained sections showed a densely cellular and poorly circumscribed tumor in the dermis layer, comprising of interwoven bundles and fascicles of uniform spindle-shaped cells arranged in “storiform” or “cartwheel” pattern [Figure and ]. The tumor cells had monotonous appearance with oval nuclei, vesicular chromatin, inconspicuous nucleoli, and scanty to moderate cytoplasm . A panel of immunohistochemistry (IHC) comprising vimentin and CD-34 was applied. Tumor cells were positive for both vimentin and CD-34, respectively [Figure and ].
He was discharged from our hospital uneventfully, and no recurrence of the mass was detected at 1-year follow-up.
