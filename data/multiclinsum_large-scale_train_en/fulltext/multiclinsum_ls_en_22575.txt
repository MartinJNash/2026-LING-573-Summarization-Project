An 80-year-old woman developed SAH (Hunt and Konik Grade II) due to a ruptured aneurysm at the anterior communicating artery (ACoA). The preoperative CTA revealed the 4.0 mm aneurysm [arrowhead, ] and the A1 portion of the anterior cerebral artery [arrow, ] with a wide description range from the main trunk to the peripheral parts . We performed left frontotemporal craniotomy and clipping using the YASARGIL titanium clip (Aesculap, Tuttlingen, Germany). On the 5 postoperative day, 3.0T TOF-MRA did not describe the left A1 [arrow, ], but 3.0T UTE-MRA described it [arrow, ]. Both sequences described the cerebral arteries from the main trunk to the peripheral parts with a wide description range, which enabled the assessment of cerebral vasospasm [ and f]. In addition, compared to the preoperative CTA , postoperative 3.0T UTE-MRA revealed the arteries in the vicinity of the clip, the bilateral A1, A2 portions, and the ACoA, in detail [ and i]. In the postoperative course, we confirmed no aneurysm regrowth nor postoperative cerebral vasospasm using 3.0T UTE-MRA, and the patient was discharged with a modified Rankin Scale score 0 on the 28th postoperative day.
