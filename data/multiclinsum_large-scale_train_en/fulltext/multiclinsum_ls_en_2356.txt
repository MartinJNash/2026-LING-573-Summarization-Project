A healthy 16-year-old Korean male presented to our clinic in September 26, 2018 with blurred vision in his left eye. He had neither past medical history nor trauma history.
On ocular examination, best corrected visual acuity (BCVA) was 0 logMAR in his right eye and Hand motion in his left eye. Intraocular pressures were 17 mmHg in the right eye and 13 mmHg in the left eye. The fundus was invisible due to massive VH in the left eye . His blood pressure and laboratory test results including coagulating factors were normal.
The VH partially decreased 2 weeks later and the BCVA improved to 0.4 logMAR in the left eye. No cause of VH other than the PHA was found . Slit-lamp examination demonstrated a Mittendorf’s dot located in the inferior nasal quadrant of the posterior lens capsule in the left eye . Optical coherence tomography (OCT) showed hyporeflective tubular structure of the PHA and elevated tissue structure of the optic nerve (Bergmeister’s papilla) . OCT angiography could not demonstrate the active blood flow of the PHA due to the technical limitation. However, we could find the blood flow in the Bergmeister’s papilla . The VH was spontaneously disappeared after 2 months. No serious disorders have been observed in the left eye during the follow-up period.
