On April 13, 2020, a 34-year old female patient at 31, 5/7 wk of pregnancy was admitted to our hospital due to the abnormalities on fetal imaging examination 10 d previously.
There was no history of present disease.
There was no history of past disease.
The family history was unremarkable.
Physical examination on admission showed no abnormalities.
Laboratory examinations showed no abnormalities.
Fetal ultrasound performed at 30, 2/7 wk of pregnancy showed that the anterior horn of the lateral ventricle of the fetus was widened by 10 mm and 5 mm on the right and left, respectively . A cystic dark area 9 mm × 9 mm × 8 mm in size was seen near the right eye, suggesting the possibility of a nasolacrimal duct cyst .
MRI performed at 31, 1/7 wk of pregnancy showed craniosynostosis in the coronal position, and the fetal skull was narrow and pointed . In the sagittal position, the forehead was uplifted and raised, the nasal root was slightly concave , eye distance was widened (the ratio of inner to outer diameter was 0.4) . Cystic shadows were seen at the inner edges of both eyes, the diameter was 3.8 mm on the left and 5.7 mm on the right, indicating a nasolacrimal duct cyst on both sides . Both sides of the anterior horn of the lateral ventricle were widened, about 7.4 mm on the right side and 5.7 mm on the left. The posterior cranial fossa was widened and the width was 10.4 mm . The midline above the cerebellum showed a cystoid shadow of about 12 mm × 10 mm × 22 mm, suggesting an arachnoid cyst . MRI also showed syndactyly of the hands and feet .
Three-dimensional (3D) prenatal ultrasound performed on the same day as MRI showed craniosynostosis , forehead protrusion , widened anterior horns of the lateral ventricle , nasolacrimal duct cyst and syndactyly .
