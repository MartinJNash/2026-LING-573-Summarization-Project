A 33-year-old male with AS (i.e., initial symptoms 2014 finally diagnosed 2017) presented with a progressive paraparesis of 5 months duration. Total spine radiographs showed AS . OLF was documented at the T9-T10 level on the T2 weighted MR where the typical beak shape hypointense right-sided dorsolateral mass was identified seen dorsolaterally on the right. The CT further confirmed the T9-T10 AS/OLF [ and ]. The T9-T10 laminectomy revealed a large right-sided OLF ossified mass that was densely adherent to the dura; it was carefully dissected free and removed. Two months later, the patient’s neurological exam was much improved (i.e., preoperative mJOA score of 7 to a postoperative mJOA score of 9).
