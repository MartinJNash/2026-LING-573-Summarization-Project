A 72-year-old male presented with slowly progressive incoordination and mild lower extremity weakness. The computed tomography (CT) scan documented an extradural T5/T6 lesion with the right T5/6 foraminal bony remodeling/extension. The MR further demonstrated that the lesion was well-circumscribed, and occupied the right T5/T6 dorsolateral spinal canal; it was hypointense on T1/hyperintense on T2-weighted images, and homogeneously enhanced with contrast . Although it caused thoracic cord compression, there was no hyperintense intramedullary cord signal on the T2-weighted study. A second left C7/T1 asymptomatic foraminal lesion showed similar CT/MR imaging characteristics, but was not pathologically confirmed to be a SCH. Of interest, neither lesion involved the vertebral bodies themselves, nor spinal angiography documented no high-flow vascular malformation at either level.
Following a T5-6 laminectomy, a soft, purple, well-circumscribed mass was easily dissected away from the underlying dura, allowing for complete resection in a piecemeal manner.
The histopathology of the T5/T6 lesion proved to be an epidural SCH.
Postoperatively, the patient experienced a full neurological recovery.
