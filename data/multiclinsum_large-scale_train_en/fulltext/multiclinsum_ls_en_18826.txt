A 22-year-old male presented with progressive weakness and wasting between the thumb and index finger of his left hand. He has a history of similar symptoms on his right hand. He had trouble writing and picking up small objects. Weakness in both arms gradually progressed over 4 years before he presented to our hospital. He also complaints of tremors in bilateral hands. There is no history of trauma, tingling, or numbness. The patient’s medical and surgical history is unremarkable. The patient reports no prior use of medications and has no known allergies. No familial occurrences of similar illnesses were identified during the patient’s family history assessment. Additionally, the patient denies any history of smoking, alcohol intake, or recreational drug use.
