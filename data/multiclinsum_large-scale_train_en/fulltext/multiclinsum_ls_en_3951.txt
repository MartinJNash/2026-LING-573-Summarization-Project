A 55-year-old gentleman was referred to the hematology clinic to evaluate high Hgb, which was incidentally noted before blood donation. He reports no specific symptoms related to the high Hgb. The patient is a nonsmoker, but he is morbidly obese, with a weight of 134 kg, a height of 171 cm, and a body mass index of 45.1.
His medical history includes controlled hypertension and type 2 diabetes mellitus. Additionally, he is being treated for OSA with a home continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) machine. His complete blood count test revealed elevated Hgb and Hct as shown in .
The EPO level was 3.12 mIU/mL (2.59–18.5 mIU/mL) and JAK2 V617F mutation was positive. The bone marrow examination revealed a hypercellular marrow with panmyelosis and pleomorphic megakaryocytes, consistent with overall findings indicative of a JAK2-positive MPN.
The diagnosis of PV was established based on the WHO criteria 2016, fulfilling all the three major criteria. The patient was initiated on aspirin and hydroxyurea.
