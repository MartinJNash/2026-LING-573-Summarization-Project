A 44-year-old Taiwanese male, an office worker, represented to us with a 2-year history of a productive cough.
He had experienced 3 episodes of pneumonia in the previous 2 years. After empiric treatment with antibiotics including levofloxacin 750 mg PO for 1 wk in the past 3 episodes, the symptoms subsided. At the time of presentation, the patient seemed uncomfortable and feverish.
He had been healthy before the cough developed.
He was a non-smoker and no specific family history.
Physical examination showed decreased breathing in the right lower lung field.
Serum laboratory data, including tumor markers such as carcinoembryonic antigen (1.91 ng/mL) and anti-squamous cell carcinoma antigen antibody (0.60 ng/mL), were within normal limit.
A plain chest radiograph demonstrated focal atelectasis of the right lower lobe of the lung. Contrast-enhanced thoracic computed tomography was also performed, and revealed bronchiectasis, right lower lobe, and a nodule with a lower density material over the right hilar region . The differential diagnosis included hematoma or mucoid impaction soft-tissue lesions .
Bronchoscopy revealed an endobronchial lobulated protruded mass in front of bronchi 9 and 10 . An endobronchial biopsy was performed with forceps. The pathological report revealed atypical cell nests with hyperchromatic nuclei. But it did not lead to a diagnosis. Therefore, he underwent surgical intervention. The final pathological report revealed glandular papilloma of lung.
