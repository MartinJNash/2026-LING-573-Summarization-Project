A 65-year-old woman, nonsmoker, was diagnosed in our outpatient cabinet with a single pulmonary nodule in the left lower lobe. Positron emission tomography was performed, and a significant SUV uptake of 8.9 was observed in the nodule . The patient had a persistent cough for 3 weeks, she did not report fever, and her laboratory findings were within normal range. It was decided to have a radial-endobronchial ultrasound with C-ARM for diagnosis with ROSE and convex-endobronchial ultrasound with rapid on-site evaluation for staging . Indeed, the ROSE technique evaluated non-small-cell lung cancer (NSCLC) for the single pulmonary nodule, and lymph node staging by ROSE revealed no metastasis in the lymph nodes or other organs. Local radiofrequency ablation with an endoscopic radiofrequency probe was proposed.
