TC, a 62-year-old male, quadriplegic secondary to a diving accident 34 years previously (at which time he was treated with a C4–C6 anterior cervical decompression and fusion), presented with dysautonomia. He had received an intrathecal phenol injection for spasticity several years later.
When MR demonstrated a large syrinx extending from T6 through the conus, he was scheduled to undergo drainage of the syrinx [Figure and ].
A laminectomy of T12 through L1 was performed and the intact dura was incised in the midline. Significant calcification was discovered . Extension of the laminectomy up to T11 and down to L2 revealed further calcification. After removing a significant amount of calcification, the tip of the conus was opened and a shunt was passed in, draining cerebrospinal fluid (CSF).
Postoperatively, TC's dysautonomia regressed. Notably, a thoracic CT scan [Figure and ] demonstrated a rim of calcification surrounding the entire cord and tapering to a posterior semi-circle in the upper thorax extending up to the T5–T6 level.
