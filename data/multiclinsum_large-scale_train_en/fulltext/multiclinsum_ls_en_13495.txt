This report describes the case of a 9-year-old male who has been admitted on August 02, 2022, and who has a large mass on the anteromedial aspect of his right lower thigh over the past 6 months. The swelling had well-defined edges, was slow-growing, painless, soft, and non-tender; it was also immobile and not attached to the skin. He had no past trauma, no complaints of pain, and no movement limitations. Before, there had been no interventions. Both a drug history and a family history were absent.
On clinical examination, an oval-shaped, elastic, immobile, and non-tender mass of approximately 10 cm × 6 cm was found on the right lower thigh anteromedial aspect. There was no sensory impairment in the right thigh of any kind.
On X-ray of the right lower thigh, there was a shadow of poorly specific soft-tissue enlargement in the lower thigh anteromedially ( and ).
On magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) of right lower thigh (plain) revealed a large 10 cm × 6 cm × 8 cm well-defined, heterointense, lobulated, and non-enhancing, primarily fat intensity lesion was identified in the anteromedial region of the right femur’s distal end and was fully distinct from the surrounding muscles ( and ).
On August 05, 2022 the patient underwent surgery under spinal anesthesia. The patient position with semiflexed knee and hip in abduction with bolster supports on lateral thigh. Longitudinal vertical incision was taken over the anteromedial aspect of lower thigh over the tumor . Subcutaneous layers were dissected and sartorius muscle retracted medially. The roof of tumor was formed by vastus medialis muscle, which was separated with the help of unipolar cautery. 10 cm × 6 cm × 8 cm sized tumor was under the Vastus medialis muscle displacing it medially . The periosteal attachment was undocked, and the tumor was removed . After achieving hemostasis, wound sutured with a vaccum drain tube in situ. The specimen weighed 350 g. Postoperatively, a Robert Jones compression bandage was applied with limb elevation. On the tenth postoperative day, stitches were taken out. The healing process was uneventful.
On gross pathology revealed a multilobulated, well-defined, irregular mass measuring 10 cm × 6 cm × 8 cm . The cut surface was uniformly sticky, mushy, and yellow . On histopathology , the lesion was made up of mature lipocytes that were closely connected to the periosteum, which is typical of a parosteal lipoma. There were no lipoblasts or cellular atypia present.
