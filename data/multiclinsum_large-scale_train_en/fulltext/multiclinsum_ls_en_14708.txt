A 19-year-old man was brought by ambulance to the emergency department with left-sided testicular pain. He reported sudden severe, non-radiating left testicular pain that woke him from sleep 30 minutes prior to arrival. He presented in severe pain, tremulous from discomfort. His exam revealed a high-riding, firm, left testicle in a horizontal lie with absent cremasteric reflex. Urology was consulted and a comprehensive ultrasound ordered. The patient’s testicles were immediately examined with point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS) . After receiving 100 micrograms of fentanyl, manual detorsion was conducted, and repeat ultrasonography was performed . The patient was then transported for comprehensive ultrasonography .
