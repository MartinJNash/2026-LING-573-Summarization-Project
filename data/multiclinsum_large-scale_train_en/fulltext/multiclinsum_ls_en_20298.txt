A 72-year-old male with a history of hypertension, dyslipidemia, CKD 3b (chronic kidney disease) presented to the emergency department with constipation for 3 months. Vital signs were stable. Routine laboratory studies revealed AST 133, ALT 68, alkaline phosphatase 748, and a normal total bilirubin. On physical examination, his abdomen was distended and non-tender. Computed tomography of the abdomen demonstrated an ill-defined 7.2 × 4.9 cm hypodense lesion in the GB fossa with extension into the hepatic parenchyma . A needle biopsy of the GB was performed. Histopathology of the biopsy revealed a large cell NET . Immunohistochemical analysis showed tumor cells were positive for CD56, synaptophysin, chromogranin, CK 20, and Ki67 and negative for TTF1 and CK7 favoring the GI origin of NEC . PET scan showed extensive involvement in liver and peritoneum . He was started on palliative chemotherapy with Topotecan and ultimately was provided with comfort care and was discharged to hospice house.
