A 77-year-old man visited our outpatient department for further examination and treatment of a left inguinal mass discovered incidentally on contrast-enhanced CT. His physical examination revealed a solid, painless, mobile tumor measuring approximately 40 mm in the largest dimension. On CT, the mass contacted with the left spermatic cord and exhibited high enhancement . T2-weighted images of MRI demonstrated the tumor had high signal intensity , and fat-suppressed T2-weighted images indicated there was no fat tissue in the tumor . On DWI, the tumor exhibited diffusion restriction . Blood and urine examinations revealed no abnormal findings.
Based on his age, the location and imaging findings, myxoid liposarcoma was suspected. The patient underwent left high inguinal orchiectomy with complete resection of the tumor. Macroscopically, the mass measuring 30 × 25 mm had not infiltrated the testis or the spermatic cord . Microscopically, the specimen was well circumscribed and exhibited proliferation of spindle-shaped cells and vessels and a mixture of collagen fibers. The cells had no atypism or abnormal mitosis . Immunohistochemical examination showed the cells were slightly positive for CD34 and negative for αSMA, desmin, AE1/AE3, S100 protein, STAT6, CDK4, and MDM2. The preoperative diagnosis of myxoid liposarcoma was excluded, and the postoperative diagnosis of CAF was made. The patient remains free of disease recurrence 12 months after surgery.
