A healthy 40-year-old man presented with a painless left infra-auricular swelling, which had been present for about 2 years. Clinical examination revealed an oval, firm, non-tender mass, which was not adherent to the overlying skin in the left parotid gland region and which showed normal facial nerve function. No other abnormality was detected in the patient. Computed tomography (CT) revealed a heterogeneously enhancing lesion with cystic necrosis in the left parotid region. Fine needle aspiration cytology (FNAC) revealed a benign lesion with no malignant cells. The lesion was considered as a benign parotid gland lesion. During surgery and after identification of the main trunk of the facial nerve, the superficial part of the parotid gland was started to be removed. A single large mass of 6×3 cm incorporating the upper division of the facial nerve was observed .
Dissection of the mass from the facial nerve failed so the nerve portion, which was involved in the mass (nearly 1.5 cm), was inevitably sacrificed with immediate tensionless neuroraphy of the upper division of the facial nerve with 6/0 prolene. The final histopathology revealed a neurofibroma of the facial nerve. Complete left side facial nerve paralysis was observed immediately postoperatively and, through patient follow up, the function of the lower half was returned within 4 months and the upper half was returned after 1 year .
Currently, after 3 years of follow up, there are no signs of recurrence with normal facial nerve function.
