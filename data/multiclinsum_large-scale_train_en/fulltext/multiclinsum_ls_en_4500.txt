A 53 years old diabetic man presented with progressive scalp swelling and headache for 2 weeks, associated with fever. Upon examination the swelling is located at the vertex of skull, with an opening with purulent discharge with cellulitis around the lesion. On examination the swelling is doughy in consistency with no fluctuation, cystic lesion is felt in the middle of the wound. The patient underwent excision of the infected cyst, debridement and drainage of an abscess. Tissue pathology revealed an infected sebaceous cyst and Staphylococcus Aureus was cultured. During post-operative period he improved slightly and cellulitis subsided.
On post-operative day 3, the wound was still discharging copious amount of pus though the patient was not febrile. A computed scan of the head revealed subgaleal collection with air pockets and evidence of outer plate of skull bone osteomyelitis . Patient was taken for surgical debridement of the infected galeal tissue, copious amount of pus was drained. The outer plate of skull bone was debrided. Two drains placed in the pocket above the eyebrows anteriorly and one in the occipital pocket posteriorly. Negative pressure wound therapy (V.A.C) was used on the first day post-op that aided in resolution of the infection. The patient was continued on intravenous antibiotics. The patient had an uneventful recovery and was discharged home after 12 days. On follow up the skin defect healed completely and the patient didn’t need any skin flap .
