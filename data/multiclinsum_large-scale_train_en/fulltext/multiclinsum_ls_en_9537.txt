A 15 years old male with no past medical history, presented to the emergency department for oculofacial trauma after being kicked by a horse. Initial ophthalmologic examination of right eye (RE) showed eyelid hematoma, visual acuity (VA) was 10/10 in Snellen chart, the rest of examination including dilated ocular fundus was normal. Examination of the left eye (LE), exhibited eyelid hematoma, subconjunctival hemorrhage, VA was limited to light perception and there was a left relative afferent pupillary defect. Dilated fundus examination of the LE revealed an extensive vitreous and preretinal hemorrhage overlaying the optic disc and retina edema ( A). Ocular ultrasound revealed posterior vitreous detachment ( B) and computed tomography of the head and orbit showed bilateral lamina papyracea fracture.
The diagnosis of LE optic nerve head avulsion was made and the patient was admitted for hospital surveillance for 10 days, there was no improvement of the final VA at the time of discharge.
Six months later, LE VA was detecting hand motion, fundus examination revealed fibroglial scarring in the optic disc area, some retinal pigment epithelium hyperplasia and narrowing of retinal vessels ( A). Fluorescein angiography (FA) showed hyperfluorescence of the fibroglial tissue ( B). Ss OCT exhibited the fibroglial scarring as well as a marked thinning of the macula ( C).
Five years after the accident, VA of the RE had remained 10/10 and on the LE, VA was detecting hand motion, fundus examination revealed a superior dragging of the optic disc, fibroglial scarring, retinal vessel narrowing and retinal epithelium hyperplasia ( A). FA showed hyperfluorescence of the cicatricial tissue ( B). SS OCT exhibited disorganisation of internal retinal layers and macular thinning ( C).
