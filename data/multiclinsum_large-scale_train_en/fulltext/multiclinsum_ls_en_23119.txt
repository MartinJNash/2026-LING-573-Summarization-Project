The 50-year-old female patient, with unexplained deafness on her left side for more than 10 years, complained of involuntary left HFS for 8 years. After admission, the ECG before surgery showed third-degree atrioventricular block . Due to the fear of surgery, heart risk and fears related to her father’s death after the implantation of a permanent cardiac pacemaker 2 years previously, the patient previously chose botulinum toxin therapy multiple times until this therapy failed to control the spasm. On admission, she had a previous history of hypertension but no chest pain or chest tightness. Except for left deafness and left HFS, her other neurological examination was normal. MRI examination showed that bilateral vertebral arteries were located on the left side of the brain stem, and the offending artery compressed the facial nerve at this root entry/exit zone (REZ) .
She agreed to be implanted with a temporary cardiac pacemaker , not a permanent one, to receive an MVD according to cardiac experts’ advice. The endoscopy-assisted MVD was performed successfully on 2019-11-14 under neuro-electrophysiological examination, with the heart rate set at 60 times/min . After the MVD, the patient’s facial spasm and abnormal muscle response (AMR) disappeared . The pacing wire was removed on the second day after surgery. The patient recovered and was discharged from the hospital.
Her ECG 5 months after surgery still showed the third-degree atrioventricular block. The patient had returned to her daily life and was asymptomatic.
