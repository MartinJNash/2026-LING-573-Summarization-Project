A 70 year old, white, British male patient was admitted as an emergency with right iliac fossa pain, tenderness, and raised inflammatory markers. A computed tomography scan of his abdomen showed an appendix mass with a small abscess and a linear opaque foreign body in it . His symptoms subsided completely on conservative management with intravenous antibiotics. Colonoscopy two months later did not show anything untoward. At a follow up review in surgical clinic, appendicectomy was not deemed necessary and the patient was discharged from further follow up.
The patient presented again to us a few months later with recurrent symptoms in the right iliac fossa and raised inflammatory markers. A repeat computed tomography scan of the abdomen showed a persistent abscess in the area with the same foreign body which had migrated into the iliopsoas muscle . The patient underwent appendicectomy, drainage of abscess and removal of the foreign body . He made a satisfactory postoperative recovery and was well at the time of his clinic appointment 3 months after the surgery.
