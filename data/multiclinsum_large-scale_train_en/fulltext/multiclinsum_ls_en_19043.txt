An 82-year-old woman being treated for pyelonephritis was referred to our hospital because of a nodule in the left anterior segment of the lung on chest CT. The nodule was part solid, 1.9 × 1.1 cm in size and reveal a pleural tag . The maximum standardized uptake value of the tumor is 5.7 by 18F-fluorodeoxyglucose-positron emission tomography. The nodule was not confirmed by any histopathological examination; however, it was strongly suspected to be lung adenocarcinoma. Brain magnetic resonance imaging and abdominal CT revealed no obvious metastases. The clinical stage was T1bN0M0 stage IA2 as categorized by the UICC TNM Classification (8th edition). Spirometry demonstrated an FEV1.0 of 1.47 L and FEV1.0% of 53.4%. Moreover, the cardiac function was maintained to be normal.
We recommended the patient to undergo a lung resection for the diagnosis and treatment of the nodule, and she agreed. We believed that this lesion could be completely resected by anterior and lingular segmentectomy based on preoperative imaging diagnosis. However, if the nodule is not palpated, then a sufficient tumor margin may not be secured; thus, we decided to perform a CT-guided nodule marking prior to the surgery.
