A-46-year old male presented to the emergency department complaining from abdominal pain and repeated bilious vomiting after blunt abdominal trauma due to a car accident.
The patient was admitted to the emergency ward, during examination the patient had tachycardia with normal blood pressure, the patients had no features of allergic reaction. Abdominal examination showed asymmetrical counter of the abdomen with visible bulging in the right flank, the patient had generalized abdominal tenderness with guarding, the patient had umbilical hernia for three years, . There past medical and surgical histories were negative.
CT-scan of the abdomen showed a large hydatid cyst (24 cm x 14 cm) in the right lobe of the liver, with free fluid in the peritoneal cavity suggesting ruptured of the cyst, .
During laparotomy the peritoneal cavity was filled with bile stained fluid with multiple floating daughter cysts inside, , .
Aspiration of the fluid was done and extraction of a countless number of daughter cysts from the peritoneal cavity and from the cyst cavity in the right lobe of the liver was done, areas of bile leak from the inside of the cyst were sutured with slowly absorbable suture material, .
The patient was discharged eight days later, the drain remained inside for twenty-five days when the bile leak stopped then. The patient received anthelminthic drugs for three months after the operation.
