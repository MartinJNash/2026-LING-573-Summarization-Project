A 59-year-old male was diagnosed with stage III carcinoma of the external auditory canal, with no apparent brain metastases. The patient had no previous medical history of atrial fibrillation, ischemic heart disease, or stroke. After cancer was removed, the patient was treated with cisplatin to prevent cancer recurrence. Seven days after cisplatin administration, he suddenly presented with a disturbance of consciousness. Head magnetic resonance imaging showed basilar artery occlusion, which was associated with chemotherapy administration . Once diagnosed, an endovascular stent retrieval thrombectomy was performed [Figure and ]. The patient recovered consciousness after thrombectomy, although there were some ischemic lesions visible on diffusion-weighted images . The patient then began taking a direct oral anticoagulant to prevent thrombosis. Serum von Willebrand factor (vWf) activity increased during the occurrence of cerebral infarction. This vWf activity gradually decreased after the cerebral infarction and normalized 5 months after cisplatin administration . The patient did not experience a recurrence of cerebral infarction after cisplatin administration.
