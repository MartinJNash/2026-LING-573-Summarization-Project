A 33-year-old male presented after a high-velocity motor vehicle collision. On presentation, his Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) was 3T, with reactive pupils but no corneal/cough/gag reflexes. Imaging demonstrated AOD , with associated intraventricular hemorrhage (IVH) and subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH) . An external ventricular drain (EVD) was placed for low GCS and progressive dilatation of the ventricles. Shortly thereafter, he was able to follow simple commands with the right extremities. He underwent occiput to C4 fixation/fusion. His EVD was weaned within 7 days. He continued to progress neurologically. At 1 month, he exhibited bilateral abducens nerve palsies, worsening headaches, and fatigue. A computed tomography (CT) of the head showed interval enlargement of his ventricles . He underwent a ventriculoperitoneal shunt placement. At discharge, 2 months after presentation, he was doing well, ambulating with stand-by assist/walker.
