A 59-year-old male on prophylactic antiplatelet therapy was admitted to our institute with sudden headache and dysphasia, accompanied by nausea and vomiting. The patient complained of the following influenza symptoms: Weakness, fever, runny nose for the last 7 days. There is no history of traumatic events. Laboratory tests showed coagulation indices within normal limits. Computed tomography scan (CT scan) of the head showed an acute subdural hematoma that extended from the left frontal lobe to the left temporal lobe .
A left pterional craniotomy was performed. A massive acute subdural hematoma was evacuated and an extra-axial lesion was identified on the left sphenoid wing with evidence of tumor bleeding. A Simpson grade I excision was performed. The histopathological diagnosis deposed for angiomatous and papillary meningioma with angio-invasiveness aspects of the dura . Postoperative magnetic resonance (MR) showed no residual tumor . Follow-up at 1 year showed no recurrence of the disease in the absence of neurologic deficits.
