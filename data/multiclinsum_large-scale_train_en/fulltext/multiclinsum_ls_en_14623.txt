A 69-year-old non-smoking female with hypertension, dyslipidaemia, hypothyroidism, and no prior cardiac history presented with atypical chest pain to the outpatient department. Her medication included pravastatin 20 mg, lisinopril 20 mg, and levothyroxine 75 μg. General physical examination was with in normal limits. Cardiovascular and respiratory system examination was normal. Electrocardiogram and left ventricular ejection fraction were normal. According to the 2019 ESC guidelines, the pre-test probability of CAD was 11%, so computed tomography (CT) coronary angiography (CCTA) was done in an outpatient setting at the same appointment and showed a lipid-rich plaque with very low attenuation (<30 HU) in the mid left anterior descending (LAD) artery, and in the left main stem (LMS) extending into the proximal LAD . The maximum plaque burden in the LMS was 67% with a remodelling index of 1.375, and an area and diameter stenosis of 22% and 11%, respectively.
