We present the case of a 63 years old Caucasian female, referred to our echocardiography lab for routine evaluation 5 years after chemotherapy including cyclophosphamid and stem cell transplantation for chronic myeloid leukemia. She was in good general condition and asymptomatic. Physical examination, electrocardiography, prior thoracic X-ray and CT-scan were unremarkable. On trans-thoracic echocardiography (TTE), performed with a Philips iE33, a DOMV could be detected in standard 2-dimensional views without stenosis or regurgitation . We classified the DOMV as a complete bridge type with a fibrous bridge separating the valve into two orifices with a slightly larger anterolateral orifice. Each orifice had its own leaflets and subvalvular apparatus, each connected to one papillary muscle resulting in a double parachute appearance in apical views . Two separate jets of antegrade flow into the left ventricle were detected with color Doppler flow . We furthermore evaluated the valve with three dimensional TTE , illustrating the relative dimensions of the two orifices. Interestingly, no other congenital cardiac anomaly or structural cardiopathy was found. In the absence of cardiac symptoms in this patient with a DOMV with normal function no further diagnostic or therapeutic steps were necessary. The patient was instructed to return to our clinic if any symptoms should evolve.
