A phenotypically normal 21-year-old female with an obstetric history of gravida 1 and para 0 (G1P0) requested an amniocentesis at the 18th week of gestation because of anxiety. Although prenatal ultrasound examinations appeared normal, amniocentesis revealed a sex chromosome-discordant chimeric karyotype with trisomy 21 in the XX lineage (47,XX,+21/46,XY). After receiving genetic counselling, she and her non-consanguineous husband, a 29-year-old with acquired mutism caused by suspected viral infection at the age of 7 months, decided to terminate the pregnancy via induced vaginal delivery at the 21st week. They agreed to donate their blood and the aborted fetus for further medical research.
