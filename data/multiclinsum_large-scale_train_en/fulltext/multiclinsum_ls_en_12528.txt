During the routine dissection of a 78-year-old male cadaver, an unusual source of the middle meningeal artery was noted. The variation was found on the left side only, and no other intracranial variations were noted. The donor died from pneumonia. The dura mater was cut circumferentially after removing the calvaria with a bone saw. The brain was removed. Laterally, the distribution of the middle meningeal artery was typical. However, medially, the vessel’s origin was from the petrous portion of the ICA . The artery entered the floor of the middle cranial fossa from the petrous part of the ICA by traveling through a small unnamed foramen. The left foramen spinosum was absent in its typical position posterolateral to the foramen ovale. The vessel size was comparable to the normally arising middle meningeal artery.
