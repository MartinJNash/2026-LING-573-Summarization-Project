A 33-year-old male presented to OPD with swelling over the right knee joint . Clinically the swelling was adhered to skin, presence of hair follicles, bony hard, tender, and movements of right knee were painful. X-ray right knee joint showed a calcified mass over the anterior aspect of the knee joint . The patient was investigated and once fit was taken for surgery. The patient was treated surgically by excising the swelling. Complete excision was performed and the swelling was sent for histopathological examination. The report showed it was calcinosis cutis circumscripta . The patient was given short hinge knee brace for 2 weeks till the sutures were removed. After 2 weeks, he was allowed full weight bearing on the affected limb. At 2 yrs follow-up, patient has functional range of movement and there is no recurrence of the swelling.
