During a dengue outbreak in 2013 in the state of Rio de Janeiro, a 54-year-old gentleman presented to an urgent care facility with fever and dyspnoea. Initially, the diagnosis of community acquired pneumonia was made and the patient was discharged home with a prescription for a 10 day course of oral amoxicillin with clavulanic acid, 500 mg every 8 h. Three days later, he was readmitted with severe headache, fever and confusion, with peripheral cyanosis and a petechial rash. On laboratory testing, the patient was found to be thrombocytopenic and had acute renal failure, requiring urgent haemodialysis. Due to the presenting clinical picture, blood samples were collected to test for dengue and meningococcal infection. Blood cultures were negative for N. meningitidis. A lumbar puncture with cerebral spinal fluid analysis disclosed an opening pressure of 30 cm H2O, a cell count of 750 white blood cells per field (90 % neutrophils), protein levels of 102 mg dl−1 and glucose levels of 45 mg dl−1. Cerebral spinal fluid cultures were not performed by the admitting hospital. The patient was immediately started on ceftriaxone, 2 intravenously every 12 h. The results of NS1 antigen testing and RT-qPCR for dengue were positive for DENV-4 and anti-dengue IgM was negative. A nspA-PCR assay performed to detect meningococcal disease confirmed N. meningitidis infection, and serogroup C was determined by siadA-PCR . The amplified nspA DNA fragment was sequenced in both directions and confirmed as the nspA gene. The DNA sequence was submitted to GenBank under accession number . Unfortunately, after 5 days in a critical care unit, the patient developed multi-system organ failure and died. The medical team in charge reported meningococcal disease as the cause of death on his death certificate.
