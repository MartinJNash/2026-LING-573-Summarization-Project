We present a case of a 22-year-old male with history of medically refractory epilepsy since age 7 after suffering from viral encephalitis. He typically has 1–3 seizures per month. Scalp EEG monitoring was able to localize onset to the left temporal lobe and/or left parieto-occipital lobe. Most of the seizures during the study were complex partial with unresponsiveness, orofacial automatisms and right hand twitching. One seizure was atypical, starting with visual changes and appeared to have onset from the left occipital region. MRI was consistent with left mesial temporal sclerosis as well as left parieto-occipital encephalomalacia related to prior encephalitic infarct . PET scan was pertinent for decreased metabolism in the left mesial temporal lobe. Ictal SPECT was consistent with a left temporal focus. WADA demonstrated left hemispheric language and adequate memory function bilaterally and visual field testing was normal. The patient met criteria for major depressive disorder with psychiatric features on psychiatry evaluation.
