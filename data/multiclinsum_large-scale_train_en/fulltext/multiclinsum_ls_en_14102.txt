A 61-year-old female patient presented with FI of subacute onset (i.e. occurring up to 6 times a week) for the past 5 weeks (Cleveland Clinic Fecal Incontinence Score [CCFIS] 18; severe incontinence). She had no accompanying; back pain, saddle anesthesia, radiculopathy, myelopathy, or urinary incontinence.
The high-resolution manometry (HRAM-pressure reading) of the anal canal and colonoscopy was normal. The MR imaging of the lumbar spine showed a large left-sided LDH at the L5-S1 level [ and ] resulting in significant cauda equina compression. Following and urgent microdiscectomy, the patient demonstrated significant improvement in her FI (i.e. FI [CCFIS 13; moderate incontinence]).
