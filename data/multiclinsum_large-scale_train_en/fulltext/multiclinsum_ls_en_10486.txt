A 32-year-old woman presented with dull left flank pain. Physical examination was normal. Abdominal ultrasonography revealed a solid mass with a diameter of 7 cm in the left renal hilum. Abdominal CT scan showed a single well-defined homogeneously enhanced mass close to the left renal pedicle with no calcification or necrosis . Laboratory data including catecholamine metabolites were in normal range. Chest and abdominopelvic CT scans were normal otherwise. The mass was resected completely laparoscopically using the transperitoneal approach. Although the mass was close to the renal vessels, there were no significant adhesions to the pelvis and to the renal parenchyma itself. The renal vessels were effectively shaved off the mass and the mass was resected with satisfactory margin. The mass was close to the renal vessels; however, the planes were distinguishable during laparoscopy and tumor was resected with acceptable margins.
The pathologic examination was compatible with Castleman disease . This woman underwent follow-up with no adjunctive therapy. No evidence of recurrence was present in the CT scan imaging at 1-year follow-up.
