A 33-year-old woman, living in a rural area in the vicinity of Qom, Iran, without any underlying disease visited our center with the chief complaint of a wound on her right wrist. According to the patient, the wound had developed following small vesicles, while she had no history of trauma such as cut, scratch, scrape, puncture wound, bruise, etc. The wound was painless with no discharge, and the patient had decided to have a medical examination 10 days after the onset of the symptoms due to the progression and appearance of the wound and its itching . Following physical examination, no systemic symptoms were observed in the patient.
