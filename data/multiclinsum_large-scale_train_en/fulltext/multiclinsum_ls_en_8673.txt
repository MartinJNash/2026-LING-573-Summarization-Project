A 33-year-old male patient was referred to an oral pathology center for the evaluation of a well-demarcated, non-tender, pink to yellowish mass with a delicate papillary (roughened) surface on the right ventral surface of the tongue measured at 2.2 × 1.2 cm that had been present at least for 4 years .
The lesion consistency was soft on palpation. There was no evidence of extra-oral lesions or cervical lymph node enlargements, and the laboratory results were normal. With the provisional diagnosis of benign papillary lesions, such as large squamous papilloma, VX, and condyloma acuminatum, an excisional biopsy was performed under local anesthesia. Microscopic sections showed a papillary hyperparakeratotic acanthotic epithelium with parakeratin plugging between the papillary projections. Rete ridges were elongated to a uniform depth. The connective tissue papillae were composed of numerous large macrophages with foamy cytoplasm (xanthoma cells) (,). The diagnosis of oral VX was confirmed considering clinical and histopathologic features. The patient was free of disease within 15 months following the operation. Written informed consent was obtained from the patient.
