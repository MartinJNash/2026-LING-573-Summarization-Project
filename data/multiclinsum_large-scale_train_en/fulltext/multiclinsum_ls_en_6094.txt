A 71-year-old woman presented with an intracranial aneurysm. The patient was earlier diagnosed with acute ischemic stroke in the right periventricular white matter and centrum semiovale at another hospital 2 months ago after exhibiting weakness in the left upper and lower extremities and was prescribed aspirin. On admission, mild dysarthria and left upper and lower extremity muscle weakness (Medical Research Council Grade IV) were observed. The brain magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) showed complete occlusion in the bilateral proximal MCA (M1) and a large 11 × 11 mm nonruptured cerebral aneurysm in the A3 segment of the left anterior cerebral artery (ACA) . Perfusion delay was observed in both MCA regions on the brain perfusion MRI. On transfemoral cerebral angiography, the patient was diagnosed with Suzuki grade VI moyamoya disease with bilateral MCA occlusion. After 7 years, a brain MRI was performed for the observation of the cerebral aneurysm. The aneurysm increased to 17 × 13 mm in size, but it remained unruptured. The patient provided informed consent, and the study conforms to the provisions of the Declaration of Helsinki (as revised in Tokyo 2004).
