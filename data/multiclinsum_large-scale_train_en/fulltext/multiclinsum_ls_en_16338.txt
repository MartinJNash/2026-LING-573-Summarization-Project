A 56-year old male with a history of hypertension presented to the emergency department with one hour of crushing chest pain radiating to the left arm and neck. The symptoms began following exertion, but failed to alleviate with rest. Initial electrocardiogram (ECG) demonstrated hyperacute T-waves with associated ST-segment depression in the precordial leads, consistent with a de Winter T-wave pattern . Due to concern for acute myocardial infarction (AMI), cardiology was consulted for possible percutaneous intervention (PCI).
Emergent cardiac catheterization was performed and revealed a 100% occlusion of the proximal left anterior descending artery . After successful PCI, the patient experienced no further complications and was subsequently discharged on appropriate medical management.
