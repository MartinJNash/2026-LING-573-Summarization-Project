A 43-year-old Japanese man presented with multiple chondromas and hemangiomas. His disease had been diagnosed as Maffucci syndrome. His available clinical information, however, was limited. One of the hemangiomas was removed and examined pathologically. Macroscopically, the lesion showed a serpentine appearance . The cut surface of the lesion showed a blackish area filled with blood and a whitish area .
The morphological picture was an admixture of cavernous hemangioma and spindle cell hemangioma without cytological atypia or mitosis. Sheets of vacuolated endothelial cells were also observed . In the cavernous component, organized thrombosis was observed (image not shown). No epithelioid hemangiomatous area was found.
