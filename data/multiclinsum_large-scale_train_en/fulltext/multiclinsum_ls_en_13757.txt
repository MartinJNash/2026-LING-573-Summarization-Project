A 67-year-old Chinese man was admitted to our hospital complaining of postoperative urinary leakage and incontinence for 11 mo.
The man was diagnosed with BPH following frequent urination and dysuria in April 2018. TURP was performed in the same month, and no malignant lesions were found. He had not undergone any previous surgeries. After removal of the catheter, unconscious leakage of urine was observed. Oral medicine, behavioral therapy, and other conservative treatments were ineffective. Up until presentation, urinary incontinence had gradually increased. The clinical manifestation was continuous leakage of urine; therefore, the patient was using five pads/d to ensure local dryness.
In April 2018, TURP was performed in the hospital due to BPH; the patient denied any history of other diseases and allergies.
No smoking or drinking history was reported; no genetic family medical history was reported.
Physical examination showed continuous leakage of urine but no sign of redness, swelling, or eczema on the skin around the penis.
Laboratory findings were unremarkable.
Routine examination, cystoscopy, urodynamics, and urethral pressure profilometry were performed. During cystoscopy, no urethral stricture was observed; however, the urethra showed incomplete closure . Urodynamics and urethral pressure profilometry showed normal bladder function and compliance. The MUP was 52 cmH2O and MUCP was 17 cmH2O .
