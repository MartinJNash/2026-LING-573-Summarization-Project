A 58-year-old man with a history of rheumatoid arthritis presented with several years of left knee pain. Evaluation showed severe left knee degenerative osteoarthritis complicated by the presence of a large lateral femoral condyle cyst. After failing conservative management, a robotic-assisted cementless cruciate-retaining TKA was indicated. The large bone cyst was managed with augmentation using synthetic bone grafting. 1 year postoperatively, he showed excellent clinical outcomes and radiographic evidence of osteointegration.
