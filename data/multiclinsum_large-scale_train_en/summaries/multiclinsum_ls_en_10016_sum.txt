We report the case of a 17 years old male patient reporting worsening shoulder pain since 3 years with right scapula winging. Computer tomography (CT) scan revealed an osteochondroma on the ventro-medial surface of the right scapula extending into the scapulo-thoracic space. Surgical excision was done and histopathological study confirmed osteochondroma of the scapula. We noted excellent post-operative pain alleviation after two weeks, full range of motion and a better self-esteem.
