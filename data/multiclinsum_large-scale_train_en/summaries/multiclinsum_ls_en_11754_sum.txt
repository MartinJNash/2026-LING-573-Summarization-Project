A 7-year-old child was brought to the emergency room with an alleged history of fall on outstretched hand sustaining injury to the left elbow. He was diagnosed with a posteromedial elbow dislocation, along with fracture of the lateral condyle. The patient underwent emergency reduction of the elbow under appropriate anesthesia following which there was persistence of varus and valgus instability, for which the lateral condyle was fixed with a standard pinning through a lateral approach with three Kirschner wires. The patient was immobilized for 6 weeks in a plaster, after which mobilization was started. At 3 months follow-up, the patient showed good functional outcome with full range of motion.
