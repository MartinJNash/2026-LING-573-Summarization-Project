A 72-year-old woman was diagnosed with vesicovaginal fistula induced by radiation therapy. Repair of the vesicovaginal fistula was achieved via laparoscopic approach. The fistula was exposed, followed by excision of fistula tract, fine dissection to achieve a traction-free approximation of bladder mucosa, then water-tight closure. An interposition graft derived from the perirectal fat was inserted to reduce the risk of repair failure. The patient did not have the incontinence problem at 1-year follow-up.
