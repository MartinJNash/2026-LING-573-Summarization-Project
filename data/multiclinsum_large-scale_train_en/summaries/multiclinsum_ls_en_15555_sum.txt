We report a case of a febrile postural instability as the primary presentation of an infective endocarditis secondary to a severe mitral stenosis in a young patient with a history of mitral stenosis for which he benefited from percutaneous mitral dilation. The diagnosis was based on the modified Duke criteria. In this case, the treatment was based mainly on antibiotic therapy. The outcome was favorable; with a clinical, biological and radiological improvement. The patient was subsequently referred to the cardiovascular department for surgical treatment of his valve disease.
