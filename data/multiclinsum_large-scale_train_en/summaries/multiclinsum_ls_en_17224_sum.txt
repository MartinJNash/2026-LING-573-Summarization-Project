A 66-year-old man presented with retrosternal pain after swallowing and underwent endoscopy. Pathological biopsy showed high-grade squamous intraepithelial neoplasia. The lesion was removed by endoscopic submucosal dissection (ESD) after magnification and endoscopic ultrasonography. Postoperative pathology proved that the lesion was collision cancer comprising SqCC and AdCC. After six months of postoperative follow-up, there was no recurrence of esophageal cancer.
