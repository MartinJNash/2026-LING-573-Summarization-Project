A 78-year-old woman with hyperthyroidism and paroxysmal atrial fibrillation (AF) presented with positional hypoxaemia exacerbated by supine positioning. Diagnostic testing revealed intermittent right-to-left shunting through a PFO triggered by worsening atrial functional tricuspid regurgitation and elevated right atrial pressures. Diuresis, rate control, and thyroidectomy initially led to resolution of positional hypoxaemia, but recurrent AF episodes triggered right-to-left shunting with recurrent desaturation. Left atrial and cavo-tricuspid isthmus ablation led to restoration of normal sinus rhythm and resolution of positional hypoxaemia without PFO closure.
