A 66-year-old man with a history of cluster headaches presented with a six-month history of a new constant right-sided headache. The new headaches were associated with tearing and redness of the right eye and responded to indomethacin, thus meeting the International Classification of Headache Disorders (ICHD-3) diagnostic criteria for hemicrania continua. The history and physical examination suggested a cervical source of the headache arising from the ipsilateral SCM muscle. Injection of the muscle with 1% lidocaine resulted in the elimination of the pain for 1 month without indomethacin.
