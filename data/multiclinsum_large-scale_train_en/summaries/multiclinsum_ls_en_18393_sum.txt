A 49-year-old man with no significant past medical history was transferred to our institution with sudden-onset fatigue from working on a farm. A 12-lead electrocardiogram showed monomorphic sustained wide QRS tachycardia with a right bundle branch block pattern and superior axis deviation with a cycle length (CL) of 300 ms. Sustained monomorphic VT originating from the left ventricle due to underlying vasospastic angina was diagnosed by contrast-enhanced cardiac magnetic resonance imaging, coronary angiography, and the acetylcholine stress test, and implantable cardioverter defibrillator implantation was performed. Nine months later, a clinical VT episode with a CL of 300 ms was observed, which could not be terminated by three sequences of conventional burst pacing. Ventricular tachycardia was finally terminated by a third iATP sequence without any acceleration.
