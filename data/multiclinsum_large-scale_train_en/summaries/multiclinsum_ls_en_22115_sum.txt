The patient was a 20-year-old male with bilateral CEU and glaucoma. The intraocular pressure (IOP) was 48 mm Hg in the right eye (OD) and 52 mm Hg in the left eye (OS). The vertical cup-to-disc (C/D) ratio was nearly 1.0 in both eyes. Despite maximum medical therapy, the target IOP could not be achieved. Therefore, CO2 laser-assisted sclerectomy surgery (CLASS) was performed in OS, and the IOP was remarkably decreased. 1 month after the surgery, the IOP rebounded slightly and was controlled with a fixed-combination anti-glaucoma medicine. 3-month postoperatively, a YAG laser goniopuncture (LGP) was performed to enhance the IOP-lowing effect and the anti-glaucoma agent was discontinued. An ab externo microcatheter-assisted trabeculotomy (MAT) was performed in OD, and the IOP was also significantly decreased. During the follow-up period, the IOP was well controlled for both eyes without any medication. Shallow anterior chamber and complicated cataract developed in OS after CLASS, and there was no obvious late complication in OD after MAT.
