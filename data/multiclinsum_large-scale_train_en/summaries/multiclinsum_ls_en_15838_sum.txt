A 36-year-old Japanese woman presented with epigastric pain and anemia. Gastrointestinal endoscopy revealed a submucosal tumor in the gastric antrum. Because a definitive diagnosis could not be obtained and the tumor was located near the pylorus, we performed laparoscopic endoscopic cooperative surgery as diagnostic therapy. The postoperative course was favorable with no complications, such as delayed gastric emptying or outlet obstruction. The tumor was pathologically diagnosed as gastric plexiform fibromyxoma.
