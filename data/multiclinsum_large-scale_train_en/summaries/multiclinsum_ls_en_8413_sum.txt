We present an unusual case of a patient who presents with haemopericardium and haemodynamic instability after saphenous vein bypass grafting. A pseudoaneurysm rapidly expanded from 5 cm to 7 cm over the follow-up period producing compressive mass effect on the lateral right ventricle with transient haemodynamic instability. The patient's declining clinical condition did not warrant a redo thoracotomy and sternotomy, so an endovascular approach was deemed to be the best treatment option.
