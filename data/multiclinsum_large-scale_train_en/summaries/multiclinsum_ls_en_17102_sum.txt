A 15-year-old boy with GS was referred to our outpatient clinic due to severe underweight (BMI of 12.7 kg/m2) and food intake disorder with avoidant restrictive features. After a diagnosis of avoidant-restrictive food intake disorder (ARFID) was made, an inpatient multidisciplinary intervention and outpatient follow-up program were provided, which resulted in the improvement of the boy's weight and FED psychopathology.
