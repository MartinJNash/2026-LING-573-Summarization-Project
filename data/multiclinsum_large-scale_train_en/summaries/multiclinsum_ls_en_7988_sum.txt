A 52-year-old male with a history of severe acute biliary pancreatitis presented with cough, chest pain and fever. Physical exam showed absent respiratory sounds in the right base. Chest tomography revealed a right pleural collection. The patient underwent a right thoracotomy which confirmed a pancreaticopleural fistula, and then an ERCP was performed for conservative management and the patient was discharged. The patient was re-admitted on the 27th postoperative day where we confirmed a superinfected pancreatic necrosis and persistence of the fistula; the patient underwent a VARD for the treatment of both conditions. The patient was discharged after completing 5 weeks of antibiotic management.
