A 45-year-old man visited our hospital for treatment of left renal cell carcinoma. He had a history of antiphospholipid syndrome and took two antithrombotic agents. We performed a robot-assisted partial nephrectomy. We selectively ligated only the feeding branch during the procedure. Postoperatively, there were no complications, and the patient was discharged on postoperative day 10. One year after surgery, there was no worsening of antiphospholipid syndrome.
