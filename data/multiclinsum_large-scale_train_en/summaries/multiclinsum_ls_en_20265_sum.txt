A 67-year-old woman was referred to our hospital for the evaluation of acute abdominal pain after the conservative treatment of an NLC at another hospital. She had stable vital signs and no abdominal rigidity. We performed an elective laparoscopic deroofing following an examination of the cyst relative to the bile ducts and the patient's general condition. Computed tomography (CT) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) showed that there was no solid mass in the cyst. During the laparoscopic surgery, the cyst wall was resected and the back wall of the cyst was incinerated using an inverse-opal-structure electrode. The patient's postoperative course was stable without any complications.
