We present a bizarre case of a 2-year-old female child who presented to us with a tapestry needle embedded in the true pelvic cavity after a history of fall on the buttock while playing at home. The impaled needle was eventually successfully extracted surgically using the modified Stoppa's approach and the post-operative period was uneventful. We also briefly review the literature surrounding such unusual injuries and discuss tips and tricks regarding the surgical technique in such cases.
