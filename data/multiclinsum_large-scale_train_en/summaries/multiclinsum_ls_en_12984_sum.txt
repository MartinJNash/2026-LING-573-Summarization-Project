A 56-year-old Sri Lankan woman was discovered to have a large lytic lesion in her lower femur on routine X-ray following a fall. A history and physical examination, along with selective imaging and tissue sampling, were necessary to arrive at the diagnosis of schwannoma of the femur. The clinical presentation, radiology, pathology, and surgical management are discussed and contrasted with the other five cases documented in the literature. The tumor was successfully treated with evacuation through a lateral surgical approach and internal fixation. She remains well and disease-free 2.5 years later.
