A 65-year-old-man with a history of idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis status post-right single lung transplant was admitted for dizziness and resting tachycardia. Electrocardiogram showed a narrow complex, long R-P tachycardia with upright P-waves in lead V 1. An initial workup suggested an arrhythmia associated with the consumption of an herbal supplement containing heart-leaf sida, a banned botanical ephedrine alkaloid. After the supplement was discontinued, the patient's heart rate abruptly decreased without other intervention. Electrocardiogram showed a change in P-wave morphology in lead V 1 from upright to biphasic (+/-) after conversion to normal sinus rhythm. Thus, a diagnosis of atrial tachycardia originating at or near the donor right superior pulmonary vein was favoured.
