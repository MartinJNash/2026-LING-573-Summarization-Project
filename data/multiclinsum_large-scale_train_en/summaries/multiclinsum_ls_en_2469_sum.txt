We present the case of a 57-year-old woman who was admitted for intra-abdominal abscesses after a gastric bypass surgery and was found to have evidence of severe CS. Her 24-hour urinary free cortisol level was 898.6 μg/24 h. She was diagnosed with Cushing disease and underwent transsphenoidal resection of a pituitary adenoma, with an appropriate postoperative drop in the cortisol level. She received thromboprophylaxis during hospitalization; however, this was discontinued upon discharge, on postoperative day 9, because she was ambulating. Five days after hospital discharge and 14 days after her surgery, she developed left lower extremity edema and was found to have 4 deep venous thromboses.
