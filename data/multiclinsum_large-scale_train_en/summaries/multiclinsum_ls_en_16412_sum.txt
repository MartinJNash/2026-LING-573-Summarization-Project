We present the case of a 73-year-old man with VGPN misdiagnosed as trigeminal neuralgia. The patient was diagnosed with sick sinus syndrome, and a pacemaker was introduced. However, syncope still recurred. Magnetic resonance imaging revealed a branch of the right posterior inferior cerebellar artery contacting the root exit zone of the right glossopharyngeal and vagus nerves. We diagnosed VGPN due to neurovascular compression and performed microvascular decompression (MVD). The symptoms disappeared postoperatively.
