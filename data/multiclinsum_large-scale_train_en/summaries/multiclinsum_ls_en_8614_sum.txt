Here, we report a case of a 42-year-old man who presented with pain and swelling in the distal phalanx of his right ring finger following trauma. Plain radiographs showed a fracture through a probable enchondroma in the distal phalanx, and this report was corroborated by an MRI. However intraoperatively, the lesion was found to be a caseous lesion encased in a capsule and histological examination revealed the lesion to be an intraosseous epidermoid inclusion cyst.
