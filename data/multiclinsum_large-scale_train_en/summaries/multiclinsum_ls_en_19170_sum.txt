A 52-year-old woman was referred to our hospital with a submucosal tumor of the ascending colon. Clinical analysis by ultrasound colonoscopy and computed tomography revealed a partially ossified tumor with irregular edges continuous with the muscular layer. High F-18 deoxyglucose uptake was detected by positron emission tomography. Radical resection with lymph node dissection was performed, yielding a tumor specimen approximately 6.5 × 4.0 × 3.2 cm. Neoplastic spindle cell proliferation was found from submucosa to subserosa. Well-differentiated adipose tissue surrounded the tumor, but contained atypical nuclei with condensed chromosomes. Immunohistochemical staining was positive for p16, CDK4, and MDM2 expression. Based on these findings, a diagnosis of well-differentiated/dedifferentiated liposarcoma was given. Dedifferentiated liposarcomas are more aggressive than their well-differentiated, low-grade counterparts. While local recurrence can occur with both tumor types, dedifferentiated liposarcomas are more prone to developing distant metastases. The patient received no postoperative therapy, and no recurrences have been observed 12 months after surgery.
