This study reports a case of PLG without apparent immunodeficiency, suggesting the possibility of this disease occurrence during the treatment of pulmonary nodular lesions in children. Initially, the cause was assumed to be an atypical pathogen. Following conventional anti-infective treatment, chest computed tomography findings revealed that there were still multiple nodules in the lungs. Additionally, the patient was found to be infected with the Epstein-Barr virus. Histopathological examination of the resected lung revealed lymphoproliferative lesions with necrosis.
