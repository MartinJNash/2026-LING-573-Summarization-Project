An 81-year-old Asian female presented with clinical right-sided heart failure. Echocardiogram showed a large mass compressing and obliterating the right atrium. Trans-jugular tissue biopsy was performed. Initial pathology report was consistent with an angiosarcoma, based on an expression of Fli-1 (Friend leukaemia virus integration 1) from immunohistochemical staining. She died shortly after refusal to surgery. Autopsy was performed with diagnosis change to a diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) after tissue pathology.
