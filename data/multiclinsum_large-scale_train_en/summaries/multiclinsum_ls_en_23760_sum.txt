A 66-year-old woman was diagnosed with adenocarcinoma stage II and underwent a left lower lobectomy. The histopathological evaluation revealed stage IVA with pleural dissemination. The patient did not harbor an epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) mutation or anaplastic lymphoma kinase (ALK) rearrangement, and PD-L1 expression of the surgical specimen using 22C3 assay was 0%. Single brain metastasis was detected, and carboplatin and nab-paclitaxel were administered. After three cycles, asymptomatic multiple brain metastases were identified, and the patient was treated with nivolumab as second-line chemotherapy. Six months later, MRI revealed an intracranial complete response (CR). Nivolumab was discontinued after 23 cycles due to immune-related adverse events (irAEs) of grade 2 rash. However, its effects were sustained for 13 months after discontinuation. We were unable to evaluate the PD-L1 expression of brain metastases, which may show heterogeneity.
