We report the case of a 72-year-old patient, treated with insulin for 26 years after a diagnosis of type-1 diabetes (T1D), resulting in inadequate glycemia control and a severe evolution of cardiovascular complications. After an accurate evaluation of the clinical history, phenotype and laboratory data, including the determination of C-peptide serum levels, a diagnosis was made of T2D not T1D. Considering the patient's very high cardiovascular risk and dysmetabolic profile, insulin therapy was discontinued and more appropriate therapy with dulaglutide and metformin was instituted. These overall therapeutic modifications yielded remarkable clinical advantages in terms of the glycometabolic profile, weight reduction, abdominal circumference and body mass index decrease, as well as a better quality of life, with complete resolution of the dangerous hypoglycemic episodes.
