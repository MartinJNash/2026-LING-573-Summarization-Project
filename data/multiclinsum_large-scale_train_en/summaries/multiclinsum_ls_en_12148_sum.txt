We report the case of a 32-year-old female patient who presented with progressive abdominal distension of four months. On the abdominal CT scan, the left and middle hepatic veins were not visualized; the right hepatic vein and intrahepatic IVC had luminal narrowing; and there was caudate lobe enlargement suggestive of Budd-Chiari syndrome (BCS). Six months after the diagnosis of BCS, the patient developed other clinical features suggestive of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) and was finally diagnosed with SLE.
