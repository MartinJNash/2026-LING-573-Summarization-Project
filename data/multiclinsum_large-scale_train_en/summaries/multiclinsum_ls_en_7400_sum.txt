Current report present a case of pain at the tip of the middle finger of the left hand in a woman for six years and worsening in the last two years. The patient has visited several doctors with analgesic therapy, but the complaints have not improved. A bluish nail was found on physical examination, and a clinical study with the Love's pin test and the Hildreth test had positive results. Radiographic examination showed destruction with cortical thinning of the medial aspect of the distal phalanx of the left middle finger, and MRI showed a lesion with an erosion of the distal middle finger. In this case, complete surgical excision and biopsy were performed using a transungual surgical approach. The sample was sent for microscopic examination, showing a glomus tumor.
