We report a case of an 82-year-old female who underwent a transcatheter valve in ring procedure (Edwards Sapien S3, Edwards Lifesciences) for symptomatic severe mitral regurgitation from a bioprosthetic annuloplasty ring failure complicated by acute haemolytic anaemia a week after the procedure manifesting as dark coloured urine, profound icterus, and acute renal injury. She was treated with a post-dilation balloon valvuloplasty leading to reduction in haemolysis, but the patient was readmitted with acute haemolysis episode again. At this time, a decision was made to perform a repeat valve in valve TMVR with a 29 mm S3 Edwards Sapien valve which led to a resolution of haemolysis.
