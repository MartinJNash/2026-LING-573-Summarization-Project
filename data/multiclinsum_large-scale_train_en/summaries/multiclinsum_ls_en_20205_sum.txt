A 28-year-old Asian female presented with a 3-month history of nasal congestion culminating in epistaxis. Physical examination was notable for a tissue mass obstructing nasal cavity and the sphenoid sinus. Computed tomography (CT) and magnetic resonance (MR) imaging revealed a lesion primarily involving the upper nasal cavity extending intracranially through the cribriform plates into the anterior cranial fossa. Histologic and immunohistochemical analysis of the specimen obtained through a transnasal biopsy revealed an ALK(+) ALCL. The patient underwent two cycles of chemotherapy and focal radiation therapy, achieving minimal residual disease. The patient remained neurologically unchanged with stable minimal residual disease at the 1-year follow-up.
