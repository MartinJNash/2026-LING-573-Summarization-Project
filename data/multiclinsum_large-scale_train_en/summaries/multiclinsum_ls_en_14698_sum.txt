We present the case of an 83-year-old Thai woman with no history of exposure to tuberculosis. She presented with swelling and mild pain at her right wrist and the fifth finger of her right hand for 3 months. Ultrasonography revealed tenosynovitis in the right hand and wrist. Mycobacterium tuberculosis was confirmed with tissue diagnosis after an open biopsy. 2-months regimens containing Isoniazid, Rifampicin, Pyrazinamide and Ethambutol/6-months of isoniazid and rifampicin treatment was successful without complications. We follower her up for 1 year, at which time she had returned to do normal daily activities. Her final DASH score was 10.8.
