A 24 years old male presented at SIUT with the complaint of left flank pain on and off for one month. CT scan showed soft tissue density mass in left renal pelvis extending from mid-pole calyces to pelviuretric junction, leading to obstruction and ultimately mild uropathy. We found a partially obstructing staghorn calculus with asymmetrical cortical thinning. Left Robot-Assisted Nephro-ureterectomy plus excision of bladder cuff was planned in which 3 × 4 cm mass involving the left renal pelvis was excised. To date, there is no radiologic evidence of disease recurrence.
