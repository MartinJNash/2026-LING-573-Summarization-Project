A 71-year-old Japanese patient was diagnosed with prostate cancer, and active surveillance was selected. After 1 year, prostate biopsy was performed by a transperitoneal approach, and 16 biopsy cores were taken. One biopsy core contained skin tissue showing extramammary Paget's disease. Careful skin examination confirmed the presence of an extramammary Paget's disease lesion in the left perineum, and curative surgical resection was performed. Recurrence and metastasis did not occur after 6 months of follow-up.
