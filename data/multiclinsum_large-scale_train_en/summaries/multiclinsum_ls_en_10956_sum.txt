A 44-year-old female patient without a specific medical history presented with intermittent dyspnea that started 1 month prior to presentation. A heterogeneous mass was observed in the left atrium on echocardiography and a large mass was observed in the left atrium on computed tomography. Surgery was performed under the suspicion of atypical cardiac myxoma, and the tumor was successfully removed. However, postoperative histopathological examination revealed cardiac osteosarcoma. The patient underwent chemotherapy and has been well maintained without recurrence for 10 years.
