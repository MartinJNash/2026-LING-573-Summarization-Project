A 14-year-old girl presented with pain and swelling over the dorsum of the right hand for 2 months which was progressively increasing in size. The range of movements of the metacarpophalangeal (MCP) joint was normal. Radiological evaluation showed a lytic lesion with a well-defined margin over the metaphyseal region of the second metacarpal without articular involvement. The lesion was diagnosed as GCT on biopsy. Reconstruction of bone loss was managed by hemi-fibular grafting technique which involves selective osteotomy of the anterior half of the middle third of the fibula for the reconstruction of bone loss. This new technique ensures a renewable source of autograft with good incorporation at the recipient site with good hand function despite maintaining the esthetic appearance of the hand. Lesion being very aggressive had two episodes of recurrence at 2-3 years of post-operative period which was excised.
