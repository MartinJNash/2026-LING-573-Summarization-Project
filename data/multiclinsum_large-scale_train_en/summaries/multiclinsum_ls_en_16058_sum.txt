A 5-year-old girl complaining of vomiting, fever and abdominal pain came to hospital. In Laboratory findings, leukocytosis (WBC = 22.5 ∗ 103/μl) was observed and mild interloop fluid with a whirlpool-like structure which suggests volvulus like obstruction was seen in sonography. The patient underwent emergency laparotomy. A necrotic congested Meckel's diverticulum was found during the surgery, which was axially twisted. After the operation, the patient recovered and was discharged six days later.
