A 45-year-old man was injured because his ring finger was kicked during a Futsal game. Radiographs and computed tomography revealed a comminuted intraarticular fracture of the proximal phalanx of this ring finger. We treated the fracture with open reduction and K-wires and external fixation. We removed the K-wire and external fixator 5 weeks postoperatively and initiated range of motion exercises. Five months postoperatively, his finger motion was fully recovered without restriction.
