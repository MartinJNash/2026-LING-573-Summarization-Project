We present a case of a woman with lung metastatic melanoma who developed pericarditis after two infusions of pembrolizumab. The initial response to steroids and colchicine was favourable, and steroids were successfully tapered, after which the immunotherapy was reintroduced. A complete metabolic remission was achieved after six cycles of pembrolizumab, but pericarditis symptoms recur each time the steroid dose is lowered below 10 mg. After introduction of azathioprine, steroids were successfully tapered over the course of 6 months.
