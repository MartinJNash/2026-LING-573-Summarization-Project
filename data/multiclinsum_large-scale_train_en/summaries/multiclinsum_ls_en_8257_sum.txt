The patient was a 69-year-old male who was referred to our hospital for examination of a right chest tumor and related pain. Contrast-enhanced computed tomography and tumor biopsy were performed, leading to a diagnosis of collecting duct carcinoma. A combination of pembrolizumab plus axitinib was initiated as first-line therapy; right chest pain decreased, and tumor shrinkage was observed. Seven months after treatment initiation, tumor progression was noted. Cabozantinib was initiated as second-line therapy; however, was discontinued due to patient fatigue. The patient died 15 months after the initiation of treatment.
