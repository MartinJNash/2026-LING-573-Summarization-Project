A 47-year-old male patient with XLHR attended the outpatient clinic with severe left hip pain. X-rays revealed a left proximal femoral varus deformity and a femoral neck stress fracture. After 1 month without improvement of pain, and no radiographic sign of healing, correction of the proximal femoral varus deformity and fixation of the cervical neck fracture was achieved by a cephalomedullary nail. At 8 months follow-up, hip pain relief was achieved with radiographic healing of the femoral neck stress fracture and the proximal femoral osteotomy.
