A 54-year-old woman with a complex medical history including cerebral palsy, Crohn's disease, and transfusion-dependent iron-deficiency anemia experienced a pulmonary embolism. Due to failed anticoagulation therapy resulting in gastrointestinal bleeding and high transfusion requirements, she underwent placement of an IVCF as a preventive measure against recurrent pulmonary embolism. Three years later, the patient presented with lower extremity swelling and a sudden decline in hemoglobin levels. Diagnostic imaging revealed adherent nonocclusive thrombus within and above the indwelling IVCF. Utilizing the novel Protrieve sheath with the self-expandable Nitinol funnel, successful endovascular removal of the embedded IVCF and adherent thrombus was performed, while mitigating the risk of intraprocedural pulmonary embolism.
