A 78-year-old Hispanic female with a significant past medical history of type 2 diabetes mellitus was admitted with altered mental status, severe hypernatremia and hyperglycemia. She was diagnosed with hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state, and fluid therapy was started. A continuous electroencephalogram revealed left frontocentral and temporal periodic lateralized epileptiform discharges that resolved as her hypernatremia and dehydration were treated. She survived and was discharged after 1 week of treatment.
