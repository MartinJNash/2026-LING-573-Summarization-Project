A 59-year-old man with history of migraine with aura and multiple cryptogenic strokes was referred for percutaneous closure of a PFO with associated ASA. He had been previously submitted to repeat attempt of percutaneous closure with not self-centering and self-centering devices that failed due to unfavorable anatomic characteristics (dextrocardia with situs viscerum inversus, huge ASA, multiple fenestrations, large PFO). Based on this "complex" anatomy, a sequential 2-step interventional approach aiming to reduce size and mobility of the atrial septal aneurysm with a suture-based approach (Noblestich™ EL, HeartStitch, Fountain Valley, CA, USA) and to close any eventual accessory fenestrations with a not self-centering occluding device was planned. At the end of the procedure, the ASA completely disappeared and no residual shunt was imaged at TEE bubble test.
