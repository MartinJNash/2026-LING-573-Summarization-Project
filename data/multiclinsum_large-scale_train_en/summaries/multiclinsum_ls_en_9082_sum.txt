This is a rare case report of ligneous conjunctivitis in a 67-year-old male patient who presented a recurrent conjunctival granuloma after five excisions following cataract surgery in his left eye. We performed a tumor excision with free conjunctival autograft. The histology showed a fibrin crust including macrophages, granulocytes, lymphocytes, and reactively altered squamous cell nests. These findings were consistent with a ″pseudomembrane in conjunctivitis lignosa″. We administered a topical combination of plasminactivator, heparin, cortisone and cyclosporine.
