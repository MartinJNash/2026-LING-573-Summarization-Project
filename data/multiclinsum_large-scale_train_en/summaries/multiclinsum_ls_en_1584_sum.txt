We report the case of an 11-year-old male with a sessile osteochondroma of the tibial tubercle and concomitant involvement of the distal extension and attachment of the patellar tendon into the tibial periosteum. A prior diagnosis OSD had been made. The lesion was resected and repair of the extensor mechanism was required at the time of surgery. The patient was followed for 20 months postoperatively and had restoration of knee function with minimal pain, as demonstrated by a PEDI-IKDC score of 94.6 at 19-month.
