A 68-year-old Chinese female was diagnosed with stage IV right lung adenocarcinoma (cT3N3M1c). The targeted next-generation sequencing (NGS) of 14 cancer-related genes identified an IGR (chr2: 30,316,870)-ALK fusion. Her lung lesions have been successfully converted from a partial response to a complete response after administrating crizotinib for 1 year combined with 6 cycles of chemotherapy with pemetrexed. So far, her progression-free-survival has reached 21 months.
