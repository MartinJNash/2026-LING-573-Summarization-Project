A 33-year-old female presented with loss of vision in her right eye and features suggesting optic neuritis. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) of the brain and orbits revealed a cystic lesion with peripheral rim enhancement in the optic nerve substance at the orbital apex. An enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay test for cysticercosis further established the diagnosis as optic nerve cysticercosis. She was treated with oral albendazole and steroids, resulting in remarkable improvement in visual acuity and resolution of the lesion.
