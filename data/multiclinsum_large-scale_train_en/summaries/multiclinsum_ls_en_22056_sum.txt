We present the case of a 14-year-old Afghan boy with severe abdominal pain who was initially diagnosed with bilateral undescended testicles and abdominal complex cysts. He was supposed to undergo a cystectomy and orchiopexy surgery. During the surgical intervention, an unexpected finding was made whereby fibrotic-like ovaries, fallopian tubes, and a segment of the uterus were identified, ultimately leading to the diagnosis of PMDS. The MD was carefully removed, and the testicles were delicately repositioned during an orchiopexy procedure.
