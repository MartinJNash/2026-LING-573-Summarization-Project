A 64-year-old male was scheduled for gastrojejunostomy because of gastrointestinal obstruction. He fasted from the night before surgery. General anesthesia was induced, and cricoid pressure was applied during intubation. However, he vomited huge amount of gastric contents. The scheduled surgery was performed without surgical complications, and postoperatively respiratory management, including mechanical ventilation with prone positioning, was performed in high care unit. He was extubated on postoperative day 2. He was discharged from the hospital on POD 25.
