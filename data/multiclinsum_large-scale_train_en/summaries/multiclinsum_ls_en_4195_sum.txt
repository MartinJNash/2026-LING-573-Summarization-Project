A 33-year-old Iranian man with the fatigue, jaundice, elevated liver enzymes and alkaline phosphatase, and hypereosinophilia was referred to our hospital. Viral and autoimmune hepatitis were excluded, and secondary workups for hypereosinophilia were all negative. Magnetic resonance cholangiopancreatography showed beaded appearance of intra- and extrahepatic biliary tree, and liver biopsy revealed interface hepatitis. Therefore, the diagnosis of autoimmune hepatitis-primary sclerosing cholangitis overlap syndrome was made, and prednisolone, azathioprine, and ursodeoxycholic acid was initiated. His jaundice and peripheral blood eosinophilia resolved after 2 weeks, and he became completely asymptomatic.
