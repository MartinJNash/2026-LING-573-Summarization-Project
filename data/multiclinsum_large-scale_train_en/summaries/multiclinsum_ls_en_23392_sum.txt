A 49-year-old female with bilateral knee pain and intermittent swelling for over 20 years presented to our clinic with complaints of bilateral knee pain and swelling. She had previous steroid injection but no relief. After MRI was obtained concerning for LA, a surgical discussion was had with the patient about arthroscopic removal. She elected to proceed with surgery and underwent arthroscopic debridement of both knees. At her follow-up at 6 months for the right knee and 2 months for the left knee, she had a significant improvement in pain and quality of life.
