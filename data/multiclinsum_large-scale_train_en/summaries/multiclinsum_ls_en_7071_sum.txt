Case report of a 37-year-old female patient presented with diffuse abdominal pain, with any associated symptoms. Contrast computed tomography was performed; a retroperitoneal mass was found, contacting the third portion of the duodenum. Intraoperative hypertensive crisis was documented with the manipulation of the mass. Octreotide infusion was administered with the normalization of the clinical condition. Patients do not present any postoperative morbidity after 90 days. Pathology reports chromaffin cells concluding pheochromocytoma.
