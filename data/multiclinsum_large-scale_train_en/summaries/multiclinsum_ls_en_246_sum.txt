A 19-year-old male presented with diffuse abdominal pain, watery non-bloody diarrhoea, nausea, vomiting and intermittent low-grade fever that lasted for more than 3 weeks. Multiple stool cultures on enriched and selective media gave negative results. Microscopic examination of wet mounts of stool prepared from formalin/ethyl acetate concentrates, together with permanent staining helped in making a definitive diagnosis and ruling out other coccidian parasites. Diagnosis of the parasite as S. hominis was made based on the size and morphology of the individual sporocysts that were observed in the wet-mount preparations. This severe case of intestinal sarcocystosis in a healthy adult after eating undercooked beef shawarma meat is described.
