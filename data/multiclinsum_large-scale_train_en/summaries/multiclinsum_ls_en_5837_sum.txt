A 37-year-old male patient reported to the outpatient department with bilateral upper limb weakness and gait impairment. The individual presented as a documented case of previous Hodgkin's lymphoma, with magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) revealing the presence of anterior epidural soft tissue mass at the C6-D1 vertebral levels. This pathological condition resulted in the compression and posterior displacement of the spinal cord. The patient was scheduled to have an emergency decompression surgery involving a C7 corpectomy and C6-D1 fusion procedure utilizing a mesh cage and plate.
