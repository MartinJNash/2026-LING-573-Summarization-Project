A 34-year-old white man with type 1 diabetes mellitus presented to our hospital with severe, rapidly progressive bilateral neovascular glaucoma following coronary artery bypass graft surgery. The patient was previously treated for proliferative diabetic retinopathy with complete peripheral pan-retinal photocoagulation. Prompt and adequate treatment with antiglaucoma medication and cyclodiode laser did not halt the progression of the disease. The patient ended up with no perception of light within a few weeks of initial presentation.
