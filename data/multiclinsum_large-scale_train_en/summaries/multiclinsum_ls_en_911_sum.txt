A 26-year-old patient with a novel autosomal-recessively inherited c.1 A > G variant in BEST1 diagnosed as ARB and ACG, presented as widespread vitelliform deposits in the posterior pole, retinoschisis in the macula, vitreoretinal interface abnormalities, shallow anterior chamber depth (ACD) and angle closure with uncontrolled intraocular pressure (IOP). Combined phacoemulsification, intraocular lens implantation and goniosynechialysis did not improve any clinical presentation. However, low dose transscleral cyclophotocoagulation with subsequent vitreous liquefaction effectively lowered IOP, deepened ACD, and rehabilitated retinoschisis in both eyes.
