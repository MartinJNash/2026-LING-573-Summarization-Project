A 33-year-old HIV-positive woman with dysmenorrhea for two years was diagnosed with uterine fibroids and scheduled for a myomectomy. After spinal anesthesia, the patient developed nonresponsive myoclonic seizures, so she was sedated, intubated, and hooked up to a mechanical ventilator. However, her condition continued to deteriorate, and she developed narrow complex tachycardia, which was controlled but later developed systole and she died. A second check for anesthetic drugs revealed that she was given tranexamic acid rather than bupivacaine.
