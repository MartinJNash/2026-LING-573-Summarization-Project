A 70-year-old man participated in ten 20 min sessions of HAL intervention, twice weekly for five weeks. The effects of each HAL session were evaluated based on changes in performance on the 10 m walk test (10 MWT), lower limb kinematics quantified from motion capture, and the activation ratio of the gastrocnemius, measured before and after the intervention. Muscle activity was recorded using surface electromyography and synchronized to measured kinematics. The HAL intervention improved gait speed and step length, with an increase in the hip flexion angle during the swing phase and a decrease in the activation ratio of the gastrocnemius. The modified Ashworth scale improved from 1+ to 1 and International Standards for Neurological and Functional Classification of Spinal Cord Injury motor scores from 34 to 49.
