A 74-year-old female presented with incidental findings of elevated TSH levels and normal thyroid markers. Ultrasound was performed and revealed multiple bilateral thyroid nodules measuring up to 1.9 cm. Fine needle aspiration was performed, and cytology showed one Bethesda category 5 nodule. Total thyroidectomy with neck dissection was performed, and the pathology showed follicular adenoma with bizarre nuclei. Based on the results of immunohistochemistry, the neoplastic cells exhibited staining for wild-type p53 and low levels of the proliferation index Ki-67.
