A 70-year-old man presented to our hospital with stent thrombosis due to non-absorption of antiplatelet agents, 3 days after an elective percutaneous intervention to the right coronary artery. The patient, who had had a laparoscopic high anterior resection due to previous colorectal cancer, had noticed tablets passing whole into his colostomy bag. Repeat balloon angioplasty and stenting were performed and the patient received further antiplatelet therapy in a crushed form.
