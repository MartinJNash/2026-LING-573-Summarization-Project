We present the case of a 55-year-old woman who underwent bilateral staged TKR using oxidized zirconium implants and subsequently developed eczematous skin reactions. In both instances, she presented with a peri-incisional erythematous blistering skin reaction that was successfully treated with topical corticosteroids. Investigations revealed no evidence of infection or allergic-typeallergic type reactions to the metals contained in the knee replacements.
