We describe a case of ETV6-MECOM fusion-positive refractory AML, where molecular analysis and in vitro high throughput drug screening identified a tolerable, novel targeted therapy and provided rationale for avoiding what could have been a toxic treatment regimen. Ruxolitinib combined with hydroxyurea led to disease control and enhanced quality-of-life in a patient unsuitable for intensified chemotherapy or allogeneic stem cell transplantation.
