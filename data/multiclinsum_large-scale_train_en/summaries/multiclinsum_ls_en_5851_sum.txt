This case report presents a 69-year-old female patient with a keloid treated using a spring-powered needle-free injector (NFI) for drug delivery. The keloid was assessed using the Vancouver Scar Scale (VSS) and the Patient and Observer Scar Assessment Scale (POSAS). The patient's pain level was measured using the Numeric Pain Rating Scale (NPRS). TA and 5-FU mixed with lidocaine were loaded into the NFI and injected at a dose of 0.1 mL/cm2. The treatment was repeated twice a week. After four sessions, the keloid flattened by 0.5 cm, VSS score decreased from 11 to 10, and POSAS scores decreased from 49 to 43 (observer) and from 50 to 37 (patient). The NPRS during each procedure was 1, indicating minimal pain.
