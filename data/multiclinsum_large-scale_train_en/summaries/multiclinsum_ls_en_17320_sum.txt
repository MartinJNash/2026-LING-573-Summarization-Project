A 51-year-old man with a middle-third clavicle fracture due to a direct trauma after falling down the stairs. A brachial plexus injury experienced symptoms, including numbness occurred in the affected upper extremity, at 1 day after the injury. The patient's motor power in the elbow, wrist, and hand decreased at 3 days after the injury. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) showed no loss of continuity in the brachial plexus, but showed nerve compression by displaced fracture fragments. Electromyography revealed brachial plexopathy. Conservative treatment, including a shoulder sling, was performed with satisfactory outcomes; the patient reported a 70% improvement at 6 months after the injury.
