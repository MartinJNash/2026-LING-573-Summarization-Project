A 35-year-old woman presented with a rapidly growing painful mass 3 months following incomplete removal of a MPNST from her forearm. Staging investigations showed no evidence of metastasis. The patient underwent en-bloc surgical excision, split skin grafting, and adjuvant radiation therapy. Histology and immunohistochemical analysis confirmed a MPNST. Five years after having surgery, the patient shows no evidence of recurrence and has excellent function.
