We present the case of an 83-year-old woman with symptomatic severe AS, mildly decreased left ventricular ejection fraction and history of coronary artery bypass grafting with right internal mammary artery (RIMA) to the right coronary artery (RCA) and left internal mammary artery to the left anterior descending artery and further percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) to the circumflex. First, we performed a transcatheter aortic valve implantation (TAVI) to treat the severe AS. Because of persistent symptoms despite good result, we then performed RA of the native RCA through the RIMA with a Guidezilla® guide extension catheter.
