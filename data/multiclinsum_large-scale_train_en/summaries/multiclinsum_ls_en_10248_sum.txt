A 75 year old female remained unresponsive after a hemi arthroplasty was performed because of a 1 day old femoral neck fracture. She rapidly developed respiratory insufficiency and an obstructive shock with right ventricle dilatation on transthoracic echocardiography. The diffusion-weighted MRI brain images showed the "Starfield" pattern, a radiologic phenomenon typical for FES. During 3 weeks of ICU admission the neurologic state slowly ameliorated.
