A 20-year-old male patient presented with a history of pain in the left groin for 5 months. Radiological examination showed a lytic lesion in the head of femur. The patient was managed by safe surgical dislocation of the hip, curettage with autogenous iliac crest bone graft with countersunk screw fixation. Histopathology confirmed the lesion to be enchondroma. At the latest follow-up after 6 months, the patient was symptom-free and there was no evidence of any recurrence.
