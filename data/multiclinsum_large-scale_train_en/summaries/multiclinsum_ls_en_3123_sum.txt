A 77-year-old patient with rheumatoid arthritis had taken low-dose methotrexate for 13 years. The patient developed left renal mass 3 cm in size and multiple pulmonary nodules. Initially, renal malignant tumor with lung metastases was considered and the renal biopsy was planned. However, under possible diagnosis of methotrexate-related lymphoproliferative disorder, we withdrew methotrexate treatment at first and then observed spontaneous regression of the tumorous lesions of the kidney and lungs.
