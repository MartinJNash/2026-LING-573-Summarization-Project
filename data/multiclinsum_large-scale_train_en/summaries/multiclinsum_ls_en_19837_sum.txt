A 51-year-old man complaining of epigastric pain was referred to our hospital. Computed tomography (CT) at admission revealed massive PVG and extensive PI, but no PVT. Emergency laparotomy was carried out, but bowel resection was unnecessary. On follow-up CT on postoperative day 5, thrombosis was noted in the portal venous system, and anticoagulant was started immediately. This patient was discharged and continued to take the anticoagulant. Seven months after discharge, PVT had disappeared on CT without any thromboembolic complications.
