A 53-year-old male, with a history of smoking and regular sildenafil use, was brought to the emergency department due to loss of consciousness with right-side weakness, he initially experienced with nausea, vomiting and dizziness after taking sildenafil 100mg tablet once. The Glasgow Coma Score (GCS) was 10 with side hemiparesis. Non-contrast CT revealed left thalamic acute hemorrhage with ventricular extension. Furthermore, a head CT angiography ruled out any vascular anomalies after that the patient was admitted to the intensive care unit (ICU) for conservative management. After three days on clinical and neurological improvement, the patient was transferred to the inpatient ward for further management, monitoring and physiotherapy. On day 6, the patient was discharged and planned for flow up.
