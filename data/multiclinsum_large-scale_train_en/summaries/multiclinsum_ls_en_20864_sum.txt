The case was a 70-year-old man who visited his local doctor with right hypochondrial pain, which was identified by computed tomography (CT) as a giant mass. Upper endoscopy revealed a 30-mm-sized 0-IIc lesion in the greater curvature of the angular incisure and a 15-mm-sized 0-IIa lesion in the anterior wall of the lower body of the gastric body.
