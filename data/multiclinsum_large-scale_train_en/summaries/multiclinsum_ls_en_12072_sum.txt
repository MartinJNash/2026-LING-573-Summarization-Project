A 51-year-old female presented in the one-stop breast clinic with 6-month history of having a static and painless left breast lump. Mass was firm, non-tender and 2 cm in size. It was not adherent to skin or muscle and it was present in the upper outer quadrant of the left breast. Mammo-sonography revealed a circumscribed mass of 17 mm in the outer quadrant of the left breast. There were enlarged ipsilateral lymph nodes. Core biopsy suggested atypical lymphoid infiltrates. She underwent wide local excision of breast and axillary nodal mass. The definitive histological diagnosis revealed non-Hodgkin's follicular lymphoma grade 2/3. Staging computed tomography scan features were suggestive of cervical lymphadenopathy. Hence, staging workup proved this to be a case of secondary BL.
