We describe a case of a 50-year-old man with history of metastatic thymoma on immunosuppression (gemcitabine and capecitabine) who presented with progressive dyspnoea. Echocardiography and computed tomography (CT) of chest showed filling defect in the pulmonary artery. The initial differential diagnosis was of pulmonary embolism and metastatic disease. The mass was subsequently excised, which revealed a diagnosis of Aspergillus endocarditis of the pulmonary valve. Unfortunately, he passed away despite medical treatment with antifungal therapy after surgery.
