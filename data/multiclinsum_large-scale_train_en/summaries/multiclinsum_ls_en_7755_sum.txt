A 74-year-old man was referred to our hospital with a chief complaint of chest pain and was admitted. The patient was found to have had a myocardial infarction with ST-segment elevation and underwent an emergent percutaneous coronary intervention. Owing to a rapidly increased blood glucose level, computed tomography was performed and showed autoimmune pancreatitis. An elevated serum level of IgG4 led to a diagnosis of IgG4-RD involving acute coronary syndrome (ACS).
