A 34-year-old adult was previously healthy and had no medical history of any significance. The patient had suffered only facial trauma 8 months before admission. One month prior to admission, he showed rapidly progressing changes in his behavior and higher mental functions. He was admitted to the emergency room with an occipital headache with 2 months of history. By the time he arrived, he suffered from total disability and was prostrate. He was diagnosed with meningeal and demential syndrome in the process of being studied. After starting the diagnostic approach by investigating cerebrospinal fluid, a magnetic resonance of the skull, an electroencephalogram, a brain biopsy was indicated. The histopathological study reported the presence of the hyphae characteristics of Aspergillus. The patient died 7 days after the diagnosis.
