An adolescent female, who is an academic high achiever, living with parents, presented with unresponsive spells which were initially treated with antiepileptics by the paediatrician without any significant improvement. Later, after further assessments and revision of her diagnosis to conversion disorder, she was referred to the child and youth mental health service team. Further evaluation revealed her symptoms to be a result of multiple psychosocial stressors in the context of her having undiagnosed ADHD. Individual therapy, treatment with stimulant, resulted in significant improvement in her school and home adjustments.
