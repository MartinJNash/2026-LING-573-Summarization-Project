We present a rare case of a Meckel's diverticulum causing small bowel obstruction. A 50-year old male presented to the emergency department (ED) with vomiting, abdominal pain and distension. Computed tomography (CT) of the abdomen showed dilated small bowel loops consistent with a small bowel obstruction. The patient was taken to the operating theatre for a laparotomy and was found to have a transition point from a mesodiverticular adhesion causing upstream dilatation which was released. The patient recovered with no postoperative complications and was discharged home.
