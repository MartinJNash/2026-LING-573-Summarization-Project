An 83-year-old female complaining of chest pain was referred to our hospital for severe aortic stenosis. The multi-slice computed tomography showed a congenital single coronary artery originating from the right sinus of Valsalva. The left coronary artery branched off of the right coronary artery, and passed between the aorta and main pulmonary artery. The heart team of the hospital decided to perform TAVI via femoral artery with a balloon-expandable prosthesis, with coronary angioplasty devices on standby in case of coronary occlusion. The TAVI procedure was performed successfully without coronary occlusion.
