The patient presented with a rapid onset of localized cervical swelling, accompanied by monoclonal B-cell lymphocytosis displaying a CLL immunophenotype. The histopathological analysis identified a Burkitt lymphoma (BL) located in the submandibular gland and adjacent lymph node. The patient's bone marrow displayed a minor infiltration of monoclonal B-cells with a CLL immunophenotype (< 10%). Molecular analysis demonstrated the presence of the same monoclonal rearrangement in the framework region (FR3 region) of the immunoglobulin heavy chain (IGH) locus. High-throughput sequencing of the immunoglobulin heavy and light chains also confirmed the presence of the same rearrangement in SLL/CLL and in the Burkitt lymphoma sample, but also highlighted the presence of a second rearrangement in the Burkitt lymphoma cells, not shared with the SLL/CLL cells in the bone marrow. The patient was treated with DA-EPOCH-R, which lead to a complete metabolic response.
