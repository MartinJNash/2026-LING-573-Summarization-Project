The patient was a 34-year-old pregnant woman who was diagnosed with acute heart failure associated with infective endocarditis. We performed cesarean section under general anesthesia using remimazolam, with percutaneous cardiopulmonary support on standby. The mother's mean blood pressure was maintained above 65 mmHg during the surgery, without catecholamines or vasopressors. The infant's Apgar scores were 4 at 1 min and 7 at 5 min.
