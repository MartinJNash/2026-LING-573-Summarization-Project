A 59-year-old Chinese man was admitted because of an incidental finding of a right atrial mass on transthoracic echocardiography during a routine check-up. The mass was initially diagnosed as a myxoma, and the patient was scheduled for minimally invasive excision via right anterolateral thoracotomy. An additional mass on the non-coronary cusp of the aortic valve was identified using intraoperative transesophageal echocardiography. The patient still underwent complete tumor excision via right anterolateral thoracotomy. Both neoplasms were pathologically diagnosed as CPFs.
