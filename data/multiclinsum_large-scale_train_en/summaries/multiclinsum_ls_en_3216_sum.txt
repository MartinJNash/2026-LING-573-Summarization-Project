This is a case report of a 50-year-old male that presented with right septic hip arthritis in the acute setting. He complained of pain with attempted range of motion and log-roll. Laboratory workup demonstrated no leukocytosis, but elevated inflammatory markers. A hip aspiration under fluoroscopy was performed, revealing a cell count of 100,600/cm3 with 91% neutrophils. His right hip underwent irrigation and debridement with a muscle-sparing anterolateral approach (Rottinger).
