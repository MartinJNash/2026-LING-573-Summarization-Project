A 48-year-old G3/P2 woman presented to the Department of Gynecology with a physical examination. Ultrasound examination and CT revealed pelvic masses. The woman underwent surgical treatment because of the pelvic masses and underwent a hysterectomy for a recurrent mucinous borderline ovarian tumor. However, the results of the postoperative pathological examination showed diffuse large B-cell lymphoma of the endometrium. After four courses of chemotherapy, the woman was in good condition. The clinical manifestations were nonspecific, which made a timely diagnosis complex.
