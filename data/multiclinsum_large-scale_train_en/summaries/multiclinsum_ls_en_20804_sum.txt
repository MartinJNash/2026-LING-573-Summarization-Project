A 16-year-old female presented at the emergency room with a right-sided lip droop and subsequently developed symptoms consistent with a malignant hemispheric infarction, including altered consciousness, hemiplegia, and forced gaze deviation. Her laboratory results were within the normal range. However, a brain MRI revealed cerebral edema and a massive infarction in the middle cerebral artery (MCA) region. Subsequently, her serologic profile was indicative of SLE, leading to a later diagnosis.
