A newborn male is born with omphalocele-exstrophy-imperforate anus-spinal defects (OEIS) complex. As primary surgical reconstruction was pursued, the patient was found to have a non-inflamed duplicated appendix, which was left unremoved. In the following months, the patient experienced episodes of small bowel obstruction, eventually requiring surgical intervention. During this operation, the duplicated appendix was noted to be inflamed, prompting removal of both appendices.
