We report a patient who presented with worsening axial and bilateral upper extremity pain. He was found to have a locally aggressive tumor involving the vertebral body of T2. After a repeat magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and a computed tomography (CT)-guided biopsy, the diagnosis of a solitary plasmacytoma was confirmed. It destroyed over 90% of the T2 vertebral body, resulted in 22° of local kyphosis, and caused spinal cord compression. The tumor was treated with a T2 vertebrectomy, posterior arthrodesis from C5-T4, and anterior arthrodesis from T1-3.
