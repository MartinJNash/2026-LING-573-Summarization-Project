We report the case of a 50-year-old man, without past medical history, complaining of abdominal pain and constipation for 3 months. Biological tests were normal. Radiological investigations and endoscopy revealed a sessile polyp in the right colonic angle measuring 4 cm in greatest diameter. Biopsy concluded to a tubular adenoma with low-grade dysplasia. The patient underwent right hemicolectomy. Microscopically, an invasive adenocarcinoma was identified occupying the colonic mucosal with an invasion of the submucosa. The tumor showed a tubulovillous pattern on the surface and was made mostly of jagged crowded glands in the depth. Some areas exhibit Paneth cell differentiation. No metastatic lymph node was found, and the tumor was staged T1N0. The postoperative course was uneventful. The patient remained free of symptoms at the 6-month follow-up and had no evidence of recurrence.
