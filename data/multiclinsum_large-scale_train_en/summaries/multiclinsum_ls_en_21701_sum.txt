An immunocompetent and otherwise healthy 14-year old Indian male who was operated 1 year ago for chronic osteomyelitis presented to us with an acute tense swelling, unbearable pain, and toxemia, with a cement spacer and multiple cement beads in situ. With regards to his initial injury, he had history of fall from height with a puncture wound, possible humerus fracture, treatment by local healers as well as surgical procedures in an outside facility with debridement and placement of cements spacer and beads. On presentation to our facility, removal of retained cement plus debridement was carried out, and after serial surgeries, the defect was successfully treated with the use of antibiotic loaded absorbable calcium sulfate.
