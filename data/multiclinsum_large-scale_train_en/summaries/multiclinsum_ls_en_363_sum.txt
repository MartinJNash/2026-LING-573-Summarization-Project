The patient is a 54-year-old female who presented with a 2-year history of progressive headaches, cognitive decline, and papilledema. A cerebral angiogram demonstrated a complex dAVF to the vein of Galen (VoG). She underwent transarterial embolization with Onyx-18 which resulted in minimal reduction in arterial venous shunting. She subsequently underwent a successful transvenous coil embolization resulting in complete occlusion of dAVF. The patient's postoperative course was complicated by interventricular hemorrhage; however, she had a remarkable clinical recovery with resolution of headaches and improvement in cognitive function. A follow-up angiogram completed 6  months post-embolization demonstrated very mild residual shunting.
