A 22-year-old Iranian woman presented with symptoms of ataxia, dysphasia, paresthesia, and acute numbness of her left upper limb four weeks after the AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccination. Laboratory data suggested hemolytic anemia and thrombocytopenia. Also, schistocytes were noted on her peripheral blood smear. Acquired thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura (aTTP) was diagnosed in accordance with clinical manifestations along with initial blood test results and was confirmed later through findings of ADAMTS-13 low level activity and the ADAMTS-13 positive inhibitor. She underwent 22 sessions of plasma exchange, receiving corticosteroid and rituximab. Finally, the treatment was successful.
