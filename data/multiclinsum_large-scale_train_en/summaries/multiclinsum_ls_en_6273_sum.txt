A 60-year-old male with a chronic pretibial wound with underlying osteomyelitis of the right leg presented himself at the emergency department and was admitted to the surgical ward. He had complex chronic osteomyelitis of a tibial non-union after an earlier right crural fracture (a previous work-related accident). He was treated with an ALT flap and double-barrelled vascularized fibula graft, which was complicated with an additional fracture and breakage of osteosynthesis material.
