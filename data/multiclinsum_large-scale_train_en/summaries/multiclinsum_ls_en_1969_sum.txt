A 36-year-old female presented with a sudden onset of severe abdominal pain for 6 h. The patient underwent Mini gastric bypass (MGB) 2 years prior to presentation. Computed tomography (CT) scan revealed a 14-cm dilatation of the cecum occupying the left upper quadrant of the abdomen with the swirling appearance of the mesentery. These findings were confirmed through emergency exploratory laparotomy to be cecal volvulus. a right hemicolectomy and conversion of MGB to a conventional Roux-en-Y gastric bypass.
