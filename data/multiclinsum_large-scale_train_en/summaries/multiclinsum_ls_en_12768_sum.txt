The patient, a 14-year-old male, had swelling in his right leg with intermittent pain for one year. Plain radiography, computed tomography (CT) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) were performed. Based on the radiological and histological examinations, a diagnosis of OFD-like adamantinoma was suspected. After admission, the patient underwent tumor resection of the right tibia, free transplantation of the left fibula and internal fixation. After resection of the tumor, the wound recovered well, the vital signs were stable, and activity was normal. The patient has been followed up for more than a year with no recurrence or distant metastasis.
