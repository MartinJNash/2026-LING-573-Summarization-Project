13 years earlier, this patient had undergone aortic valve replacement for severe aortic regurgitation caused by infective endocarditis with aortic valve vegetations. Since then, the patient had been treated with the oral anticoagulant warfarin. The aneurysm was successfully treated by a saphenous vein graft and administration of antibiotics. The patient likely developed septic ankle and aneurysm as a consequence of infective endocarditis.
