We report a case of a 66-year-old Russian female who developed chest pain accompanied by electrocardiogram (ECG) changes-biphasic T waves in V1-V4 leads after an initially uncomplicated premature ventricular complex CA procedure. After examination and investigations, including transthoracic echocardiography (TTE), cardiac magnetic resonance imaging (CMR) and cardiac computed tomography (CCT), she was diagnosed with regional pericarditis, which occurred even though the ablation was uneventful with the limited number of radiofrequency applications. Furthermore, the diagnosis was difficult due to normal body temperature and the absence of pericardial effusion and myocardial abnormalities on TTE, findings that are not characteristic of pericarditis. The patient's last office visit was in 6 months after the procedure. Neither patient had any complaintsnor there were any changes on ECG and TTE.
