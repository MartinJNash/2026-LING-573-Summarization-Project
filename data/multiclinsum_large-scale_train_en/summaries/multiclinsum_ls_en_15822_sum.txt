A 41-year-old Saudi woman presented to our gynecological clinic with abnormal vaginal bleeding. The initial clinical diagnosis was left ovarian cyst. The results of the remainder of her abdominopelvic examination were normal. Peri-operatively, the left ovarian mass resembled a hemorrhagic solid tumor. It was sent for frozen sectioning, which revealed it was an undifferentiated neoplasm. The final histopathological examination showed a vascular neoplasm showing vasoformative arborizing channels of variable sizes and shapes lined by atypical endothelial cells with intact capsule. Areas of necrosis were seen, along with fused anastomosing solid vascular area. She was diagnosed as having an angiosarcoma of intermediate grade, International Federation of Gynecology and Obstetrics stage IA.
