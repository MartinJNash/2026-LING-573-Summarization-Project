A 73-year-old man with a history of radical cystectomy and reconstruction of the ileal conduit and right nephroureterectomy was diagnosed with recurrence at the uretero-ileal anastomosis site. Bleeding from the tumor could not be controlled by flexible gastrointestinal endoscopy. The patient underwent coagulation via an ileal conduit approach using a rigid scope and bipolar electrocautery, which is usually a modality for transurethral resection.
