We report a case of extensive acute demyelinating encephalomyelitis (ADEM) with massive brain swelling. A 45-year-old female presented to the emergency room with status epilepticus. Patient has no history of any associated medical problems. Glasgow coma scale (GCS) was 15/15. CT brain was normal. Lumbar puncture was done and cerebrospinal fluid showed pleocytosis and increased protein content. About 2 days after admission, the conscious level rapidly deteriorated and GCS was 3/15, with the right pupil fully dilated and unreactive to light. Computed tomography and magnetic resonance imaging brain were done. We performed an urgent decompressive craniectomy as a life-saving procedure. Histopathological examination was suggestive of ADEM.
