The main focus of this study is to outline a rare case of CASTLE compressing the trachea in a 50-year-old female patient who was initially diagnosed with undifferentiated thyroid carcinoma, for which she underwent unsuccessful surgery, as well as postoperative radiotherapy and chemotherapy. After receiving a second medical opinion, the patient underwent a challenging radical resection consisting in total thyroidectomy and central neck dissection, with no local recurrence after 6 months and 2 years of follow-up and negative metastatic follow-up. The correct diagnosis has been established based on pathological and immunohistochemical examinations.
