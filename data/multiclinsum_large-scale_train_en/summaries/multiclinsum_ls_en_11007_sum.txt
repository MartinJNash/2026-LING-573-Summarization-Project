A 69-year-old female was admitted to our hospital with shortness of breath and abdominal pain. Abdominal computed tomography revealed intramural gas in the bowel and free gas in the mesenteric veins and portal vein. Cranial computed tomography, which was performed due to impaired consciousness, demonstrated cerebral air embolism with the appearance of a gyriform pattern. A bedside echocardiography and chest computed tomography revealed no evidence of right-to-left shunt.
