A 32-year-old lady presented to us with the chief complain of left proptosis, diplopia, and left eye soreness without decline of visual acuity for about 2 months. Her orbital magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) disclosed a mass lesion located in the left frontal base, orbital roof, and upper medial orbital region with adjacent dural-tail sign favoring meningioma. She underwent a left supraorbital pterional craniotomy with the gross total removal of tumor and dura reconstruction. Histology examination of the tumor showed a picture of giant cell tumor of bone. Considering giant cell tumor of bone is locally aggressive, postoperative adjuvant therapy with Denosumab was introduced after full explanation.
