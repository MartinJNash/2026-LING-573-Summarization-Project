A 46-year-old man underwent transurethral resection of the bladder tumor for a bladder tumor 61 × 52 mm in size on contrast-enhanced computed tomography. The patient only had micturition attacks and was suspected to have urothelial carcinoma on magnetic resonance imaging. The patient had acute respiratory distress syndrome after the operation which improved conservatively. The 123Iodine metaiodobenzylguanidine scintigraphy, urinalysis, and pathological examination revealed bladder paraganglioma. Robot-assisted radical cystectomy and ileal neobladder reconstruction were performed.
