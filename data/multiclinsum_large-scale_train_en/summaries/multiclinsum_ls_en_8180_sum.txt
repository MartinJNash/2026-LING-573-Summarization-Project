We report the case of thoracoscopic surgery consisting of stapler-closure of the left atrial appendage and bilateral epicardial clamp-isolation of the pulmonary veins performed in a 66-year-old male patient with symptomatic persistent non-valvular AF who became unable to take in anticoagulants or anti-arrhythmic drugs because of thromboembolic SMA occlusion and subsequent total resection of the small intestine. The patient has been free from thromboembolic or arrhythmic symptoms during 6 months of follow-up despite taking no anticoagulant or anti-arrhythmic drugs. Electrocardiographic monitoring demonstrated a stable sinus rhythm for 48 h at postoperative Months 3 and 6. Echocardiography manifested an improvement of the left ventricular ejection fraction from a preoperative value of 44-69% at postoperative Month 6.
