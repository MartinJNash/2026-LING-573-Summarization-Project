A 36-year-old gravida 6, para 1-1-3-1 with elevated prepregnancy PAI-1 levels, a history of early-onset preeclampsia with severe features superimposed on chronic hypertension, intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR), and recurrent pregnancy loss (RPL), presented with a dichorionic-diamniotic twin gestation. She was managed with aspirin and enoxaparin and delivered appropriately grown twins at 36 weeks and 3 days, due to the development of preeclampsia superimposed on chronic hypertension. She was discharged not on enoxaparin and represented with pulmonary edema on postoperative day 8.
