A 77-year-old man underwent carotid endarterectomy and bailout stenting. Although there were no obvious preoperative risk factors for intraoperative ventricular tachyarrhythmia, ventricular fibrillation (VF) had occurred during a maneuver of the carotid artery under hypercapnia. QTc was prolonged from 317 ms before surgery to 458 ms before the onset of VF. QTc dispersion between leads II and III was also increased to 50 ms. Hypomagnesemia was noted after resuscitation by electrical defibrillation, adrenaline, and noradrenaline.
