A 25-year-old man experienced double vision for 1 month. Computed tomography and magnetic resonance imaging revealed a clival tumor. The endonasal endoscopic transsphenoidal approach (EEA) was used, and partial resection was performed because of massive bleeding. Histological examination showed a GCT. After radiation therapy, the tumor recurred; the EEA and the anterior transpetrosal approaches were used to perform second and third operations, respectively. The MIB-1 index increased from 4.2% to 26.3%.
