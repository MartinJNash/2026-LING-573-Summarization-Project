An 18-year-old man was admitted to our hospital with a head injury caused by a traffic accident. On arrival, he was conscious with a mild headache. Computed tomography (CT) revealed no intracranial hemorrhages or skull fractures but an AC was seen in the left convexity. One month later, follow-up CT scans showed an intracystic hemorrhage. Subsequently, an SDH appeared then both the intracystic hemorrhage and SDH gradually shrank, with the AC disappearing spontaneously. The AC was considered to have disappeared, along with the spontaneous SDH resorption.
