An 86-year-old female presented in the ED of our hospital with a complaint of chest pain. She recalled a fall at home the previous day and described how her fist was impacted between the floor and her chest. A physical examination revealed local tenderness in the mid-chest. A lateral chest x-ray indicated a sternal fracture, and a chest computed tomography scan ruled out concomitant injuries. There were no acute changes on her electrocardiogram (ECG). Conservative outpatient treatment was started, and referrals were made with a recommendation to initiate fall prevention measures and osteoporosis screening in primary health care.
