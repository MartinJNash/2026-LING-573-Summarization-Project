A 45 year old office worker presented to the Casualty of our hospital with bilateral anterior shoulder dislocations with greater tuberosity fractures following an episode of seizure. Both shoulders were reduced by Kocher manoeuvre using total intravenous anaesthesia (TIVA) & were strapped to the chest for 6 weeks. At the end of 1 year follow-up, there were no reasonable loss of strength or restriction of motion and the shoulders were defined as stable.
