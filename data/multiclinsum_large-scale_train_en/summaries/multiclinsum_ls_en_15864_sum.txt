A 41-year-old woman with SS complicated by recently progressive ILD was scheduled for an elective radical hysterectomy under the diagnosis of cervical cancer. We performed CSE with separate needle technique (SNT) using two different interspaces. An epidural catheter was inserted at T11-T12 before administration of spinal medication at L3-L4. We could achieve successful anesthetic management for radical hysterectomy, maintaining stable hemodynamic variables. Postoperative analgesia, using epidural catheter, was effective and devoid of any postoperative pulmonary morbidity.
