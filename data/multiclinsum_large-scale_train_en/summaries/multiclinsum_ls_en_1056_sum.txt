We present a case of an 87-year-old woman who had an extraction of a ventricular pacing lead that had perforated to an extra-cardiac location, most likely to the left pleural space. Peri-procedural TOE was used as a precaution. The entire pacing lead was successfully extracted with gentle traction using standard equipment (mechanical technique). Extraction was followed by development of pneumomediastinum and a left pleural effusion, initially attributed to pulmonary injury from the pacing lead but which proved to be related to oesophageal injury from the TOE.
