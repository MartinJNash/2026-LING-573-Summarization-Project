We report a case of a young male patient who sustained ipsilateral acetabular, femoral neck, and shaft fractures in addition to a contralateral floating knee injury following a motor vehicle collision. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first report that describes the simultaneous occurrence of these fractures and their management. The patient attained an excellent functional outcome with union of all his fractures. We, therefore, propose a management flowchart for this rare case scenario.
