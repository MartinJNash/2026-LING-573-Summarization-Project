In this case report, we describe the clinical history and presentation of a 76-year-old man who developed mild sarcopenic dysphasia following a period of physical inactivity after spinal stenosis surgery, which resulted in a loss of 10 kg of body weight in the 10-month period after surgery. The patient's dysphasia was managed with laryngeal suspension, performed via a minimally invasive thyromandibulopexy, in combination with rehabilitation and nutritional support. After a brief period of postoperative rehabilitation, the patient was able to eat soft meals on postoperative day 14, and a regular meal on postoperative day 18, without aspiration. We include a brief description of our surgical technique in the case report.
