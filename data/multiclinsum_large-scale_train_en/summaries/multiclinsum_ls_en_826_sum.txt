We present a case of a 34-year-old man of Nilo-Hamitic ethnicity who presented to our unit with paraplegia following injury from the collapse of a concrete wall. Radiographic images showed spondyloptosis at the fourth lumbar vertebral level. He underwent surgery where decompression, reduction, posterior instrumentation and bone grafting through a posterior approach were done. He started regaining motor power 48 hours postoperatively. He is currently undergoing rehabilitation and is steadily improving, 2 months postoperatively.
