This report details the case of a 64-year-old-male who developed a left common iliac artery mycotic pseudoaneurysm from Proteus mirabilis, which was associated with a prevertebral abscess. The patient presented with isolated, left lower extremity edema and intermittent fevers. The case is unique in both the pathogen (P mirabilis) and in its association with presumed direct arterial wall infection from an adjacent prevertebral abscess.
