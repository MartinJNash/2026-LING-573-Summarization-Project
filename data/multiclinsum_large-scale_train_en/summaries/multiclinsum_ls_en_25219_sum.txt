A 4-year-old girl was admitted in the pediatrics department for fever and bilateral knee joint pain for 3 days. She was a diagnosed case of with cerebral palsy, psyco-developmental delay, and epileptogenic disorder put under valproic acid. She was toxic and febrile. Within 4 h after admission, both knees developed tense shiny intense swelling associated with pain, redness, and local rise of temperature with limited active range of motion. Near-complete passive range of motion was noted. There were no abnormal findings on the rest of the musculoskeletal examination. Aspiration of the knee revealed subperiosteal hematoma diagnostic of scurvy.
