An 86-year-old female with appendicitis complicated by HPVG was reported in the present study. Abdominal examination revealed rebound tenderness at the McBurney's point. Moreover, abdominal computed tomography (CT) revealed gas in portal and mesenteric veins in addition to appendicitis. An emergency operation was planned on the appendix. However, the patient refused surgical treatment. Therefore, conservative treatment of antibiotics and frequent imaging observation was conducted for this patient. Although imaging results suggested disappeared gas in intra- and extra-hepatic portal veins, the small intestine was dilated, after conservative treatment of antibiotics. In addition, signs of diffused peritonitis could also be observed and an exploratory laparotomy was performed. Intra-operative findings had confirmed suppurated appendix, mesenteric ischemia and small intestinal necrosis.
