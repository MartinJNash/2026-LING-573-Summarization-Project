The patient was a four-year-old Iranian boy, without any history of health problems or hospitalization, except for jaundice and phototherapy in the neonatal period. He had no neurological signs or symptoms during admission, and he was admitted only with chief complaints of acute onset of fever, coryza, and icterus. In the neurological consultation, brain MRI was requested to analyze the possibility of brain damage. The results indicated the involvement of cerebellum, thalamus, and basal ganglia, which led to the diagnosis of ANEC.
