We report the case of a 45-year-old female patient with a history of seronegative rheumatoid arthritis (RA) who presented with swelling and tenderness of the third metacarpophalangeal joint of the right hand. A flare of her RA was suspected based on clinical and ultrasound findings which showed a tenosynovitis with intense power doppler activity. Her steroid dose was increased but the clinical response to glucocorticoid therapy was very limited. Subsequently, she developed skin manifestations of 'swimmer's granuloma' over the next 2 weeks after first presentation. Finally, a diagnosis of a Mycobacterium marinum infection was established with the help of tissue biopsy and culture, and the patient received appropriate antibiotic treatment with the desired effect.
