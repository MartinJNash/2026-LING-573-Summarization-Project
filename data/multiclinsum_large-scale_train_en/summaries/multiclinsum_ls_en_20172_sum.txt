A 12-year-old Syrian girl was referred to Damascus University's Children's Hospital for recurrent pulmonary infection. Her mother had noted an absence of tears when crying since birth, diffused pigmentations since birth, especially on the cheeks and genitals, recurrent vomiting of both solid and liquid foods, and recurrent exacerbations of bronchitis and recurrent pneumonia. ACTH and blood cortisol levels indicated an adrenal insufficiency, chest computed tomography and barium swallow test results indicated achalasia, tear break-up time as well as eye examination indicated alacrimia, which led to the diagnosis of triple A syndrome. Treatment included Heller cardiomyotomy, artificial tears, and hydrocortisone (15-30 mg/m2), as well as continuous observation of ACTH levels.
