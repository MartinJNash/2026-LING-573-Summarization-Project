A 64-year-old woman was admitted to our hospital because she became malnourished due to loss of appetite. She had no family history of gastrointestinal polyposis and was diagnosed with gastric polyposis and polyp-related anemia eight years previously. She received endoscopic submucosal dissection of early gastric cancer twice in another hospital. Thereafter, the patient received an annual upper gastrointestinal endoscopy and took iron supplements for anemia due to occasional bleeding from polyps. However, the number of polyps increased over time. Enhanced computed tomography showed gastric wall thickening and multiple gastric polyps. She was diagnosed as having JPST and underwent laparoscopic total gastrectomy. She was discharged on postoperative Day 10.
