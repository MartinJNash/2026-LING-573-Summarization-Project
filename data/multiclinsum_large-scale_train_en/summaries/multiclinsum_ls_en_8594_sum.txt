A 31-year-old woman was admitted to our hospital because of a mass in the right neck. Surgical exploration validated the absence of the left lobe of the thyroid and parathyroid glands, and pathological examination of the excised nodules confirmed Hashimoto's thyroiditis. Patients with TH might show accompanying absence of the ipsilateral parathyroid gland. The case described here, in which TH was combined with Hashimoto's thyroiditis, is rare in the medical literature. The operation should be ended at once if Hashimoto's thyroiditis is diagnosed during surgery.
