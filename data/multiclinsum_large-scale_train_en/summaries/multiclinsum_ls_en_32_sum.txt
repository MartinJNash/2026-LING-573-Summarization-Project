A 31-year-old gravida 4 para 0 who presented at 22-weeks gestation with vaginal bleeding to an outside hospital and expelled a mass 11 cm in diameter from the vagina during her admission. Findings were consistent with a high grade gynecologic sarcoma. She underwent planned cesarean section at 36 weeks gestational age with uterine pathology showing no sarcoma. At her 3 month postpartum visit she was found to have a 1 cm posterior vaginal wall lesion which was resected and consistent with vaginal sarcoma. She underwent adjuvant brachytherapy.
