A 52-year-old male presented with the right paracentral disc herniations at the C4-C5 and C5-C6 levels. A two-level ACDF was performed. Notably, on the 5th postoperative day, the patient developed an acute respiratory distress due to a large right lateral retrotracheal hematoma requiring emergency evacuation at the bedside. This was followed by formal ligation of a branch of the right superior thyroid artery in the operating room. In addition, an emergency tracheotomy was performed. By postoperative day 15, the tracheotomy was removed, and the patient was neurologically intact.
