In this study, we presented a case of a 64-year-old man exhibiting symptoms of intermittent fever, asthenia, and dyspnea following light exertion. Physical examination, laboratory tests, and electrocardiograms were suspected of IE, though the results of blood cultures were exactly negative. Three-dimensional transthoracic echocardiography, as well as a series of novel advanced techniques, was adopted to clearly visualize the lesions of the aortic valve and aortic root. However, despite active medical treatment modalities, the patient eventually suffered from a sudden, unexpected death 5 days later.
