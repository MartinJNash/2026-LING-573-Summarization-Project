A 42-year-old man was referred to our department for further evaluation of an incidentally detected right adrenal tumor. He was a patient at another hospital for neurofibromatosis type 1. The serum and urinary catecholamine levels exceeded the normal range. Abdominal computed tomography and magnetic resonance imaging showed a 2.8 cm diameter right adrenal tumor, and 123I-metaiodobenzyguanidine scintigraphy showed radioisotope uptake. He was diagnosed with pheochromocytoma and underwent a right laparoscopic adrenalectomy. Histopathological examination revealed that the tumor consisted of a pheochromocytoma and ganglioneuroma. The final diagnosis was composite pheochromocytoma-ganglioneuroma. Five years after surgery, no recurrence was observed.
