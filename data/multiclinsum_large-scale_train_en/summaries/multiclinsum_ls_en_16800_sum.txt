A 24-year-old male presented with a progressive paraparesis attributed to multiple vertebral hemangiomas (MVH) with epidural extension (i.e. resulting in D1-D3 significant cord compression.), plus congenital cutaneous lesions at the D2-D7 levels. Following preoperative angioembolisation, a D1-D7 laminectomy was performed along with a C7-D8 pedicle screw fixation. Pathologically the bone and cutaneous lesions were spinal cavernous hemangiomas. Postoperatively, the patient regained normal function. As complete excision was not feasible, he subsequently received radiotherapy to prevent tumor recurrence.
