A young man was admitted with intermittent fever. Echocardiography confirmed vegetation on the mitral valve with evidence of valve perforation and severe mitral regurgitation. Three sets of blood cultures became positive for A. defectiva. As he presented with manifestations of mesenteric arterial branch pseudoaneurysm, splenic and renal infarction, mitral valve replacement, and embolization of superior mesenteric aneurysm were operated during 8 weeks' targeted antibiotic therapy.
