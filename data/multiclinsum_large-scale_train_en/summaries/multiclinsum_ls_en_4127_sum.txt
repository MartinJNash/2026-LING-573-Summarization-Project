A healthy 31-year-old man underwent extraction of horizontally impacted wisdom teeth in the bilateral mandible under general anesthesia. During the surgery, no significant changes in respiratory and cardiovascular parameters or neurosurgical abnormalities occurred. After the surgery, the patient was diagnosed with aspiration pneumonia. Furthermore, the results of otorhinolaryngological and neurological examinations led to a diagnosis of a combination of bilateral glossopharyngeal and vagus nerve paralysis, right recurrent nerve paralysis, and right hypoglossal nerve paralysis. In this case, seriously associated peripheral laryngeal paralysis with repeated episodes of aspiration pneumonia improved in approximately 6 months with rehabilitation and vitamin B12 administration, and no complications remained.
