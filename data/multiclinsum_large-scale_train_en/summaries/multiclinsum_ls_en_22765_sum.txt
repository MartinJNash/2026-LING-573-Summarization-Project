We describe an acquired case of SMA syndrome, in an 88-year old male who underwent an open appendicectomy 20 years prior. The patient presented with an 18-month history of progressive anorexia, weight loss, and intractable vomiting. After inconclusive initial investigations, an exploratory laparotomy demonstrated extensive postoperative adhesions, placing traction on the SMA through its ileocolic branch, resulting in acute angulation of the SMA and subsequent external duodenal compression.
