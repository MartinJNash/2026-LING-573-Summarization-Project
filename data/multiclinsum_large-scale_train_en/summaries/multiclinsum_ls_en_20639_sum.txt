A 48-year-old man presented with altered mental status and shortness of breath requiring intubation. Medical history was significant for rheumatoid arthritis, diabetes mellitus, chronic kidney disease, sarcoidosis, and polymyalgia rheumatica. Home medications included prednisone, methotrexate, and tocilizumab. Computed tomography chest revealed multifocal pneumonia with a cavitary nodule with halo sign. One week after extubation, the patient remained confused. Lumbar puncture (LP) was positive for Cryptococcus neoformans within 5 days. Bronchoalveolar lavage (BAL) yielded similar results on fungal culture one month later.
