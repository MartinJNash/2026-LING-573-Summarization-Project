A previously healthy 44-year-old male patient presented with acute heart failure and developed complete atrioventricular (AV) block requiring pacing. Acute heart failure was managed with inotropic support, non-invasive ventilation, and implantation of a permanent AV-sequential pacemaker. Cardiac magnetic resonance imaging was suggestive of myocarditis and endomyocardial biopsy diagnosed EM histologically. Endomyocardial biopsy was essential for definite aetiologic assignment, thus dispelling initial reservations about immunosuppressive therapy. Final treatment strategy consisted of steroids and Azathioprine.
