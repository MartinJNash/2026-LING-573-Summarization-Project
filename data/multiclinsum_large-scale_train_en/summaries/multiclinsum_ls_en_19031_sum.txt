We present a 53-year-old male of Aboriginal descent with end-stage renal disease due to diabetes who underwent insertion of a peritoneal dialysis (PD) catheter. Five weeks after PD catheter insertion, he started to notice a painful ulcer surrounded by a bed of erythema. The lesion eventually progressed to a purulent, hemorrhagic ulcer surrounded by a raised, irregular, violaceous border along the entirety of the PD catheter tunnel. There was no history of underlying systemic diseases commonly associated with PG. The catheter was removed, and an elliptical biopsy was taken, which ruled out infection, malignancy, and vasculitis. The changes were felt to be consistent with PG. The patient underwent treatment initially with topical corticosteroids, followed by oral prednisone, which unfortunately worsened his diabetic control; due to this, he was transitioned to cyclosporine, with complete resolution.
