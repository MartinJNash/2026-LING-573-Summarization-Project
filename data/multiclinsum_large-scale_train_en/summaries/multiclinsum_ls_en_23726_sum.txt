A 75-year-old man with metastatic lung cancer and an abdominal aortic aneurysm presented with high fever for 3 days. He was diagnosed with septic shock and was admitted to our hospital. The blood culture was positive for S. pyogenes, and streptococcal toxic shock syndrome was diagnosed. During treatment, enhanced computed tomography revealed an increase in the size of the abdominal aortic aneurysm, leading to the diagnosis of an infected aortic aneurysm. Replacement of the aneurysm with a synthetic graft was carried out successfully. The patient gradually recovered after the surgery.
