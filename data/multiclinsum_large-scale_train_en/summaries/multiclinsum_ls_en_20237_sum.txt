We present the case of a 33-year-old Caucasian male who developed first episode of psychosis at the age of 29 years. There was no history of previous mental illness either in the patient, or in any of his family members. He had been a long- term heavy smoker and was experiencing progressive lower limb claudication since the age of 22 years; however, all inflammatory, autoimmune and atherosclerotic markers were negative. His psychosis was characterized by retention of a warm affect, and despite some amelioration, was generally resistant to a fair trial of several anti-psychotic medications including Clozapine.
