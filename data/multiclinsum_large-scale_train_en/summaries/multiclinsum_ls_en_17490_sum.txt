We presented with a 63 y.o. Female, who admitted to our Hospital with a chief complaint of pain when urinating and frequent haematuria. She has a history of Turner Syndrome (45 XO). Local examination in the lithotomy position revealed labial agglutination involving the labia majora. We did a Cystoscopy to evaluate the bladder and urethra, and then biopsied the tissues. Vaginal Reconstruction was performed to repair the Labial Synechiae. The biopsy showed that this histological finding is consistent with Follicular Cystitis that could cause by chronic UTIs.
