We reported on a 34-year-old female patient who was admitted to the hospital with periumbilical pain for 3 days at 34 + 1 weeks of pregnancy. The result of imaging examination showed that there was an inflammatory mass in the middle and lower abdominal wall in the third trimester of pregnancy. The periumbilical abscess was punctured and drained first, and then the pregnant woman was assisted to give birth to a baby girl through vagina after the condition was stable.Subsequently, laparotomy + abdominal abscess resection and drainage + partial small bowel resection + ileostomy were performed. Pathology showed inflammatory mass.
