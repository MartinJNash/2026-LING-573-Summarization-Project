A female infant was born at 37 weeks of gestation by vaginal delivery with no asphyxia. Her mother had no abnormal findings, including glucose metabolism disorders, upon periodic prenatal visits. Upon routine examination at the first hour of life, the baby was lethargic, pale, hypotonic, and rarely cried. An emergent systemic evaluation was performed, and she was diagnosed with severe hyperinsulinemic hypoglycaemia with blood glucose of 11 mg/dL and insulin of 2.7 µU/mL. She was soon fed with milk and her symptoms of hypoglycaemia was resolved before receiving intravenous glucose infusion. Her blood glucose level reached 78 mg/dL 3 h after delivery. She was discharged home with her mother on day 6 of age without relapse of hypoglycaemia. Upon review, we determined that the mother was diagnosed with GDM during her previous pregnancy but not during this current pregnancy. The infant had no developmental delay upon check-up at 6 months.
