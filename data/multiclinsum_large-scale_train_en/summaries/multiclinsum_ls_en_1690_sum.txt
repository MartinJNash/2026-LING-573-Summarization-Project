A 58-year-old man was admitted with high suspicion of endocarditis lenta complicated by severe kidney dysfunction and anaemia. He was treated with penicillin and gentamycin. Transthoracic echocardiogram revealed a large vegetation at the mitral valve and MALDI Biotyper Massa Spectrometer (Microflex) revealed S. sinensis as the pathogen. The patient refused to receive blood transfusions or surgery. Due to cerebral infarction with haemorrhagic transformation, the patient died 8 days after admission.
