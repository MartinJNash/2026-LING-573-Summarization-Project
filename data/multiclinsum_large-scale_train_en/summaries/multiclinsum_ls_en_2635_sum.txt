A 15-year-old male cross-country runner presented with bilateral hip pain after sprint training. The patient describes going "flat out" and "overstriding" before collapsing with immediate pain. MRI imaging confirmed an acute bilateral Anterior Inferior Iliac Spine Injury (AIIS). Conservative management included protected weight-bearing, a hip strengthening program and graded return to play. At 6 months, the patient returned to full sporting activities with no symptoms.
