A 75-year-old man presented with abdominal distention, vomiting, and constipation. On radiological imaging, a mass in the terminal ileum was detected. After 2 days of ineffective conservative therapy, the authors decided to perform an open surgery. Enterotomy approach was chosen after failing to milk the object into the colons. The foreign body impacting the bowels was identified as a meat bolus bezoar. The patient improved after the surgery. The authors recorded no recurrence or complications with our patient after 18 months of follow-up.
