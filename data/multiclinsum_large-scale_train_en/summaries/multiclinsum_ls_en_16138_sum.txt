An elderly 70-year-old female presented with Lower abdominal pain radiating to the medial side of the left leg over a year that has become persistent and continuous over the 3 day period. Patient was found to have a Richter's type of strangulated obturator hernia on diagnostic laparoscopy. A laparoscopic assisted resection of the strangulated small bowel and repair of the Hernia was performed. Postoperatively patient had an uneventful recovery.
