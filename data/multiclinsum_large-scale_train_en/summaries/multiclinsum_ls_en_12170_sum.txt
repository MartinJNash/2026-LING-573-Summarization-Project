A 32 year-old woman who presented with hypoxia after the delivery of her fourth child was found to have heart failure with severe mitral regurgitation and multiple abnormal intracardiac shunts. Echocardiography showed a large circular structure with Doppler color flow into the left atrium and between the atria. Cardiac computed tomography showed multiple dilated coronary arteries including a left circumflex coronary artery aneurysm measuring >10 cm in diameter with fistulous communication to the left atrium and a large atrial septal defect. A right heart catheterization was performed, and the patient was diagnosed with high-output heart failure. Surgical closure of the coronary cameral fistula was deferred due to the risk of worsening pressure in the coronary aneurysm, and the patient was referred for cardiac transplantation.
