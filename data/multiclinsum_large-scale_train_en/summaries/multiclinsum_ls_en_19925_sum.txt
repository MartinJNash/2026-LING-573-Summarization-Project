A 30-year-old female presented with a 5-week history of a painful rapidly-growing mass over the proximal phalanx of the right ring finger. The mass infiltrated the entire flexor sheath over the proximal phalanx as well as the checkrein ligament of the proximal interphalangeal joint. The common digital artery of the 3rd web space was obliterated by the tumor. The radial digital nerve of the ring finger was stretched and attenuated. Total excision was done and histology confirmed the diagnosis of myositis ossificans. No recurrence was seen at the one-year follow-up.
