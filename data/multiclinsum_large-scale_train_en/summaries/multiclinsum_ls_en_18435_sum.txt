A 51-year-old male, smoker, with hypertension and diabetes mellitus was referred to our hospital for rest pain in left limb and peripheral cyanosis. Ultrasound doppler (US) showed an occlusion after common femoral artery with patency of Anterior-tibial artery (ATA) two centimeters after the origin. The unavailability of adequate autologous conduit necessitated an alternative solution and was chosen a composite femoro-anterior tibial artery bypass with successive ATA angioplasty to ensure the patency of graft.
