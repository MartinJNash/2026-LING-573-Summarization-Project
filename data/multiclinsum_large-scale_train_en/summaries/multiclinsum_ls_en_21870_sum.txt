A 46-year-old female patient presented with recurrent epistaxis for 7 months. A 0-degree rigid scope examination revealed a left nasal cavity mass. A non-contrasted CT scan of the paranasal sinus showed heterogeneous and hypodense lesion in the left nasal cavity. Functional endoscopic sinus surgery was performed and multiple nasal-mass biopsies were taken which showed high grade spindle cell sarcoma favoring synovial type. Further workup and management were done from MRI, cranial tumor resection with skull base reconstruction to adjuvant radiotherapy. Patient has regular follow up with involved specialties and thus far is doing well.
