An 81-year-old immunocompetent female local farmer noticed the presence of a nodule on her right ankle 5 years before her first visit to our hospital. A cystic mass of approximately 45 mm × 30 mm was present at the tip of the right lateral malleolus. Culture of the aspirated fluid revealed visibly black colonies and characteristic blackish hyphae; nucleotide sequence of the internal transcribed spacer region was determined and compared in a GenBank database. The results indicated Kirschsteiniothelia infection.
