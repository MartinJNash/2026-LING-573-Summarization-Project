A 20-year-old Japanese man presented with intermittent fever lasting for 2 months. Radiological imaging demonstrated multiple nodules of uneven shape and size in both lungs. Video-assisted thoracic surgical lung biopsy showed abnormal lymphocyte infiltration, which was positive for CD3, CD56, and perforin. In situ hybridization for EBV-encoded RNA was positive. From these findings, he was diagnosed with ENKL with lung involvement. The patient was successfully treated with intensive combinational chemotherapy followed by allogeneic cord blood transplantation. He has been alive with continuous complete remission for 1 year after diagnosis.
