A 64-year-old female was referred from a primary care clinic due to longstanding headache. A non-enhanced CT scan of the brain revealed osteolytic bony lesions at the petrous apices and an empty sella. A brain MRI with contrast showed CSF-containing lesions in the petrous apices, communicating with Meckel's cave bilaterally. The patient was managed conservatively and is currently followed up in the neurosurgery clinic.
