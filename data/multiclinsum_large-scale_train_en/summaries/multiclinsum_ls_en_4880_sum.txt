A 66-year-old Syrian male with a history of psoriatic arthritis presented for evaluation of 2-month nonpainful ulcers on his feet and hands that were treated with warfarin. Biopsies confirmed the diagnosis of calciphylaxis. The patient received sodium thiosulfate, zoledronic acid, intralesional sodium thiosulfate injections, and an intravenous infusion of vitamin K with dramatic improvement. At the 3-month follow-up, his wounds had been completely remitted.
