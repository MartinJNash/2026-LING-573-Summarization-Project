A middle-age male patient presented with acute abdominal pain and repeated vomiting after blunt abdominal trauma. CT-scan showed a large hydatid cyst of the right lobe of the liver with features of intraperitoneal rupture. Emergency laparotomy was performed with extraction of a countless number of daughter cysts from the peritoneal cavity. We further examined the bowel and found no abnormalities. The cyst was not causing any compression on the bile duct. Omentoplasty was performed for the cavity. The patient was discharged home eight days after the operation and the drain removed after twenty-five days when the bile leak stopped, anthelminthic drugs were given for three months after the surgery.
