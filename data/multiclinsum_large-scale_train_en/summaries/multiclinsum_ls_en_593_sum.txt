A man with well-controlled HIV developed nephrotic range proteinuria, and kidney biopsy revealed lupus-like glomerulonephritis with a predominantly membranous pattern of injury. He opted for conservative therapy and experienced spontaneous and sustained remission. Subsequent testing revealed neural epidermal growth factor-like 1 (NELL1)-positive glomerular immune deposits. NELL1-positive glomerular immune deposits were identified in a total of 2 of 5 tested HIV-associated membranous nephropathy (MN), which were morphologically dissimilar and one of which weakly co-expressed phospholipase A2 receptor (PLA2R).
