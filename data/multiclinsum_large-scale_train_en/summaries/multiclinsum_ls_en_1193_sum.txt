We described an extremely rare case of a 64-year-old woman who developed reconstitution of the occluded ICA through hypertrophied vasa vasorum supplying recurrent TCCF previously treated by ligation of the internal and external carotid arteries. Usual endovascular treatment may be challenging for this patient due to inaccessible route from the affected ICA through multiple small vessels. The patient was successfully treated with trapping procedure and obliteration the fistula using combined surgical and endovascular treatment.
