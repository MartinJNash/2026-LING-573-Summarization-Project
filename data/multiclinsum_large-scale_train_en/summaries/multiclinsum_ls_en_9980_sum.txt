We herein present a rare case with GC and high level of serum AFP. A 64-year-old Chinese female underwent gastrectomy was diagnosed as gastric adenocarcinoma and the pathological stage was T1bN0M0, IA. With the progression of disease, the tumor widely metastasized and the serum AFP level increased progressively with the highest level of 3396 ng/mL. She successively entered into 3 lines palliative systematic chemotherapy and fourth-line targeted therapy of apatinib, a small molecule tyrosine kinase inhibitor targeting vascular endothelial growth factor receptor-2. Although previous studies suggested that the prognosis of this special type of GC was poor, this patient lived for 22 months after tumor transfer. Apatinib kept her progression-free survival for 5 months, and the overall survival was 4.5 years.
