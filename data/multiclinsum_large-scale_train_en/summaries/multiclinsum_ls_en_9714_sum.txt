We report a case of cryptococcal osteomyelitis in a 45-year-old female who presented with swelling and pain in the left inner thigh. After a biopsy of the pubic bone and surrounding soft tissue, the pathological results and bacterial culture of the biopsy tissue confirmed Cryptococcus neoformans infection. After draining the pus by aspiration and administering oral fluconazole (400 mg/d) treatment, the patient's symptoms disappeared.
