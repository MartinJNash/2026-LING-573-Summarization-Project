We present a 27-year-old female post ASD repair in childhood with a unique presentation of recurrent abortion in adulthood besides exertional dyspnoea. A full workup of investigations was normal except for haemoglobin of 21 and oxygen saturation of 70%. TEE revealed abnormal drainage of IVC to the LA. Surgical correction was done to release the IVC opening to drain in the right atrium and the oxygen saturation reached 99% after weaning from the bypass machine. The postoperative course was uneventful, and the patient was discharged 5 days later. Two years later, she got pregnant twice and completed her pregnancies to term with well-developed infants.
