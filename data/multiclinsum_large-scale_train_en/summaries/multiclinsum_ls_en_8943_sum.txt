A 71-year-old man underwent ultrasonography at the outpatient clinic through which an upper abdominal tumor was identified. Following computed tomography and endoscopy, a 4.5 cm submucosal tumor in the lesser curvature of the stomach was highly suspected. A laparoscopic partial resection of the stomach was performed. Histopathological examination revealed the tumor to be a leiomyosarcoma arising from the lesser omentum that did not invade the stomach. Immunohistochemical staining showed that the tumor was negative for CD34, c-kit, and S-100 and positive for desmin and α-smooth muscle actin. No recurrence had been observed 1 year after surgery without adjuvant chemotherapy.
