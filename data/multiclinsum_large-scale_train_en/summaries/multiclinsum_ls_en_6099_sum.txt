A 55-year-old female patient presented with complaints of right flank pain and swelling on the right lumbar region. On abdominal examination, there was round protruding swelling immediately beneath the 12th rib of the right upper flank area. The swelling was palpable and bowel sound was heard over it. The swelling was easily reducible and protruded when the patient was coughing or straining. The opposite side of the lumbar region was normal, and chest examination was clear and normal. The hematological and urine analysis laboratory findings were normal.
