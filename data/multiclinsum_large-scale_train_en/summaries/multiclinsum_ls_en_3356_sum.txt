A 38-year-old woman was evaluated for chronic diffuse joint pain and a low alkaline phosphatase level of 27 U/L (reference, 31-125 U/L). In addition, she presented with a history of hypermobility, limb bowing, and hyperextensible skin, prompting genetic testing for EDS and HPP. The results returned significant for a synonymous sequence variant at c.441G>A in the B4GALT7 gene indicative of spEDS. HPP was clinically diagnosed by a repeat low alkaline phosphatase level of 23 U/L and high vitamin B6 level of 24.4 ng/mL (reference, 2.1-21.7 ng/mL), despite the absence of the ALPL gene sequence variation on genetic testing.
