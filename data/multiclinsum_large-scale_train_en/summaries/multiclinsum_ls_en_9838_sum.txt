We report the case of a 65-year-old female patient, who consulted our ENT department for a large ulcerating lesion of the nasal pyramid. CT scan revealed a lesion of the nasal pyramid measuring 38 mm in long axis, which appeared to come into contact with the anterior part of the nasal septum. The pathological findings were consistent with an infiltrating basal cell carcinoma. The patient underwent surgical resection with reconstruction using a forehead flap.
