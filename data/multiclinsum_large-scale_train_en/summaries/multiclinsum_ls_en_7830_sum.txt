A 45-year-old patient was diagnosed with posterior dislocation of hip with ipsilateral femur neck and acetabulum fracture. Plan for emergent hip reduction and primary surgical fixation of fracture fragments was made. Intraoperatively, posterior wall acetabulum fracture was found to be excessively comminuted and non-amenable for fixation and femoral head was found to be avascularized. Subsequently, plan was changed to total hip replacement with acetabular defect reconstruction. At 1-year follow-up, no aseptic loosening was observed on radiographic views of pelvis, with total Harris Hip Score of 91.
