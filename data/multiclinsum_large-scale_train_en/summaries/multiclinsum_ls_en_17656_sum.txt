We report the case of an 87-year-old woman with severe symptomatic aortic stenosis (AS). Pre-procedural multidetector computed tomography (MDCT) showed a calcium bridge between the non-coronary and left cusps. Although these cusps appeared to be fused together in the centre by the calcium bridge, the commissure area between the fused cusps appeared to normally open. We cracked apart the calcium bridge from the commissure area using balloon aortic valvuloplasty, which was guided by transoesophageal echocardiography. Subsequently, we successfully implanted a balloon-expandable transcatheter heart valve (THV).
