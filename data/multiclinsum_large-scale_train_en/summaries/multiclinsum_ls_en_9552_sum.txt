A 2-year-old female child had nephrotic syndrome (urine protein-creatinine ratio 749.1 mg/mg; blood urea nitrogen 11 mg/dL; serum creatinine 0.3 mg/dL; and serum albumin 1.8 g/dL.) Proteinuria resolved on treatment with daily prednisolone for 4 weeks at the dose of 45 mg (2.5 mg/kg/day) but recurred with taper from 25 mg/day to 10 mg/day.
