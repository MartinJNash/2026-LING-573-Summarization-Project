A 19-year-old male patient from a consanguineous family of Bambara ethnicity was seen for progressive walking difficulty and frequent falls. Neurological examination found distalmuscle weakness and atrophy and reduced tendon reflexes in four limbs. Electroneuromyography (ENMG) showed an axonal neuropathy pattern with reduced distal motor amplitudes. Charcot-Marie-Tooth (CMT) gene panel testing (Medical Neurogenetics LLC, Atlanta, GA) was negative. However, whole exome sequencing (WES) revealed a novel biallelic variant in GNE (c.1838G>A:p.Gly613Glu), segregating with the phenotype in the family. This variant is predicted to be pathogenic by several in silicoprediction tools including CADD= 29. Moreover, protein folding model showed major structural disruptions in the mutant protein.
