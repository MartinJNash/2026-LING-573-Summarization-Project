A 39-year-old male presented with pain in the left thigh. He had planned for open reduction internal fixation due to left femur fracture. During the surgery, the surgeon found abnormal appearance of the bone and the surrounding tissue. The histopathology examination showed spindle-to-polygonal epithelioid cells arranged in nodular pattern with necrotic areas. Immunohistochemistry examination revealed vimentin and CK positive expression, while myogenin was negative. Further immunostaining using CD34 and HMB45 were also negative.
