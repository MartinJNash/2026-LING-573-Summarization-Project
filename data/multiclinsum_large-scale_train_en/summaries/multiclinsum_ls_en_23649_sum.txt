A 53-year-old, previously healthy man developed 2 months of progressive shortness of breath. After being treated for presumptive pneumonia, he was admitted with hypoxic respiratory failure, altered mental status, and severe PH. He developed RV failure and subsequent liver failure. He was ruled out for pulmonary embolism. Despite aggressive management with inhaled nitric oxide and epoprostenol, inotropes, and continuous renal replacement therapy, the patient passed away. Post-mortem examination revealed the presence of IVLBCL with extensive involvement notable of the brain, heart, lungs, and pulmonary arteries.
