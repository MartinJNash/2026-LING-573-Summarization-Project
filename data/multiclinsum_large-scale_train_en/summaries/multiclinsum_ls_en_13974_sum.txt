A young pregnant woman (20-year-old) at 12-week's gestation was transferred to our hospital due to chest pain (last 7 days on treatment) in severe medical condition: conscious responses to stimuli, oxygen 2 l/min directly through endotracheal tube, and hypertension (170/90 mmHg). The chest computed tomography scanner showed aortic arch aneurysm with the image of rupture. An urgent surgery for total aortic arch replacement with cardiopulmonary bypass (185 min) including heart arrest period (cross clamp time 80 min), and general hypothermia (25 °C) performed. She was discharged after 20 days with a normal pregnancy development.
