High-grade MEC of the lacrimal gland of the right eye was diagnosed in an orbital exenteration specimen of a 60-year-old HIV-positive man. He presented to the eye unit of a mission hospital in Jos, Nigeria with significant visual impairment in the right eye and a rapidly growing painful superolateral orbital mass. The tumour invaded and completely obliterated the anterior chamber. The lens, ciliary body, and iris were displaced posteriorly but were free from tumour infiltration. The tumour was exenterated, and the patient was subsequently referred for adjuvant radiotherapy. No evidence of recurrence or metastasis has been noted 15 months post-surgery.
