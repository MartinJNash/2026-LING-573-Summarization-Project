A 64-years-old female, complained of acute precordial pain in our hospital for 2 days. She was diagnosed with an acute non-ST-segment elevation myocardial infarction. Aortic angiography revealed emptiness of the left coronary sinus, and coronary angiography showed that the tortuous right coronary artery supplied blood to the left coronary artery through collateral circulation, and the contrast medium spilled from the opening of the left coronary artery. It was suspected that the left coronary artery was opened in the pulmonary artery. This finding was subsequently confirmed by coronary artery CT. The patient refused surgery to restore double coronary circulation and was administered standardized drug treatment. There was no chest pain during the 6-month follow-up.
