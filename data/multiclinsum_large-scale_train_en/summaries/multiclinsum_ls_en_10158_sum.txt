A 65-year-old man presented to the outpatient department for unexplained headache and ptosis for 1 month. Brain imaging showed a slight enhancement tumor in the pituitary fossa, and the endocrinological assessment showed normal results. We considered the tumor as a non-functioning pituitary adenoma before surgery. Then, the tumor was resected by an endonasal endoscopic transsphenoidal approach. The histopathological examination results revealed the pituitary metastasis of HCC. Additional abdominal imaging revealed tumors were located in the left and right liver lobes with portal vein invasion and bilateral ilium metastases. After multidisciplinary cooperation, the patient chose chemotherapy.
