A 24-year-old man complaining of abdominal pain was referred to our hospital and admitted. Computed tomography showed formation of a closed loop of small bowel with a hernia orifice near the hilar region, and poor contrast of the prolapsed small bowel. We suspected ischemic bowel obstruction caused by an internal hernia with a fissure of the greater omentum as the hernia orifice, and performed emergency surgery. Laparoscopic observation revealed that the medial and lateral segments of the liver formed a bridge on the dorsal side at the liver portal, and that the small intestine was ischemic in the gap created between the bridge and the medial and lateral liver segments. A Meckel's diverticulum was also invaginated in the gap. The bridge was dissected out and the hernia orifice was opened to release the bowel obstruction. The small bowel was preserved and the Meckel's diverticulum was resected. The patient's postoperative course was uneventful.
