A 74-year-old Japanese female with a history of cerebral aneurysm clipping and ventriculoperitoneal shunting due to aneurysmal subarachnoid hemorrhage presented with lower abdominal pain, fever, and disturbed consciousness. Clinical findings indicated a diagnosis of large-bowel perforation and ventriculoperitoneal shunt-transmitted bacterial meningitis. Thus, sigmoidectomy and shunt externalization were performed, and the ventriculoperitoneal shunt was converted to a ventriculoatrial one.
