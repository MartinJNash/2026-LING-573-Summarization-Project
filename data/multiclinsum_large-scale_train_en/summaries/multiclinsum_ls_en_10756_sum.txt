A 35-year-old male came to the emergency with alleged history of roadside accident. Radiography showed bilateral Monteggia fracture type III (by the Bados classification). Surgical intervention was required with locking compression plates put on the both sides. Closed reduction of the radial head was done on both sides. The patient was given pop slabs for bilateral forearms for 21 days. After removing the splints, the function of the elbow was determined by the Broberg Morrey score which was on the right side 45 and on the left side 47 and 100 on both sides after 5 months of follow-up.
