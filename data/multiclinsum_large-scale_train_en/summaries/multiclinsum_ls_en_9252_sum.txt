We present a case of 70 years old female patient who presents a voluminous goiter with an 11 cm mass of the left sixth rib. Trans-parietal biopsy proved its metastatic origin from a thyroid papillary carcinoma. The patient was treated with total thyroidectomy and radiation therapy as the metastatic tissue is radioiodine refractory. Pathology revealed a papillary carcinoma with an insular component. A year later, the patient develops another metastasis to the sphenoid bone extending to the sella turcica, cavernous sinus, and carotid arteries. Treatment was based on kinase inhibitor.
