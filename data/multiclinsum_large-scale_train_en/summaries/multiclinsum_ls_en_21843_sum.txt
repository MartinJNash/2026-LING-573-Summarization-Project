A 72-year-old man presented to our hospital with frequent cough and dyspnea. Contrast-enhanced computed tomography revealed renal cell carcinoma cT3bN0M1. Ipragliflozin, a sodium glucose co-transporter 2 inhibitor, treatment was initiated to control blood glucose levels. Two years after diagnosis, computed tomography revealed remarkable tumor regression without any systemic therapy other than ipragliflozin.
