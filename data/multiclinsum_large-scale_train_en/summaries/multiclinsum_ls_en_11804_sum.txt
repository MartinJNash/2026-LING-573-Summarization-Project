A 72-year-old man was referred to our division with a muscle-invasive bladder cancer. He had a history of intensity-modulated radiation therapy for localized prostate cancer. After three courses of platinum-based neoadjuvant chemotherapy, he obtained a radiologic complete response. He elected for robot-assisted radical cystectomy, standard lymph node dissection, and intracorporeal ileal conduit urinary diversion. Pathological findings revealed no residual tumor within the bladder and residual tumor in the prostate. He had discharged without any complications; and quality of life had improved.
