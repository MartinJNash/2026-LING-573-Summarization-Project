We report the case of a young gentleman who presented with acute stupor following his first instance of heroin vapor inhalation. He later confessed to trans-conjunctival application of the drug as well. His Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) score improved within four days of admission, however, the neurologic sequalae such as cognitive impairment, spastic paraparesis and urge incontinence only partially resolved at three months. Abnormal white matter hyperintensities with restricted diffusion on brain magnetic resonance imaging and history of heroin abuse led to diagnosis of toxic leukoencephalopathy.
