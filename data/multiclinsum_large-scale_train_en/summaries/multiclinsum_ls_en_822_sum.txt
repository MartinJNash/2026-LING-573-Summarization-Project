A 59-year-old female with a history of diabetes and hypertension, presented with the acute onset of a high-grade fever, generalized back pain, and an evolving quadriparesis. Preliminary laboratory studies revealed elevated inflammatory markers. The magnetic resonance scan showed a ventral epidural abscess extending from C1-2 to the L5 level. She underwent urgent surgical decompression using a Nelaton catheter placed through an L4-L5 hemilaminectomy and threaded cephalad (40 cm); this resulted in a complete recovery.
