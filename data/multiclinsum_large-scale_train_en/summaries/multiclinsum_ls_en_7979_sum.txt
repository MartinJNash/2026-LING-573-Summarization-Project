We report bilateral triceps tendon avulsion injuries in a 49-year-old, right-hand dominant airline pilot. The patient fell while running and injured both his elbows. X-rays of both elbows showed displaced olecranon avulsion fractures, confirming triceps tendon injuries. Bone tendons were repaired with the suture anchor technique, and a solid repair was achieved. 3 months post-surgery, the post-operative progress of the patient was satisfied with full function, a range of motion of 5-150° on the left arm, and 0-150° on the right arm and ability to return to work with no restrictions.
