The patient was a 59-year-old female with 2 prior pregnancies and child births. She was examined by a local doctor for swelling of the genitalia, and a 1.8 × 1.0 cm large tumor was detected on the left side of the vaginal wall. A biopsy indicated leiomyosarcoma, and she was referred to our hospital. The tumor was resected, and histopathology of the resected sample confirmed the diagnosis of malignant melanoma based on a positive surgical margin. Additional courses of treatment included left inguinal sentinel lymph node biopsy using an isotope and extended vaginectomy. Although the sentinel node was negative, we performed a modified radical hysterectomy and left vaginectomy during the third operation because the surgical margin was positive. We could not confirm whether the lesion in the extracted sample was malignant, and the final diagnosis was primary malignant melanoma of the vagina T4bN0M0 Stage IIc (UICC 2009). Postoperative adjuvant therapy consisted of 6 cycles of DAV-Feron therapy (dacarbazine, ACNU, vincristine, IFN- β). After 5 months of postoperative adjuvant therapy, a 2 cm single lung metastasis was detected in the lower left lung. We performed a laparoscopic lower left lobectomy and are planning additional chemotherapy.
