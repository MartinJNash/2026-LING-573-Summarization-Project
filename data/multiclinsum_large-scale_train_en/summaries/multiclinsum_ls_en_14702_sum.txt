An 11-year-old girl presented outpatient for scoliosis evaluation and was found to have what appeared to be a right L4 peripheral nerve sheath tumor on MRI, causing dextroconvex scoliosis. She underwent a gross total resection via a retroperitoneal approach and was found to have what appeared to be an extraosseous, extradural, extra-spinal canal lumbar chordoma. Immunohistochemical features on surgical pathology were consistent with chordoma. The patient was referred to radiation oncology for adjuvant radiotherapy and pediatric hematology/oncology for recurrence monitoring.
