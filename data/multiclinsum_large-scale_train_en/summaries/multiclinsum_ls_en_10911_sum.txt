A 56-year-old man with history of hypertension presented with acute onset of altered mental status and right hand numbness. Initial vital signs were remarkable for bradycardia with heart rate 36/min. Physical exam was only significant for decreased sensation for light touch on right hand. Electrocardiogram showing marked sinus bradycardia. Initial computed tomography (CT) head did not show any acute abnormalities. On Day 2, patient complained of new weakness in left upper extremity. On exam, he had dysmetria, left upper extremity drift and cold to touch. Blood pressure was significantly different in both arms with 141/83 mmHg on right and 44/23 mmHg on left. He underwent immediate CT angiography chest showing Type 1 aortic dissection with extension into the brachiocephalic artery and right common carotid artery (RCCA) with thrombosis in RCCA. Patient was emergently taken for surgical repair of aortic dissection and resuspension of aortic valve.
