We report a case of a 3-year-old preschool girl from an endemic area for coccidioidomycosis who presented with pain and swelling on her left ankle. Initially, antibiotic treatment is administered for a suspected insect bite. However, her symptoms worsened and X-rays showed a lytic-like lesión. She was admitted to the hospital for biopsy and drainage surgery. The histopathological study confirms coccidioidomycosis osteomyelitis. She was started on long-term anti-fungal treatment. At 6-month follow-up, symptoms and signs of infection have been solved and X-ray image shows evidence of bone healing.
