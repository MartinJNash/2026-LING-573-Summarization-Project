He was a 25-year-old young man, with no known pathological history, examined as part of a physical fitness assessment and who presented with thoracic asymmetry. The clinical and radiological explorations made it possible to conclude to a Poland syndrome in its minor form without any other associated malformation. In addition, three other paternal uncles of the patient presented with the same clinical symptomatology. In the absence of a clear indication, the patient received no treatment.
