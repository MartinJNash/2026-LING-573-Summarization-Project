A 47-year-old man presented with miction pain and back pain. Abdominal CT revealed a huge prostate mass extending to the left retroperitoneum. Serum sIL-2R level was abnormally high (2896 U/mL), whereas PSA level was normal. HIV antigen and antibody tests were positive. The patient was diagnosed with DLBCL after a prostate biopsy. Systemic treatments were administered; however, the tumor was refractory, and the patient died 9 months after diagnosis.
