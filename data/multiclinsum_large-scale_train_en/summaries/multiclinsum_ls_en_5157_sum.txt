A 71-year-old woman presented to our institution with paroxysmal AF. The patient had a history of cholecystectomy and SO dysfunction. She had undergone CBA for AF. Immediately after the procedure, the patient developed epigastric pain. Computed tomography showed dilation of the intra- and extrahepatic bile ducts, with the diameter of the common bile duct measuring ∼15.6 mm. Blood tests on postoperative Day 1 revealed severely elevated aminotransferase levels (aspartate aminotransferase, 3156 U/L; alanine aminotransferase, 2084 U/L; lactate dehydrogenase, 2279 U/L; total bilirubin 1.7 mg/dL).
