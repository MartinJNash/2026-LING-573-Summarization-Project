We present a case of a 42-year-old male with sRV failure and TR in the context of TGA after atrial switch. Patient had progressive reduction in exercise capacity. Electrocardiogram showed a wide QRS complex (right bundle branch block configuration). Echocardiography showed significant TR and signs of electromechanical dyssynchrony of the failing sRV with severely reduced systolic function. He underwent heart catheterization and invasive haemodynamic evaluation to assess the potential benefit of CRT. During sequential atrial-sRV pacing, 20% increase in Dp/Dt was measured, suggesting that he would be a CRT responder. Concomitant angiography showed no baffle leakage nor obstructive coronary artery disease. Hybrid CRT-defibrillator implantation resulted in successful resynchronization and improved sRV function, reduced TR and better exercise capacity.
