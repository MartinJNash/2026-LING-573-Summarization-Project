A 53-year-old woman with hypertension, dyslipidaemia, and prediabetes presented to the Emergency Department with hypoxia, hyperglycaemia, lactic acidosis, severe left ventricular dysfunction, and pulmonary oedema followed by cardiogenic shock. Onset of symptoms was only few hours after an OGTT during which she had developed severe transient hypoglycaemia. Angiography was performed due to elevated troponin levels and showed a midventricular contraction pattern typical of takotsubo. This was subsequently confirmed by cardiac magnetic resonance imaging. The patient's condition improved during the first 36 h and she was discharged home on Day 7. A positive catecholamine test prompted readmission to the Endocrinology Unit, where computer tomography confirmed the diagnosis of phaeochromocytoma. An adrenalectomy was performed, and the diagnosis was verified histopathologically.
