We presented a 15-year-old male patient whose baseline electrocardiogram (ECG) was confused with multiple rhythms. Electrophysiological study results showed sinus rhythm with nonconducted APCs in bigeminal rhythm. Nonconducted APCs were blocked without H wave. Some APCs conducted to ventricle with longer AH interval and HV interval. When APCs were abolished by radiofrequency ablation, this patient was free from any arrhythmia during follow-up.
