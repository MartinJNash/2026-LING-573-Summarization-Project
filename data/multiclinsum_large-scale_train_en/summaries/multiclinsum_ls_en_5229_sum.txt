We describe here the first case in the literature of an INI1-deficient neck carcinoma without a primary tumor managed with surgical therapy and neck dissection in a young Caucasian woman of 29 years old, followed by chemotherapy before radiotherapy, with regional control after 18 months of follow-up. Histologic analysis showed an undifferentiated carcinoma without glandular or epidermoid differentiation. Biomolecular analysis of the tumor revealed a homozygous deletion of the SMARCB1 gene on RNA sequencing.
