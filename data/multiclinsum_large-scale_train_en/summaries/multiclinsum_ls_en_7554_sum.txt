A 62-year-old male presented after his cardiac resynchronization therapy-defibrillator (CRT-D) alerted him that his device was at an elective replacement interval (ERI). He underwent an uncomplicated generator exchange; however, 2 weeks later, a remote alert showed that his device was at ERI and all impedances were above the upper limit. Device interrogation the following day demonstrated that the new device was functioning appropriately and his home monitor had in fact paired with his old generator. He obtained a new home monitor, and subsequent remote transmissions have demonstrated that his device is functioning appropriately.
