This case is about a 33-year-old woman diagnosed with Salmonella enterica serovar Hvittingfoss (S. Hvittingfoss) bacteremia and endometritis six days post uterine aspiration in the context of a missed abortion. She had traveled to Indonesia two weeks prior to the positive blood and cervical culture. She never developed gastrointestinal symptoms but was found to carry S. Hvittingfoss in her stool sample. The patient was successfully treated with a seven-day course of iv ciprofloxacin.
