We report the case of a 72-year-old man with right atrial angiosarcoma who presented with bloody pericardial effusion due to tumor hemorrhaging. Emergency surgery was performed. The right atrial free wall and vena cava were reconstructed with pedicled autologous pericardium. The patient died 12 months later because of multiple organ failure due to metastasis. Autopsy showed a maintained right atrial chamber and only mild calcification.
