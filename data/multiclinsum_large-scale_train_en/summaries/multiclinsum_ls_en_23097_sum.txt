A 51-year-old male presented with a 1-month history of penis pain during erection. CT scan showed a soft tissue mass arising from the proximal part of the penis. We diagnosed it as penile sarcoma, performing local excision. The postoperative pathological result was moderately differentiated fibrosarcoma. 3 months later, CT scan showed the recurrence of the tumor, and multiple metastases. Although he received chemotherapy, he died 10 months after surgery.
