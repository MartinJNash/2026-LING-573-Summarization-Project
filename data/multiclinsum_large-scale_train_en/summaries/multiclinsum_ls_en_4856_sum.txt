A 79-year-old Caucasian male presented to the emergency department with history of weakness, anorexia, lethargy, and mood changes for 2 months. Abdominopelvic computed tomography showed an abscess in right iliac muscle. After percutaneous drainage of the abscess and 6 weeks antibiotic therapy, an appendectomy was done for the patient. Histopathologic findings revealed the involvement of the appendiceal wall by adenocarcinoma, most probably with gastrointestinal origin. Gastric cancer was confirmed later by upper endoscopy and pathologic report.
