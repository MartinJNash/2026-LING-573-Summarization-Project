Here, we report an interesting and rare case of EGPA in a 41 year-old male with negative anti-neutrophil cytoplasmic antibodies (ANCAs), which showed abundant pulmonary IgG4-positive plasma cells infiltration and markedly elevated serum IgG4 levels without extra-pulmonary lesions of IgG4-related disease. The clinical characteristics hesitated us whether the diagnosis as EGPA overlapping with IgG4-related lung disease should be concluded because of the absence of storiform fibrosis and obliterative phlebitis with lymphoplasmacytic infiltration. The patient's systemic symptoms, pulmonary lesions, blood eosinophils count / percentage, and serum IgG4 levels were significantly improved with immunosuppressive therapy.
