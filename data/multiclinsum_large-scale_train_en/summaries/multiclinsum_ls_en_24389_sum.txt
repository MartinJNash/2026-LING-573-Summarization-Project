We present a case of a 64-year-old patient admitted to the emergency room for jaundice with delayed ST-segment elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI). Physical examination revealed signs of right heart failure, which led us to associate jaundice with signs of acute liver failure secondary to right heart failure. Echocardiography confirmed the diagnosis of a ventricular septal rupture (VSR) with left-right shunt, and a significant dilation of the right ventricle. The patient underwent surgical closure of the VSR with fatal evolution.
