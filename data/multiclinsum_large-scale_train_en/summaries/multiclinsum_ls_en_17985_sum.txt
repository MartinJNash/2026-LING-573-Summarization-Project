A 58-year-old male with known human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) infection presented in the emergency department with a massive cervical tumour on the right side of the neck and a severe occipital pain. Contrast computed tomography demonstrated a pseudoaneurysm of the proximal right internal carotid artery (ICA). Open surgery of the ICA was performed with reconstruction of the posterior vessel wall, embolectomy of the ICA, and anterior pericardial patch reconstruction. After an uneventful postoperative course, the patient was readmitted 4 weeks later with a right retrobulbous haematoma caused by a carotid cavernous fistula. Therefore a coil-embolization of the fistula and finally of the distal right carotid artery was performed.
