A 2-month-old boy had diarrhea, hematochezia, and a rash, and was diagnosed as having a cow milk protein allergy (CMPA). He was given an amino acid-based formula, but there was no sign of improvement in his condition. The patient then had a skin biopsy and was diagnosed as having multisystem Langerhans cell histiocytosis (MS-LCH). The general condition of the child deteriorated after the first two doses of chemotherapy, and the child died.
