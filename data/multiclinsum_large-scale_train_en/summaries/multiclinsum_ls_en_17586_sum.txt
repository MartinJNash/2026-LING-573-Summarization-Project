A 73-year-old man who underwent robot-assisted radical prostatectomy noted acute abdominal pain and nausea on the 11th postoperative day. Computed tomography scans revealed dilated small bowel loops. Adhesive ileus was initially suspected, which was relieved with conservative management, including ileus tube insertion. However, his symptoms worsened. Thus, a laparotomy was performed. The camera port wound was reopened, and the repaired fascia and small intestine were found incarcerated into the peritoneal defects. These findings were consistent with Richter's hernia.
