Here, we present the case of a 91-year-old woman with acute pulmonary embolism, whose initial electrocardiogram showed a complete atrioventricular block. She presented with presyncope and an initial blood pressure of 77/63 mmHg. Echocardiography confirmed signs of right ventricular dysfunction. Catheter-directed thrombolysis and a temporary pacemaker insertion were carried out sequentially. The following day, electrocardiography showed sinus rhythm with a left bundle branch block.
