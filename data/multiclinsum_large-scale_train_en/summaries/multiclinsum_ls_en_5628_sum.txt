We report the case of a 60-year-old male patient with Primary Cutaneous Leiomyosarcoma (PCL) located in the left iliac region.His history is insignificant and he has no family or genetic history of leiomyosarcoma. The lesion was itchy without any other symptoms and existed 20 years before our evaluation.A biopsy from the nodule was performed and sent to the pathology department, where the section was stained with smooth muscle actin stain (SMA) and the result was positive.We referred the patient to a surgeon to excise the nodule. The lesion was excised with a 3cm safety margin, the eradication includes also the of the major iliac muscle.After one year of follow-up there was no metastasis nor recurrence.
