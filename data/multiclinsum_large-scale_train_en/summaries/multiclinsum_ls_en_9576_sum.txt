A 17-year-old female presented with 4 months history of progressive abdominal distension associated with abdominal pain, yellow discoloration of eyes, and occasional constipation. Abdominal CT-scan revealed a huge cystic mass in the right upper quadrant extending inferiorly to the right lumbar region. Complete excision of a type IA choledochal cyst was done plus cholecystectomy in addition to bilioenteric reconstruction. The patient recovered uneventfully.
