We present a 56-year-old man of Han ethnicity with a kidney transplant who suffered from coronavirus disease 2019 twice. For the first infection, the immunosuppressants were substituted by dexamethasone when the patient used Paxlovid, and everything went well. For the second time, tacrolimus at a reduced dose concomitant with Paxlovid caused severe diarrhea, inducing combined diabetic ketoacidosis and a hyperglycemic hyperosmolar state.
