A 51-year-old male presented with acute pancreatitis. He was hemodynamically unstable, had respiratory failure on admission and was treated in the intensive care unit (ICU). He recovered and left the ICU on day 13. Although his general condition improved, a computed tomography (CT) scan showed air and fluid in the left retroperitoneum. Gastrografin enema and CT scan showed extraluminal leakage in the descending colon and retroperitoneal drainage was performed on day 27. After drainage, there was continuous voluminous feculent discharge, and a loop ileostomy was performed on day 34. A repeat CT scan revealed ascites. A percutaneous catheter injected with contrast showed a duodenal fistula. After drainage, the fever resolved and the patient was discharged on hospital day 106.
