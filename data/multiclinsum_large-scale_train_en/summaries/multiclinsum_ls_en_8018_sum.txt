We report a case of a 57-year-old man who was presented with a 3-week history of fresh bleeding from the rectum. Colonoscopy showed a polypoidal mass arising from the ascending colon; biopsy showed clonal plasmacytosis and a primary colonic solitary EMP diagnosis was made after exclusion of multiple myeloma (MM). Accordingly, the patient underwent a right hemicolectomy, followed by 6 cycles of bortezomib, cyclophosphamide, and dexamethasone (VCD). The patient continued to be disease-free 30 months after the completion of his chemotherapy.
