We present the case of a 21-year old morbidly obese caucasian patient who underwent laparoscopic Roux-en-Y gastric bypass surgery and developed an early severe transverse colon obstruction due to compression of the transverse colon by the antecolic alimentary limb. Emergency revisional surgery showed a short and tense alimentary limb mesentery and possibly tight closure of Petersen's space contributing to the compression. Through opening of Petersen's space and mobilization of alimentary limb mesentery, decompression was achieved, and the patient fully recovered.
