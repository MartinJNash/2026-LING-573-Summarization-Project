A 72-year-old male presented to the emergency department with constipation and right upper quadrant pain for 3 months. Computed tomography scan of the abdomen demonstrated an ill-defined 7.2 × 4.9 cm hypodense lesion in the gallbladder fossa with extension into the liver. Histopathological and immunohistochemical analysis of the biopsy specimen confirmed the diagnosis of large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma of the gallbladder. He was started on palliative chemotherapy.
