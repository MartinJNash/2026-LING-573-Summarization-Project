A 15-year-old female presented to the emergency department with neurological symptoms. The patient exhibited confusion but maintained stable vital signs, and physical examinations were all normal. Abdominal ultrasound and axial brain computed tomography (CT) scan were both normal. The patient's neurological condition and laboratory test results suggested diagnoses of WD. After 2 days, the patient experienced sudden bilateral blindness. The patient's condition deteriorated rapidly, and was subsequently referred to the ICU.
