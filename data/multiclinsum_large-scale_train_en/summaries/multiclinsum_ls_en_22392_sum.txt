A 53-year-old woman suffered severe left painful ophthalmoplegia. A magnetic resonance image (MRI) revealed thickness of the left cavernous sinus (CS). Steroid was administrated under the diagnosis of THS, and the symptom transiently diminished. However, painful ophthalmoplegia fluctuated bilaterally after tapering the steroid. An MRI showed development of bilateral cavernous lesions associated with sphenoid sinusitis, pituitary abscess, and an aneurysm in the left C4 segment. Biopsy and drainage of the lesions were performed with an endoscopic transsphenoidal procedure. The histological examination showed nonspecific granulomatous inflammation. The methotrexate (MTX) was effective to reduce the CS and pituitary lesions; however, the aneurysm slightly increased and remained unchanged in size thereafter.
