A three year, 11 month old girl sustained a right displaced extension supracondylar fracture (ESF) of the humerus with comminution of the lateral column after an indoor fall. At surgery, fracture reduction showed multidirectional instability. Adequate reduction was achieved by applying longitudinal traction of the arm with partial elbow flexion and forearm supination. One percutaneous medial pin, followed by one lateral cross pin, was used to immobilize the fracture. Normal posterolateral new periosteal bone formation was seen on radiograph on the lateral side. At 5-year follow-up, she had full range of asymptomatic and symmetrical elbow motion.
