We report a case of hepatosplenic sarcoidosis in a 29-year-old female.

She was a patient with no significant medical history who was seen for repeated epistaxis. The clinical examination noted a nodular hepatomegaly associated with signs of portal hypertension and a splenomegaly. The erythrocyte sedimentation rate, alkaline phosphatase, serum angiotensin converting enzyme, aminotransferases were elevated. The histological examination of the spleen and liver biopsy noted a granulomatous inflammatory infiltration without cancer lesion and without caseous. This is consistent with sarcoidosis despite the absence of a PET scan. The main challenge remains the differential diagnosis with other granulomatoses. Corticosteroid therapy is the first-line treatment that allowed clinical and biological stability after splenectomy.
