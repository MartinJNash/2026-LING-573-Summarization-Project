27-year-old woman with a viral respiratory infection 2 weeks prior to the development of a neurological syndrome characterized by paresthesia, motor deficits, status epilepticus and acute encephalopathy, which progressed to a coma and showed diffuse hemorrhagic lesions in the cerebral white matter with demyelination and peripheral edema in magnetic resonance. Steroid treatment was initiated for 5 days with improvement of symptoms, although motor and sensory deficits persisted.
