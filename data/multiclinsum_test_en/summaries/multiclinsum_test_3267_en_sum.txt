A patient with a history of HIV was presented with exanthems in various regions of the body, with a greater concentration in the gluteal region, associated with cervical adenopathies and infectious proctitis. The sample of the dermatological lesions was taken for the molecular study of real-time polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR), which confirmed the diagnosis of VS. Infection with Treponema pallidum and Neisseria gonorrhoeae was identified by serology and culture of anal secretion, respectively. Specific antibiotic treatment for gonorrhea and syphilis was applied, and VS responded favorably to symptomatic and immunomodulatory therapy.
