We present the case of a 35-year-old patient with central and peripheral nervous system involvement and a recent diagnosis of thyroid cancer, who, in the face of a compatible clinical picture of meningoencephalomyelitis, the characteristic findings in magnetic resonance and after the exclusion of alternative diseases, finally arrived at the diagnosis by the positive determination of anti GFAP in CSF. She underwent surgical treatment and radioactive iodine treatment for her tumour and subsequently received corticosteroid treatment with partial improvement of the neurological signs and symptoms.
