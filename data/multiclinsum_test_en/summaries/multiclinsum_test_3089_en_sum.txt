Full term newborn whose mother used nocturnal NIV for SCCS. During the transition period, he presented desaturations associated with hypercapnia and respiratory acidosis, without pulmonary compromise. He progressed with severe hypoventilation during sleep, without respiratory effort, peripheral oxygen saturation (SpO2) < 80%, plus respiratory acidosis. In the daytime, he presented good respiratory effort and normal SpO2 without assistance. Continuous non-invasive positive airway pressure and oxygen therapy worsened his condition when he slept. The full NIV with nasal interface and 2-level positive pressure, inspiratory/expiratory pressure 14-16/4 cm H2O, normalized the SpO2 during sleep and arterial blood gases in the daytime. The PHOX2B gene sequencing confirmed the presence of a heterozygous pathogenic variant with the genotype 20/26. At 2 months of age, he was discharged while maintaining NIV with nasal interface and PEEP 0, achieving adequate neurodevelopment.
