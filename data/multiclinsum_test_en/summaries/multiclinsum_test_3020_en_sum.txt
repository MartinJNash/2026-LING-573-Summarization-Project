A patient with a history of high-grade urothelial carcinoma of the bladder with secondary ganglionic and bone involvement presented with a case of haematohaematuria, tenesmus and rectal pain one year after her oncological surgery. The magnetic resonance of the abdomen and pelvis demonstrated a solid rectal lesion 5 cm long that narrowed the lumen and passed through the peritoneum, 6 cm from the anal margin. The pathological anatomy of the lesion reported a urothelial metastasis at the level of the lower rectum in accordance with the patient's history.
