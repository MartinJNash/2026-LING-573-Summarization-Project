We present the case of a 59-year-old man who presented to the emergency department with a sharp, diffuse pain in the left lower limb caused by extensive obstructive clots in the arteries of the left lower limb. Since an atheromatous cause was unlikely after the lower limb angioscopy, a cardiac origin was suspected. Cardiac ultrasound did not show abnormalities, however, thoracic angiotomography revealed a free-floating thrombus in the aortic arch. The patient was treated surgically with a stent graft in the aorta without complications.
