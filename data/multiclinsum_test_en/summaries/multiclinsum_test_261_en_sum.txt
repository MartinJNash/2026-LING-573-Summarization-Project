A 64-year-old Japanese woman was brought to the emergency department of our hospital in a deep coma. Computed tomography of the head showed severe intraventricular hemorrhage with subarachnoid hemorrhage. Computed tomography angiography showed not only congenital left internal carotid artery agenesis, but also rete mirabile of the left posterior communicating artery, the left posterior cerebral artery, and the left anterior cerebral artery. This unilateral vessel anomaly complex may have contributed to the formation of a peripheral aneurysm arising from a perforating branch of the pericallosal artery, which ruptured. The patient underwent urgent bilateral external ventricular drainage, but deteriorated and was declared brain dead.
