A 75-year-old man experiencing abdominal pain and frequent vomiting was transferred to our hospital. His medical records presented history of diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidemia, and gastric cancer surgery. Computed tomography of the abdomen indicated duodenal dilatation, enlarged pancreas, and fluid retention, with no bile duct stones present. Minor bleeding and duodenal bezoar were endoscopically detected with esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD). He was diagnosed with severe acute pancreatitis caused by a bezoar and admitted to the intensive care unit. The duodenal bezoar was dissected and removed with three repetitions of EGD, and the patient was discharged without any complications.
