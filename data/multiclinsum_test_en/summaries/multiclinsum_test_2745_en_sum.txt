A 29-year-old female was diagnosed with coarctation of the aorta and a bicuspid aortic valve after birth and underwent surgery at 1 month. At 8 years of age, she underwent balloon dilatation for re-coarctation. At the age of 28 years, she was diagnosed with re-coarctation triggered by hypertension. She underwent balloon dilatation as her cardiac catheterization revealed a systolic pressure gradient of 40 mmHg. Pretreatment 4D flow MRI demonstrated helical flow in the ascending aorta and descending thoracic aorta and LV blood flow analysis revealed a decrease in LV kinetic energy during systole; these improved after treatment.
