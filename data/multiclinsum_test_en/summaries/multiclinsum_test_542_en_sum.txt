A 21-year-old man visited our hospital with scrotal swelling after a baseball struck his left testis. Magnetic resonance imaging and ultrasonography indicated a left tunica albuginea tear, and emergency surgery was performed. Primary closure of the tunica albuginea was impossible since a tight closure could cause secondary damage. A vascular pedicle flap was prepared by shaping the tunica vaginalis to replace the tunica albuginea. He was discharged 2 days postoperatively. Ultrasonography showed normal size and blood flow in the ruptured testis at the 2-week and 3-month follow-up.
