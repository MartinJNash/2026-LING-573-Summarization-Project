We report a 60-year-old man who presented with a progressively enlarging tongue for 6 months, causing dysphagia and a reduction in oral opening. A biopsy of the tongue was performed, which histopathologically exhibited homogeneous material similar to eosinophilic amyloid. Congo red staining showed amyloid material with red dye under light microscopy and apple green birefringence under polarized light. Bone marrow biopsy showed 30% plasma cells, allowing a definitive diagnosis of soft tissue amyloidosis. Although a benign lesion, localized amyloidosis should be differentiated from systemic forms.
