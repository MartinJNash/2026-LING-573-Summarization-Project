A 67-year-old female patient with a history of stage IV double-hit DLBCL in remission for 3 years presented with acute-onset heart failure. Nuclear medicine PET/CT revealed a massive poorly defined right lower anterior mediastinal mass extending into the entire cardiac base and right ventricular myocardium, with cardiophrenic and retroperitoneal adenopathy. Vital signs and laboratory test results were significant for a heart rate of 56 beats per min (bpm) and elevated brain natriuretic peptide. Electrocardiogram was significant for a complete heart block, maintained by a junctional escape rhythm. Biopsies of the mass revealed recurrence of DLBCL. The patient was treated with diuretics and later started on RICE chemotherapy.
