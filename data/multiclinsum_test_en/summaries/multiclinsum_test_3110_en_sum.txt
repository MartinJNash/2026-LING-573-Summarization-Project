The clinical case of a 12-year-old boy, native of the district of Napo in the city of Iquitos in the Peruvian Amazon, who presented edema and increased volume of the face due to the bite of a snake of the genus Bothrops in the lateral face of the right leg, was subsequently hospitalized and was diagnosed with severe ophidiasis complicated with edema of the face. The patient received eight vials of antivenin, antibiotics and analgesics. Finally, after eight days of hospitalization, he had a favorable evolution and recovery and was discharged.
