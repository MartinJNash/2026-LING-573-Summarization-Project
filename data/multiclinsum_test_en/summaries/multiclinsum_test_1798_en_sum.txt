A 48-year-old female patient with a history of pericardial effusion and pneumonia was admitted to our hospital with progressive dyspnoea on exertion. Echocardiography demonstrated recurrence of pericardial effusion with 'swinging heart'. Further investigation by computed tomography, cardiac magnetic resonance imaging and coronary angiography revealed a hypervascular pericardial mass with typical 'tumour blush' after contrast injection. The tumour could be resected in toto by open heart surgery, and histological evaluation confirmed the diagnosis of a pericardial capillary haemangioma. There were no signs of recurrence of neither the pericardial effusion nor the tumour during follow-up.
