A 59-year-old male with a 17-year history of type 2 diabetes mellitus underwent fundus fluorescein angiography (FA) following vitrectomy for vitreous hemorrhage secondary to PDR. His visual acuity at the time of FA was 6/6 in both eyes. The examination focused on identifying neovascularization elsewhere (NVE) and analysing leakage patterns. FA revealed active NVEs in the left eye with late-phase leakage nasally and inferiorly to the optic disc, while the right eye exhibited a distinctive SSL pattern from residual proliferation. Interestingly, the leakage subsided in the late phase, leaving only staining of the residual fibrous tissue.
