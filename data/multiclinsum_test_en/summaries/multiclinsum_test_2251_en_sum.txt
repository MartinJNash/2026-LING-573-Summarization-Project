We present the case of a 29-year-old non-alcoholic lady who presented to our hospital with a history of two months of progressive, painless abdominal distension and progressively increasing yellowish discoloration of the eyes. Physical examination, laboratory investigations, and imaging tests led to a diagnosis of multiple metastases from breast carcinoma to the liver leading to portal hypertension after exclusion of other causes of portal hypertension. However, after three weeks of presentation to the hospital, the patient died before any therapeutic measures were initiated to address breast carcinoma.
