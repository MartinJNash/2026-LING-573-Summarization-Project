We report the case of a 69-year-old man who presented to Internal Medicine for a general condition associated with lower back pain. His main comorbidities were a HIV infection and a femoro-femoral bypass for obliterative arteritis of the lower limbs. A CT scan revealed an abscessed spondylodiscitis L3-L4, but all infectious samples with prolonged blood cultures were negative. A transthoracic ultrasound scan for infectious endocarditis was normal. A PET scan detected a vertebral hypermetabolism L3-L4 associated with a significant hypermetabolism of the femoro-femoral bypass. The serology for C. burnetii was in favour of a chronic Q fever. The therapeutic management required a multidisciplinary medical-surgical discussion. Three months later, the serology was reduced by one titre and stabilised at this titre at 6 months and 1 year.
