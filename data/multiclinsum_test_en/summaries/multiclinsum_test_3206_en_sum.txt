We report the case of a 32-year-old multiparous woman with chronic hypertension and chronic renal disease with 10 years of evolution and difficult control, with diagnosis of bicoronic/biamniotic twin pregnancy, four months after the start of haemodialysis. The pregnancy was monitored by a multidisciplinary team, the haemodialysis sessions were well tolerated clinically and haemodynamically, with good tensional and analytical control, as well as adequate development of both foetuses. She underwent a caesarean section at 34 weeks and six days due to the start of labour, and two boys were born with adequate weight for the gestational age.
