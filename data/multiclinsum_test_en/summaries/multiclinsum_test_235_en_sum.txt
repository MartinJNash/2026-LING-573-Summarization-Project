A 44-year-old female had previously undergone surgery for resection of a malignant melanoma in the lower right leg. Four years later, hepatic metastases became apparent, and transcatheter arterial embolization (TAE) was performed. Then she underwent treatment with vemurafenib. The size of the hepatic metastases markedly decreased. Two months later, they enlarged rapidly and ruptured, requiring emergency TAE. However, the patient developed hemorrhagic shock and died of renewed intra-abdominal bleeding on the 26th postoperative day.
