We report a 36-year-old woman who presented with fever and severe neutropenia and was found to have AML/Non M3. While receiving her initial chemotherapy treatment, she encountered a rare fungal infection (Saprochaete capitata) that spread throughout her lungs, stomach, spleen, liver, and kidneys, presenting difficulties in both diagnosing and treatment. In addition to being treated with both voriconazole and amphotericin B, the patient underwent surgery to remove the infection source, resulting in a cure.
