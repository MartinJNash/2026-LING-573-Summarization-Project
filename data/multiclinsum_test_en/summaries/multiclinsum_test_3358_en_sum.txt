We present the case of a young healthy woman who consulted for left peripheral facial palsy associated with fever, dry cough, dyspnoea and asthenia of two weeks evolution. Physical examination showed hypoesthesia in left dermatomes D6 to D12 and bilateral galactorrhea. In the laboratory she presented negative viral serologies, erythro-sedimentation, antinuclear antibody titers, elevated prolactin and thyrotropin, with positive anti-peroxidase antibodies. Computed tomography showed multiple cervical, mediastinal and hilar bilateral adenopathies, without pulmonary parenchymal involvement. The cerebrospinal fluid culture was negative for common germs, mycobacteria (Xpert MTB/RIF), and the cytology did not show atypia. Magnetic resonance imaging with intravenous contrast of the brain was performed without pathological findings and of the spine with alteration of the centromedullary signal from D6 to D9 of almost the entire thickness of the cord, with reinforcement with intravenous contrast. During the hospital stay she recovered the sensitivity in the left trunk and did not repeat febrile or coughing episodes. Mediastinoscopy with ganglion biopsy was performed with pathological anatomy with the presence of numerous non-caseifying granulomas compatible with sarcoidosis. She was classified as probable neurosarcoidosis and initiated treatment with corticosteroids with improvement of the remaining neurological symptoms, and a magnetic resonance was performed three months later, where the signal alteration was limited from D7 to D8.
