A 48-year-old man presented with the right groin pain after activity. A cystic lesion was found on magnetic resonance imaging. Under arthroscopic view, a cystic mass was identified between the TAL and ligamentum teres that discharged yellowish viscous liquid after puncture. The remaining lesion was resected in its entirety. A diagnosis of ganglion cyst was consistent with the histological findings. The patient has had no recurrence on magnetic resonance imaging as of 6 years postoperatively and had no complaints at the 6-year follow-up visit.
