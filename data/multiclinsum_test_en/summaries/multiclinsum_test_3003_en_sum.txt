A 67-year-old male presented with high fever, cough, and shortness of breath. He had no significant medical history. On examination, he was febrile, tachypneic, and tachycardic, with right-sided lung crackles. Lab tests showed elevated inflammatory markers and impaired kidney function. Chest radiography revealed right upper lobe consolidation, confirming pneumonia and AKI. He was treated with fluids, antibiotics, and supportive care, but his condition worsened, requiring intensive care unit (ICU). In the ICU, dark urine and elevated creatine kinase confirmed rhabdomyolysis. After aggressive fluid therapy and antibiotics the patient improved over six days and was transferred to the ward. By day 10, he fully recovered and was discharged with follow-up.
