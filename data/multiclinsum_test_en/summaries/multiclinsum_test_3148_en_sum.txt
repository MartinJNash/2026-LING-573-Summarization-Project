A 53-year-old patient was admitted to our hospital with the main complaints of diffuse abdominal pain with vomiting in the context of fever and physical asthenia. Painful hepatomegaly with jaundice was detected, as well as a non-specific inflammatory biological syndrome. An initial abdominal-pelvic tomodensitometry revealed multifocal hepatic abscesses. In the face of initial therapeutic failure with a combination of parenteral antibiotics and drainage of the abscesses, a second abdominal tomodensitometry revealed a foreign body between the antral and the segment I of the liver. A laparotomy was performed with the release of a peritoneal fluid of nearly 200 ml. A fish bone of about 5 cm in length was extracted by laparotomy, followed by a gastric suture with omentoplasty, a peritoneal wash and a drainage. A symptomatic adjuvant treatment was initiated, including a proton pump inhibitor (pantoprazole). The patient also received transfusion support due to anaemia. Antibiotics were continued for a total of 2 weeks after surgery. The evolution was favourable with a control imaging at 3 months, showing a complete resorption of the hepatic abscesses.
