A 40-year-old woman with a history of late-onset allergic rhinitis presented with a 2-week history of numbness in her right leg, along with multiple erythematous papules and vesicles, some with shallow erosions, located on the forehead and left ear. She also experienced fever, progressive dyspnea, and hemoptysis. She was diagnosed with pneumonitis, alveolar hemorrhage, and mononeuritis of the right leg. Laboratory findings revealed leukocytosis with eosinophilia, and the anti-myeloperoxidase antibody was positive. Histopathological examination of the bullous lesion on the forehead showed intraepidermal separation with necrotic keratinocytes and prominent eosinophil infiltration, along with focal leukocytoclastic vasculitis. The patient was diagnosed with EGPA and started on intravenous steroids and cyclophosphamide. EGPA is a rare disease characterized by multiorgan vasculitis, asthma, and granulomatous eosinophilic inflammation, which are its key hallmarks. While cutaneous involvement is common, bullous vasculitis is rarely observed on the forehead and ear.
