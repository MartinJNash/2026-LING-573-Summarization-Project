A 29-year-old woman presented with acute heart failure 2 weeks' postpartum and was found to have acute MR due to a flail leaflet caused by papillary muscle rupture. She proceeded to emergency surgery with mitral valve (MV) replacement and the histology revealed evidence of chronic Libman-Sacks endocarditis and papillary muscle infarction with thrombi in the intramyocardial arteries.
