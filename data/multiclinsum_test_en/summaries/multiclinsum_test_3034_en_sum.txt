We report a case of a newborn sent from secondary care with a diagnosis of undiagnosed prenatally duodenal obstruction, which resulted in duodenal atresia by annular pancreas and intestinal atresia type IIIb, according to Grosfeld classification. The presence of duodenal atresia with intestinal atresia type IIIb is an extremely rare condition and even more so associated with annular pancreas. These cases are a challenge given the short length of the small intestine and the consequent need for total parenteral nutrition for a prolonged period.
