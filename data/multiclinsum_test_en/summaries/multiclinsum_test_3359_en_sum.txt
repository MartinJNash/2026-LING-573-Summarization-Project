We present the case of a 67-year-old woman with no history of the above, who presented with fever, abdominal pain and jaundice. Magnetic resonance imaging of the abdomen showed a stony image in the common bile duct with dilation of the bile duct. She required endoscopic drainage of the biliary tract. Direct microscopic examination of the bile fluid showed yeast and Gram-negative bacilli, and the culture isolated Klebsiella pneumoniae producing extended spectrum beta-lactamase (ESBL) and Candida glabrata. The patient completed the antibiotic treatment with piperacillin tazobactam and anidulafungin with good evolution.
