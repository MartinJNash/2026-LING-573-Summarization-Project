A 45-year-old male patient with a four-month persistent cough accompanied by fever and significant weight loss presented. A chest CT scan showed idiopathic pneumonia and subsequent studies identified a positive viral load for cytomegalovirus (CMV) in the bronchoalveolar lavage. A transbronchial biopsy confirmed intranuclear basophilic inclusions indicative of CMV infection. The immunoglobulin values were: IgA < 0.13, IgG < 3 and IgM < 0.25 g/L. A bone marrow biopsy revealed an 80% increase in the number of cells, without morphological alterations. Complementary studies for agammaglobulinemia were indicated.
