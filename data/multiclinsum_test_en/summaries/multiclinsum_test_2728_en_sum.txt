We report a case of a 64-year-old man with a history of parathyroid adenoma who developed a pancreatic retroperitoneal paraganglioma. Despite having laboratory evidence of excess circulating catecholamines, the patient's only presenting symptom was hip pain. The patient underwent resection, and histologic findings were consistent with paraganglioma with lymph node metastasis. Genetic testing revealed a variant of uncertain significance within the RET gene
