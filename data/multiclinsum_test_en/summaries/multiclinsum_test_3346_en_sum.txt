A 41-year-old Inuit female complaining of blurred vision was referred to our eye department. There had been no sign of diabetic retinopathy during diabetic eye screening and the patient had been treated for tuberculosis in 2010. Telemedical assessment was suspicious for vitritis or vitreous hemorrhage in the right eye, and the patient was flown from Greenland to Denmark, where examination revealed mild vitritis in the right eye, vitreous hemorrhage in the left eye and retinal neovascularization in both eyes. Fundus fluorescein angiography showed vessel leakage and areas of retinal non-perfusion. There was left epiretinal membrane with retinal thickening on macular optical coherence tomography.
