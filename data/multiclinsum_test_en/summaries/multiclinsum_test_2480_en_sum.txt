We report a case of a 74-year-old male with progressively painful unilateral proptosis. Imaging revealed the presence of an orbital mass compatible with a thrombosed orbital varix of the inferior ophthalmic vein in the left inferior intraconal space. The patient was medically managed. On a follow-up outpatient clinic visit, he demonstrated remarkable clinical recovery and denied experiencing any symptoms. Follow-up computed tomography scan showed a stable mass with decreased proptosis in the left orbit consistent with the previously diagnosed orbital varix. One-year follow-up orbital magnetic resonance imaging without contrast showed slight increase in the intraconal mass.
