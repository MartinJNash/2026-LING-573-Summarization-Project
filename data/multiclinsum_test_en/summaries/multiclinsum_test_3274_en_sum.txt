A 62-year-old female patient with hypertension, rheumatoid arthritis and osteoporosis treated with Bonames® presented with exposed bone on the alveolar margin of quadrant III after an extraction. Under informed consent, a minimally invasive surgical procedure was performed with a piezotome® electric instrument. Histopathological analysis revealed necrotic bone tissue with chronic inflammatory infiltrate. Treatment included Blue®M teeth&bone once daily for 30 days, topical application of Blue®M gel every morning for 15 days, and 808 nm diode laser FBM (Therapy-DMC®). Laser sessions were daily until day 3, and then twice weekly for four weeks. The patient also rinsed her mouth with Blue®M every night for 30 days. Initially, the patient reported a pain of 3/10 on the visual analogue scale, which decreased to 0/10 at the end of treatment. Clinically, healing of the soft tissue was observed at 30 days and 60 days, and a hyperdense spongiform bone with well-formed cortical bone and no alterations was observed on the tomography. Finally, this case suggests that the combination of FBM with topical active oxygen therapy and lactoferrin, together with a minimally invasive surgical procedure, is promising for the management of MRONJ.
