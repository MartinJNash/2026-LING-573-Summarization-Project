A 62-year-old man with a history of diabetes mellitus was admitted to our center with meningeal syndrome. Gram-negative bacilli were isolated in blood cultures and cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) 24 hours after admission. They were later identified as Klebsiella pneumoniae. The presence of a hepatic abscess was identified by computed tomography. Despite early antibiotic treatment, the patient developed dorsal pain that prevented him from moving and right hemiparesis. An MRI of the spine identified increased signal in the central region of the spinal cord consistent with myelitis, for which he received treatment with methylprednisolone 1 g/day for 5 consecutive days. The patient developed favourably.
