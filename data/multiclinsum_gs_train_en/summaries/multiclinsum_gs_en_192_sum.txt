The case of a female adolescent patient who, after conservative treatment with a splint for a Peterson type I fisar fracture in the radius, suffered acute deficit of extension of the thumb six weeks later, diagnosed by clinical and radiological examination of a spontaneous rupture of the extensor pollicis longus. She was treated by transfer of the extensor proprius of the index finger (EPI) with satisfactory results and recovery of her usual activity one month postoperatively.
