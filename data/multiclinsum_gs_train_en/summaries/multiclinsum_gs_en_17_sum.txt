The authors report a 70-year-old man with diabetes renal disease who underwent a renal transplant (KT) in August 2014 with good clinical evolution. No previous CMV infection or episodes of acute rejection were reported. Five years after the transplant, he was admitted due to a reduced visual acuity of the left eye with seven days of evolution with associated hyperemia, without exudate. The ophthalmologic evaluation was compatible with acute retinal necrosis and presumably associated with CMV infection. He had a progressive improvement after the initiation of ganciclovir.
