We present the case of a patient with pharmacoresistant focal atonic seizures in the left upper limb. A brain MRI was performed with a diagnosis of parietal cortical dysplasia, and was monitored with video EEG of the scalp and then with deep EEG. The epileptogenic area and its relationship with eloquent areas were defined, and a surgical resection of the lesion was performed, achieving complete control of the seizures.
