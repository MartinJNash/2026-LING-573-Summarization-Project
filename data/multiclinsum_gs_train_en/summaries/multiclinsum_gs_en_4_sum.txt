We present the case of a 67-year-old man who presented with a subacute gait disturbance and urinary incontinence. Neurologic examination revealed an inability to stand and a deterioration of memory. Cranial imaging showed obstructive hydrocephalus with areas of gliosis in the cerebellar peduncles. Endoscopic treatment of the hydrocephalus was performed, and cerebrospinal fluid samples were taken that showed growth of Cryptococcus neoformans. The patient improved with endoscopic treatment and after completing ten weeks of intravenous antifungal therapy with liposomal amphotericin B and fluconazole.
