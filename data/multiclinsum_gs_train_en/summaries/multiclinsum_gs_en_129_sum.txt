We report the clinical case of a 37-year-old female patient who was admitted as an emergency for inspiratory dyspnoea in a picture of asthenia and anorexia. The cervical magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) objectified a process that was obstructing almost the entire pharyngeal lumen. The anatomopathological, immunohistochemical and cytogenetic study concluded that it was an extraskeletal Ewing sarcoma. The extension assessment was negative. The patient received chemotherapy.
