We present the case of a MSM with HIV infection on antiretroviral therapy, recurrent genital herpes and treated latent syphilis, with no recent travel abroad, with fistulized inguinal adenopathies to skin, associated with a painful genital ulcer, without anorectal or systemic symptoms, refractory to multiple antibacterial and antiviral treatments. The PCR study of the secretion of the fistula was positive for C. trachomatis. He was treated with doxycycline for six weeks, with a good clinical response, without complications.
