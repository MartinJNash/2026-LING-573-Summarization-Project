A case of a patient with SCOT deficiency, with a first atypical episode accompanied by hyperglycemia, with 4 subsequent episodes with classic manifestations of the disease, who presented a biochemical pattern of permanent ketonuria with marked elevation of ketone bodies (acetoacetate, 3 beta-hydroxybutyrate) in a study of urinary organic acids by gas chromatography and mass spectrometry, together with a clinical picture that granted the diagnosis. Maintenance therapy was initiated with a characteristic feeding plan; an adequate response to treatment was demonstrated, and an absence of permanent ketosis was inferred.
