A 86-yr-old man was referred for transfemoral aortic valve implantation. Transthoracic echocardiography revealed a severe stenosis (mean gradient: 58 mmHg, aortic valve area: 0.4 cm2), and after multidisciplinary discussion, the risk of surgery was judged too high (logistic Euroscore: 51%), and the patient was proposed for a transfemoral aortic valve implantation (TAVI). On arrival in the operating room, the patient, fully conscious, was noted to have Cheyne–Stokes breathing (CSB), which persisted after 40% oxygen administration. TAVI procedure was successful, and the CSB pattern was interrupted within 8 s.
