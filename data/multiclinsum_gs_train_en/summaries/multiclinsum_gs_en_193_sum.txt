An 18-year-old female patient presented at our clinic with complaints of multiple palpable lumps in her breasts bilaterally. On physical examination the patient had also multiple pigmented lentiginous lesions on her face, body and her sclerae, blue nevi on her trunk and upper extremities and a round moon-shaped face. The diagnosis of Carney syndrome was decided upon imaging, biopsies and genetic analysis. The patient underwent a bilateral mastectomy as a prophylactic treatment plan with tissue expanders' placement.
