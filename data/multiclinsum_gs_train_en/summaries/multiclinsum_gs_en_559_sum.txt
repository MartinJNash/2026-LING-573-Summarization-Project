We present the case of a 34-year-old male patient with a previous diagnosis of Marfan syndrome who was admitted for acute aortic insufficiency secondary to aneurysmal dilation of the ascending thoracic aorta. In the postoperative period, post-cardio-tomy cardiogenic shock was documented, so circulatory support was initiated with peripheral venoarterial ECMO, which developed hypoxemia due to bacterial pneumonia and data compatible with north-south syndrome.
