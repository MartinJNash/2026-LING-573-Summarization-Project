We report the case of a 39-year-old woman who received a renal transplant and was treated with prednisone and tacrolimus. After initiation of immunosuppressive therapy, she developed a condition characterized by warty papules and plaques in the inguinal region. A skin biopsy was performed and was consistent with epidermodysplasia verruciformis. It was decided to adjust the immunosuppressive therapy to everolimus, which resulted in a decrease in the lesions.
