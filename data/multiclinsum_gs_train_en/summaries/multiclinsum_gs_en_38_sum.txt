A 1-day-old Black Bantu African female baby was admitted to the neonatal unit owing to multiple congenital anomalies. The mother did not seek any medical attention until her early third trimester when she had an abruptio placenta. The mother has never received antenatal folic acid supplementation. The baby had microcephaly, low-set ears, right cleft palate and lip, micrognathia, contracture, and shorter limbs. A skeletal X-ray showed femoral hypoplasia. We report this rare case and highlight the challenges in diagnosis and treatment.
