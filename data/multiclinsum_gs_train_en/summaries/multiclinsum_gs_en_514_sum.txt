We hereby report the case of an 86-year-old male patient who presented with a 3-week-long nonpainful priapism. A penile Doppler ultrasound and a chest and abdominal CT scan were performed, showing a left hilar lung mass as well as lesions in the liver, the adrenal glands, the pancreas, bone structures, and the penis. Penile metastasis is associated with a poor prognosis because of the frequent disseminated malignant lesions in other sites.
