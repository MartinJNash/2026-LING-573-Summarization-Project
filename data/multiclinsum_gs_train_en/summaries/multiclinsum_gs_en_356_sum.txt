In this manuscript, we report a case of a 40-year-old female patient presenting with syncope that progressed to torsades de pointes and then ventricular fibrillation. Coronary angiography did not show coronary lesions. Ventriculography, transthoracic echocardiography and cardiac MRI were compatible with mid-ventricular Takotsubo cardiomyopathy. The ECG showed a long QTc interval with inverted T wave. During the first 48 hours, she had multiple episodes of non-sustained polymorphic ventricular tachycardia. The patient progressed with a decrease in QTc interval and regression of the segmental alterations. At the six-month follow-up, the patient was asymptomatic, with no new episodes.
