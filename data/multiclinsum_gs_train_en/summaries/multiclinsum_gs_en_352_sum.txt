Herein we present the case of a 35-year-old nulliparous woman in Northern Tanzania with a 10-year history of infertility. On presentation this patient reported a three-year history of a cyclical aching pain associated with an umbilical mass. An abdominal MRI revealed an ill-defined, enhancing mass measuring 3 × 4 × 6 cm located along the right anterior abdominal wall. The mass was connected to a sinus tract extending inferiorly to the suprapubic region but showed no communication with the peritoneal cavity, suggestive of endometriosis. Furthermore, bilateral adnexal lesions demonstrated hyperintense signals with focal hypointense areas and variable restrictions, consistent with bilateral ovarian endometriomas. The patient underwent excision of the umbilical mass, and histopathological examination confirmed the diagnosis of primary umbilical endometriosis. Despite her stable condition following management, she did not conceive over the course of the following year.
