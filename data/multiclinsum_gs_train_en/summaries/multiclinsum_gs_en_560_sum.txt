30-year-old male patient who presented with a localized dermatosis in the right upper extremity, unilateral, asymmetric, monomorphic in appearance, consisting of artificial macula, caused by red ink (tattoo), with development of papular lesions in the original line, pruritic, reported time of evolution of one month. The histopathological finding corresponds to a reaction to a foreign body with partial response to low-potency topical steroids.
