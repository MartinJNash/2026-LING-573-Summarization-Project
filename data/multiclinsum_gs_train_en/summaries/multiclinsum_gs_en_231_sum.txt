65-year-old woman with right NT (initially in V2-V3) since 2004 with regular control with carboxamides. Seventeen years later, paroxysms worsened in V2-V3, also appeared in V1 and in the territory of the right glossopharyngeal nerve (right ear and fossa amygdala when speaking and swallowing). The brain MRI showed significant arterial contact between the superior cerebellar artery (SCA) with origin of the right cranial pair V and the anterior inferior cerebellar artery (AICA) with origin of the right lower cranial pairs. Both cranial pairs were examined by MRI at the same surgical time by retrosigmoid craniotomy, and the release of the cranial pair V, in intimate contact with the SCA, and the cranial pair IX in contact with the right AICA was performed, for which teflon was placed between them. The patient had an immediate resolution of trigeminal paroxysms and a considerable improvement in intensity and frequency of glossopharyngeal paroxysms. Two years after the intervention, neuromodulatory treatment was continued with good response.
