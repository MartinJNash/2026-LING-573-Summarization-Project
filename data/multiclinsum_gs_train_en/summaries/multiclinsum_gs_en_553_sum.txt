An 83-year-old male with amyloid and valvular cardiomyopathy underwent dual-chamber pacemaker implantation for third-degree atrioventricular block following transcatheter aortic valve implantation. The active-fixation atrial lead was positioned on the right atrial free wall. Six hours post-implantation, the patient developed haemodynamic instability. Echocardiography revealed a small pericardial effusion, while pacemaker interrogation showed an increased atrial pacing threshold. Chest imaging demonstrated a moderate right haemothorax. Initially, conservative management was recommended. However, the patient's condition deteriorated, progressing to cardiac arrest requiring brief cardiopulmonary resuscitation. Emergency sternotomy revealed a large right haemothorax and laceration of the right upper pulmonary vein in contact with the atrial lead screw. The laceration was repaired, and the atrial lead was repositioned. The patient recovered well and was discharged 5 days after surgery.
