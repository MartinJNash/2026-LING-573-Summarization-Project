16-year-old male presenting after seeing several specialists with sleep paralysis during naps, which causes fear and occasional dropping of objects from hands (diagnosed as possible myoclonus). In the history, we were surprised by the presence of sleep paralysis immediately after the onset of naps and, in the directed history, these sudden movements caused by emotions were consistent with cataplexy, so we performed a nocturnal polysomnographic study and a multiple sleep latency test. With the evolution, hypnopompic hallucinations and fragmented night sleep appeared, as well as occasional daytime somnolence (the typical symptomatological tetrad of narcolepsy with cataplexy of type 1 was completed).
