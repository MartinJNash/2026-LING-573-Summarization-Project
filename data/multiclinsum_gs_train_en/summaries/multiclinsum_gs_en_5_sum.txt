A 64-year-old woman with a confirmed diagnosis of disseminated cryptococcosis secondary to tofacitinib use is presented. Other causes of immunosuppression, such as human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) infection, were ruled out. She had been diagnosed with rheumatoid arthritis three years earlier and was on a biologic agent that inhibits JAK enzymes. Very few cases of pulmonary and meningeal cryptococcosis have been reported in this type of patient.
