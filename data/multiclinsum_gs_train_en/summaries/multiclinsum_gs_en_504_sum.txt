A teenager who developed pseudohyponatraemia and hypertriglyceridaemia during treatment for non-Hodgkin lymphoma (NHL) was suspected to have a drug reaction (ADR). This suspicion of ADR was evaluated according to the modified causality algorithm (Karch and Lasagna), resulting in a "definitive" ADR for the association of L-asp and corticosteroids. He received treatment with a low-fat diet and lipid-modifying medicines. L-asp and prednisone were not discontinued due to the end of the indication, according to the protocol. The hypertriglyceridaemia recovered without complications after 14 days of treatment.
