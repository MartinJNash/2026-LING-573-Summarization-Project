A 67-year-old woman with dilated cardiomyopathy underwent M-TEER for severe functional MR. Although resistance was noted during initial device preparation and loading, limited functional testing appeared normal and the procedure was continued. During clip manipulation in the left atrium, mechanical resistance in the arm positioner led to unexpected detachment of the MitraClip, connected only by the gripper line. Through careful traction under echocardiographic and fluoroscopic guidance, we successfully retrieved the clip percutaneously. The procedure was completed with new MitraClip systems, achieving mild residual MR. The patient's symptoms improved from New York Heart Association Classes II and III to I, with sustained improvement during 4.5 years of follow-up.
