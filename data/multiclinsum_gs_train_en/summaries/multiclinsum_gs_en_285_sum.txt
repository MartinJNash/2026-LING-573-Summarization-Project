A 19-year-old man with no significant history was admitted to hospital with a painful left scrotal mass that had been present for 8 months. He had undergone an orchidectomy and the pathological examination was in favour of epididymal tuberculosis. A radiological examination had shown other sites of infection: lymph nodes, lungs, parietal and osteoarticular. An anti-tuberculosis treatment was initiated. However, in the 4th month of treatment, the patient had convulsive seizures. A brain MRI was performed and concluded that there were brain tuberculomas. The anti-tuberculosis treatment was continued in association with an anticonvulsant with good clinical and radiological evolution.
