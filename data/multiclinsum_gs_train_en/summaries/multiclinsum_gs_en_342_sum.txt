A patient with a history of single ventricle due to tricuspid atresia, double left ventricular outflow tract and pulmonary artery ring hypoplasia was presented. The patient was treated with Glenn surgery at 7 years of age and, at 16, underwent Fontan surgery with fenestration. One month after the surgery, she presented severe hemoptysis without adequate response to medical management. Percutaneous closure of the aortopulmonary fistulae was performed with coils, with favorable evolution, without recurrence of hemoptysis.
