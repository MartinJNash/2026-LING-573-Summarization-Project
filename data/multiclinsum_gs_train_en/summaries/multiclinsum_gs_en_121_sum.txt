Patient: Female, 25

Final Diagnosis: Peripartum cardiomyopathy

Symptoms: Fatigue • orthopnoea • pulmonary edema • tachycardia

Medication: —

Clinical Procedure: —

We report the case of a 25-year-old woman with a severe course of PPCM with left ventricle ejection fraction of 25–30%, complicated by ventricular arrhythmia and postpartum thyroiditis. As no traditional risk factors of PPCM were identified, the patient was referred for genetic testing. Next-generation sequencing revealed a novel titin gene-truncating mutation NM_001267550: p.Leu23499fs/c.70497_40498insT in the proband as well as in her mother.

In the patient, a very late recovery >12 months postpartum was observed, which required long-term medical treatment with bromocriptine.
