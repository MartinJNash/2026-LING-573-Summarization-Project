A young immunocompetent patient with a history of pulmonary tuberculosis presented to the hospital with a clinical picture of subacute persistent fever, weight loss, dyspnoea and abolition of the vesicular murmur. A chest tomography showed an extensive empyema in the left hemithorax. Samples were taken for the detection of common germs and a thoracic drainage tube was placed and antibiotic therapy was initiated. The MALDI-TOF MS test identified Parvimonas micra, an anaerobic bacterium, a commensal of the oral flora, associated with severe periodontitis, which is rarely reported in pleural empyema, especially in immunocompetent people. The dental evaluation led to the diagnosis of gingivitis and pericoronaritis of the third molar. The patient had a favourable evolution.
