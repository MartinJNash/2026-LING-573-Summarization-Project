28-year-old man with a 6-month history of intermittent jaundice, pruritus and coluria. A magnetic resonance imaging of the pancreatic and biliary tract, a PET-CT scan and an endoscopic ultrasound were performed, which concluded that the neoplasm was a well-differentiated neuroendocrine neoplasm. A complete resection of the main bile duct was performed with a lymphadenectomy of the hepatic pedicle with a Roux-en-Y hepaticojejunostomy, without complications. The patient had a good evolution and is disease-free.
