The patient, a man in his 70s, developed an 11.5-cm HCC (T3N0M0 Stage III) in the posterior hepatic zone after being virologically negative for hepatitis C. The tumor was near the right portal vein branch, making the liver lobe unresectable due to poor liver function. Upon tumor enlargement, he was treated with atezolizumab and bevacizumab. After 5 courses, the tumor significantly shrank, allowing for complete resection with posterior segment removal. The surgery revealed mostly necrotic tumors with no active cancer cells remaining.
