We present the case of a 72-year-old woman with pancreatic cancer and poor pain management despite opioid use. She was given splanchnic nerve neurolysis under inpatient care.

Results: Post procedure, she presented good pain management, however she developed hypoxemia, bilateral pleural and pericardial effusions. Common causes of these events were ruled out. The patient was discharged 24 hours later with good pain management.
