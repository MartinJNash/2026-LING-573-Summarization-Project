Here we present a case of a 27-year-old female, recently diagnosed with Graves' disease, who was prescribed methimazole and developed severe pruritus with cholestatic jaundice 13 days later. Concomitant causes of liver disease were ruled out. The treatment was discontinued, and a switch to corticosteroid therapy with a regimen of radioactive iodine sessions was initiated. The patient's condition showed a resolution of pruritus and jaundice, a disappearance of cytolysis with an aggravation of cholestasis followed by a gradual decrease, leading to the liver function normalization after 2 years.
