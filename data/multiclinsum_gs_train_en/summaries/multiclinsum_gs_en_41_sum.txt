4-month-old lactating infant, indigenous ethnicity, from the rural interior of Panama, 3 hours by canoe from the nearest health subcenter, with protein-caloric malnutrition, who presented with acute diarrhea and moderate-severe dehydration, receiving Enterogermina as part of the initial treatment. She was transferred to a third-level hospital, where she arrived with respiratory distress and signs of shock. The initial blood culture reported growth of methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA), the gastrointestinal panel was positive for Clostridiodes difficile, and later growth was confirmed in serial blood cultures of peripheral blood and central venous catheter, of Bacillus clausii. With a torpid evolution and resistance to multiple antibiotic regimens, she died of multisystem organ failure twelve days after admission.
