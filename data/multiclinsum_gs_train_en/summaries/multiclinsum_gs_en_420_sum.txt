A 69-year-old female patient presented to the clinic complaining of progressive vision loss. The ophthalmic evaluation was remarkable for a best corrected visual acuity of counting fingers at 5' in the right eye and 3' in the left eye. Imaging revealed deep extensive atrophy of the central macula, epithelial pigment hyperplasia, and other areas of multifocal atrophy in the right eye. Furthermore, fundus autofluorescence imaging of the macula showed central hypoautofluorescence with bilateral expansion to the periphery in both eyes. A full-field electroretinogram showed a normal rod response, with decreased cone response, bilaterally. Genetic testing was positive for a homozygous intronic mutation in the ABCA4 gene of the variant c.5714+5G>A.
