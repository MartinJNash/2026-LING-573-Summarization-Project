4-year-old patient with generalized erythematous-scaly ichthyosiform syndrome since the first days of birth. The laboratory report highlighted persistent hypertransaminemia. Among other complementary tests, the peripheral blood smear (SP) was performed, revealing the presence of multiple cytoplasmic vacuoles inside the polymorphonuclear leukocytes (PMN) and platelets. The ichthyosiform lesions together with the presence of lipid vacuoles in the SP PMN are signs compatible with Chanarin-Dorfman syndrome. The diagnosis was confirmed by genetic sequencing.
