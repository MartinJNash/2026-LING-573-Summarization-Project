A 18-year-old male smoker presented with shortness of breath and fever. He had hypoxemic respiratory failure and leukocytosis and reported recently using an e-cigarette. A computed tomography (CT) scan of the chest showed extensive bilateral ground glass opacities and areas of consolidation with air bronchograms. Antibiotics were initiated and a fibrobronchoscopy was performed that showed blood stains without endoluminal lesions. It was interpreted as EVALI and systemic corticosteroids were indicated. The patient recovered and was discharged 48 hours after the end of treatment. A follow-up CT scan showed improvement of the lesions. The diagnosis of EVALI is excluded.
