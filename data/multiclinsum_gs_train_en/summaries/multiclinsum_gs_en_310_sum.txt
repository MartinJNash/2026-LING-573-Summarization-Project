Patient concerns: In this article, we report a case of a 6-year-old girl with Berry syndrome who presented with significant ischemic and hypoxic symptoms.

Diagnoses: Based on the patient's medical history and examinations, a definitive diagnosis of Berry syndrome was established.

Interventions: She underwent a 1-stage surgical treatment and had a successful discharge. The surgical process was aimed to preserve the growth potential of the aorta and pulmonary arteries while achieving unobstructed left ventricular outflow and continuity of the aortic arch, ensuring no shunting between the main pulmonary arteries.

Outcomes: Intraoperative transesophageal echocardiography confirmed continuous flow through the aortic arch and no shunting between the MPA and the aorta.
