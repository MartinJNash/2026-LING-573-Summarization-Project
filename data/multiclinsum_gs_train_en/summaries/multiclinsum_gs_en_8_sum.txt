The case of an 80-year-old woman with a diagnosis of stage IV breast cancer undergoing treatment with fulvestrant and ribociclib, who was admitted to our center for abdominal pain and vomiting, is presented. Intestinal pneumatosis and pneumoperitoneum were diagnosed, and exploratory laparotomy was performed for suspected intestinal ischemia. There was no evidence of intestinal necrosis or perforation, so resection was not performed. She progressed satisfactorily during the hospital stay, and at the tomographic control one month after discharge, there was complete resolution of the condition.
