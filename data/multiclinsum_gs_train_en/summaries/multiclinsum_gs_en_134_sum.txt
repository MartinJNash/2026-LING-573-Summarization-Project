This report describes the treatment of a 51-year-old patient who weighed 79 kg and was admitted to hospital 36 hours after eating mushrooms, which were identified as Lepiota brunneoincarnata, a species new to Argentina. The patient presented with general malaise, with nausea and repeated vomiting accompanied by abdominal pain and diarrhea. Thanks to a rapid anamnesis and approximate and early identification of the fungus, a nasogastric tube was placed and activated charcoal was administered serially. In addition to N-acetylcysteine, phytomenadione and penicillin G EV. The patient was discharged 11 days after admission.
