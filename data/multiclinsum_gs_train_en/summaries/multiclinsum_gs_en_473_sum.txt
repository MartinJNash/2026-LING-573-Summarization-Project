A 48-year-old patient with chronic enolism who reported intranasal inhaled use of a very high dose of disulfiram without concomitant intake of alcohol developed acute encephalopathy with rapidly progressive respiratory failure. Neuroimaging revealed extensive bilateral symmetrical involvement of both pale nuclei, a characteristic finding in this intoxication. Neurological recovery was slow. Two months after intoxication, the patient had a slight residual intentional tremor and a magnetic resonance showed an evolution of the symmetrical areas of cytotoxic edema to necrosis.
