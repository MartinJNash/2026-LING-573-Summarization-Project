A 20-year-old woman with type 1 diabetes mellitus diagnosed at 8 years of age, multiple hospitalizations for diabetic ketoacidosis and recent admission for candidemia (Candida albicans) completing treatment with fluconazole for 23 days is presented. 18 days after her discharge, she consulted for dull-type flank pain and general symptoms; an abdominal tomography with contrast was performed that showed bilateral multifocal abscesses. Candida albicans was isolated in one of the samples obtained from the lesions; she received treatment with fluconazole 400 mg intravenous for 6 weeks and 2 weeks via enteral, evolving favorably with clinical and imaging improvement and continuing ambulatory clinical follow-up.
