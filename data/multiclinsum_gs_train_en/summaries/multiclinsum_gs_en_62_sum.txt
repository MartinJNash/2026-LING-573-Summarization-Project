We report the case of a 22-year-old male native of Argentina who presented with nasal respiratory insufficiency, rhinorrhea and epistaxis. Rhinoscopy showed a polypoid formation arising from the septum and occupying the entire right nasal cavity. Microscopically, the polyp was covered by respiratory and pavement epithelium and at the stromal level there was evidence of a chronic granulomatous inflammatory process with the presence of abundant cystic structures of 100 to 500 microns thick (sporangia), containing within them abundant endospores. Periodic acid-Schiff and Grocott's stains highlighted these structures. Given the clinical and epidemiological background and the morphological picture, the diagnosis of rhinosporidiosis was reached.
