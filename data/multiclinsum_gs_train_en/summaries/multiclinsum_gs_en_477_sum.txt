We report a case of a 60-year-old man who developed severe respiratory failure after collapsing in a sauna facility. Initially diagnosed with severe community-acquired pneumonia and acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS), his condition deteriorated despite standard treatment, necessitating venovenous extracorporeal membrane oxygenation (VV-ECMO). Acid-fast bacilli testing confirmed pulmonary tuberculosis (TB) on day 4, prompting the initiation of anti-TB therapy. Following clinical improvement, the patient was successfully weaned from VV-ECMO and transferred to a specialized TB hospital for ongoing care.
