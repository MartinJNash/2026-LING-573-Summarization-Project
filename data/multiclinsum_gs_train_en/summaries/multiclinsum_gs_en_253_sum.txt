A 24-year-old woman with recurrent episodes of symptomatic hypoglycemia and a first-degree family history of insulinoma presented. Initial study was compatible with endogenous hyperinsulinism secondary to two pancreatic tumors, the main one 8.5 × 4.2 cm, and PTH-dependent hypercalcemia secondary to a parathyroid adenoma. Subsequent studies with magnetic resonance imaging of the sella revealed a macroadenoma of the pituitary gland. Genetic study revealed deletion in the IAT gene, compatible with MEN1. She was managed with pancreatic and parathyroid tumor resection, with subsequent replacement management.
