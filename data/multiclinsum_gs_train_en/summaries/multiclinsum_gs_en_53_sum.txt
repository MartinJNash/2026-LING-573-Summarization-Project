We present the case of a 59-year-old man who presented with chest pain and an electrocardiogram with inferior and anterior ST segment elevation, referred to primary angioplasty and in the angiographic study, ostial compromise of coronary arteries was identified, aortic dissection was suspected, confirmed by computed tomography of the aorta, where an ascending aortic dissection with compromise of both coronary ostia was evident and was treated surgically.
