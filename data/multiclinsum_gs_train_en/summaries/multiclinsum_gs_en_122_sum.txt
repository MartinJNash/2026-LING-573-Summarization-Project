A 74-year-old woman with a history of antithyroid medication discontinuation against medical advice underwent urgent CABG. The patient exhibited extreme tachycardia postoperatively, which is highly suggestive of thyroid storm. Although a higher infection risk is an important consideration, a high-dose steroid was used to control the intractable tachycardia that did not respond to beta-blocker administration. Despite appropriate antibiotic treatment, the patient’s condition was exacerbated, and she developed multiple organ failure resulting from adult respiratory distress syndrome progression, and she died on day 8 after surgery.
