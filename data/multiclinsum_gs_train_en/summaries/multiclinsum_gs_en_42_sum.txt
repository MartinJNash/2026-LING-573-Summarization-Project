We present a case of a 25-year-old male who underwent brachial artery repair with reverse saphenous vein graft interposition after sustaining a right upper arm laceration associated with massive hemorrhage and shock due to delayed consult. He presented with profound bilateral loss of vision 12 h after surgery characterized as right homonymous hemianopsia. Computed tomography of the brain demonstrated ischemic infarcts in the occipital lobes. Close observation was instituted, and his symptom resolved spontaneously within a week.
