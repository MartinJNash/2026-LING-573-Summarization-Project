This report is about a patient who had increased function of the STAT1 factor in his first year of life. He is now 27 years old and has had a history of infection with Calmette-Guérin bacillus and Mycobacterium tuberculosis, chronic mucocutaneous candidiasis, tinea capitis, and facial and ocular rosacea. HIV infection was ruled out. Based on clinical manifestations, an innate immunity defect was suspected, specifically with increased function or activity of STAT1. The diagnosis was corroborated by sequencing of multiple genes associated with innate immunity defects. In this patient, the pathogenic variant c.961A>G (p.Arg321Gly) in the STAT1 gene was found, previously reported as a function-enhancing mutation.
