36-year-old woman with a diagnosis of NMM who, after five years of clinical stability under treatment with subcutaneous Ig at a dose of 2 g/kg/month, developed severe weakness in both hands, so it was decided to change the treatment to intravenous Ig. However, she progressed to the point where she was unable to carry out basic activities of daily living. We initiated treatment with intravenous Ig at an ultra-high dose (5 g/kg/month) with a good response, achieving functional independence in activities of daily living and returning to work. The only adverse event related to intravenous Ig at an ultra-high dose was the presence of headaches during the infusion.
