A 77-year-old man diagnosed with cutaneous sarcoidosis was admitted to our hospital resulting from heart failure with AV-block. His cardiovascular history included several previous percutaneous coronary interventions for ST-elevated MI and old MI to the left anterior descending and left circumflex arteries, respectively. Echocardiography showed hypokinesis of the posterolateral and antero-septal walls and no basal thinning of the ventricular septum. No significant coronary artery stenosis was detected during the emergency coronary angiography. CMR showed hypokinesis in antero-septal, antero-apical, and posterolateral walls with subendocardial DE corresponding with the coronary tree of previous MI. Additionally, mid-wall DE was confirmed in the basal-anterior wall with high intensity on a black-blood T2 weighted image. Subsequent FDG-PET imaging demonstrated high uptake in the basal-anterior wall. The patient was successfully diagnosed with CS using combined imaging modalities despite overlapping previous MI. The patient was treated with corticosteroids; the focal high uptake on FDG-PET was improved and had a better clinical course without further cardiac events.
